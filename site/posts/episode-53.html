Then “That’s nuts” nerdsniped me with an interview question she got from a friend. The question setup is given an n x n matrix containing the natural numbers 1 through n^2 sequentially in order, prove that if you chose 1 number from each row and column and sum them up you get some closed form formula. It was actually a pretty interesting problem but my brain was a bit overclocked at this point and I don’t think I made meaningful progress on it.

The key idea I focused on was trying to prove that no matter how you do it the result will be the same. Lets look at the example where n = 3 and we have a 3 x 3 matrix

1 2 3

4 5 6

7 8 9

Suppose we pick along the diagonals and do (1, 5, 9). Then you get the sum is 15. Now suppose I want to change things up and instead of doing 1 from the first row I do 2 instead. In this case since we can’t do 5 since its in the same column as 2 we have to pick 4 or 6. If we pick 4 then we can still do 9 as in the first case. This also sums to 15.

Actually proving that its the same requires some bashy math which my 11pm brain didn't quite have the capacity for expanding out polynomial bashes.

She did solve it though by the end which was great. Interestingly she told me the last time she got an interview question from a friend like this she wound up getting the same question in an interview later so if it happens again she will definitely be prepared.
