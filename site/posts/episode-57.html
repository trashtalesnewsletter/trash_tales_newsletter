Friday

Today I properly fixed the bug and brought my first week here to close.

After work I get dinner with “The Philosopher" and “The Fisherman” where they give me some very helpful feedback for dating (and just personal relationships in general).

One of the big overarching things they told me was that I can sometimes make a bad first impression on people for a variety of reasons. Part of it is that I take a “one size fits all” approach to social interaction and use a wide variety of jokes and bits that don’t really make sense right away. For example some of my terrorist jokes are not good to say right away (especially to a woman on a date) but even in general may unnecessarily put people on the backfoot.

Another example of this would be a time when I went crabbing with some people and they asked if I would be eating any and I made a joke along the lines of the following “Oh I can’t I’m allergic … I don’t eat anything I don’t like”. I meant the joke in a light hearted way but from what I was told their reaction was not the best. This brings up two points. The first is that a first conversation with someone (especially the first 30 minutes or so) should be mainly factual and making jokes like that can be highly disruptive to forming the first impression, and the other (which I have noticed in retrospect) is that I’m not the most aware of how others perceive me. I don’t always pick up on subtle grimaces or turning away as a response to things I may do or say. Being able to pick up on and recover from that with things like “Oh sorry did I make you uncomfortable” can be quite useful in the event I do fumble the ball.

Along those lines smiling more can be good. I don’t really have a very good “smile on command” smile (this is part of why my Hinge profile is also bad). We did a little bit of practice here as well to nail down some of the components. I should make sure that I squint my eyes, raise my eyebrows a bit, and fully use my cheeks. :D.

I also need to be mindful of my tone of voice and distance when asking questions. For example, sometimes when I’m meeting people and I ask the question “What’s something cool or interesting about you that people wouldn’t expect?” I do it in a dry tone and upclose distance that gives off the vibe that I’m sizing someone up or evaluating them rather than genuinely trying to get to know them

I can also sometimes have awkward silences when I speak due to me needing some time to think of what I’ll say next so adding in phrases like “Oh sorry I lost my train of thought one second” can be helpful.

There was also some really good and thought provoking feedback on things related to religion and materialism specifically in terms of what it is I want in a partner. I’m Muslim but I realize I don’t entirely know what I should want or expect from a partner. Someone who is already Muslim? And if so, how devout are they? For example they were telling me about some Muslim women they know who to put it bluntly are more devout then I am and for example would not be ok with me going to my weekly bar nights with the Canadians even though I don’t drink alcohol.

Would I be ok with someone who was raised Muslim but doesn’t really practice?

Would I give up the woman of my dreams just because she isn’t Muslim?

These are questions that upon thinking about it I don’t have a clear cut answer to since to be honest I don’t really have the relationship experience to say. Originally my intention was to have a long term relationship that can lead to marriage but given my inability to answer these it may be better to have some more short term relationships that can teach me more about what it is I really want and be able to answer these hard questions. Of course it's possible I go in with the intention of dating for experience and find something that works but it’s no guarantee.

Another less hard thing I need to figure out is materialistic expectations. Anyone who has been to my apartment can probably guess I’m not a very materialistic person but that’s not generally the kind of expectation I can have in a partner. As such I may need to consider things like upgrading my apartment or buying fancier things to meet somewhere in the middle. This one obviously isn’t as big as the point above but still something to think about.

I am really grateful for the feedback it has given me a lot to think about along with some tips which if applied should even boost personal relations of all kinds. And of course super grateful for friends taking the time to give me this kind of feedback!

After the feedback session I wound up going on a late night trip to El Farlito’s because a college friend of mine was in town and we met up for a while over burritos and hung out.
