Episode 24

Monday

Today is a pretty ordinary day in the bucket. I go to work, go home, and write up the newsletter that I procrastinated on last week. Pretty standard stuff.

Tuesday

Today after work I go to play spikeball over in the Pan Handle. Unfortunately I don’t get to play quite as many games as I’d like so instead I decide to practice my serves. I have this one serve I do where I hit the ball between my legs so it hits the net real low. I can do it at least 30% of the time but want to get it higher so its more useable

I have another serve I do where I hit the ball from the side and chop it towards the net rather than hitting it directly on. I tried for all of a minute to draw this but my limited stick figure skills have failed me here. Hopefully if not y’all can visualize this in your head and if not well idk man sahwee.

Wednesday

During work today I come to the realization that I”m not really using my MIXT points well enough. For those unaware Mixt is a salad chain in SF and it happens to be where I get lunch every day. Because I pay for a bunch of orders on the company credit card I’ve accumulated a whole bunch of reward points with nothing to use them on. Today I think to myself that it would be cool if I can get the custom salad I order every day as a permanent menu item (“The Iqballer”). I figure the best way to do that would be to message the CEO since Twitter taught me you can sometimes get good results by doing that. It turns out the CEO is on LinkedIn

Since she is out of my network I need LinkedIn premium to message her. Shoutout to “The Plower” and “The Plower”’s boyfriend for getting me free LinkedIn premium so I can do so. I shoot her a message and unfortuantely she hasn’t responded yet. I need to follow up with her later

Today after work is Midnight Runners. But this isn’t just any ordinary MR. THis is the one where I do the air purifier challenge. This is essentially where I do the whole 5 mile run while carrying my air purifier. Some of you may wisely be wondering why I would do something like this. Its because someone at Bay 2 Breakers jokingly suggested I do it and well you all know me at this point. When I see a bit to commit to I’m gonna do it.

To be honest this was harder than I thought it would be. I don’t think it was so much the weight as it was the clunkiness of carrying it. The weight wasn’t too bad (probably between 10-20 pounds), but it wasn’t easy to carry and I did have to shift it around a bunch to get it to keep my arms from getting too sore. Also I noticed I was definitely expending more effort. Even going at a slower pace I was much more winded cardio wise than normal. So evidently its doing something.

One other thing I didn’t expect while doing this is how many people would ask why I’m doing it.

I’m not kidding when I say that somewhere between 10-20 different people asked me why I’m carrying an air purifier. Many people thought I was doing this because I just bought it and didn’t want to leave it there, or that I lost a bet of some sort. I’m surprised this is so much of a curiosity for folks but apparently it was. Afterwards is the post MR hang which is great as always.

Thursday

Today I start the morning with a little stroll before work since the weather is absolutely amazing. I catch the tail end of the Almost Friday Run club as part of this. By tail end I specifically mean the part where people are socializing and have already finished running. I figured I’d rather enjoy the nice weather with a leisurely walk then try to run. One hack I’ve realized is that if you join them just for the socializing they don’t really care if you actually did the running or not. Its even funnier if before revealing I didn’t run I crack a little smirk and mention how I didn’t even break a sweat.

At this run club I met somebody who is actually a professional musician. I’ve never met one before so it was pretty cool hearing about her experiences. Apparently the job has you contract out to different harmonics for short stints and move around. She said she did it so then even if it fails she will have cool stories to tell and evidently she did.

From there I go to work. Some of my coworkers make me a fruit birthday cake since they know I’m on a diet

It was really nice and very much appreciated!!

After work I go to a dating event. This isn’t quite like the Shuffledating events I’ve gone to in the past. Instead this is an event run by some Stanford students doing an AI matchmaking company. The whole premise here is that before the event you have a conversation with an AI model describing your personality along with what you’re looking for in a partner and they use that to match you up with someone. It seemed intriguing so I decided “why not”. When I get there it turns out the event starts with a bunch of regular mingling with the AI match part coming later.

The mingling part is essentially a couple hundred people chatting each other up. I guess its just like a bar except you know everyone there is single. I have a bunch of conversations with people many of whom seem cool but nobody I really vibe with. I meet one person who did research on cows in undergrad trying to figure out what environmental factors lead them to being the happiest and it turns out the way you brush them had the biggest impact.I have a bunch of other miscellaneous conversations that don’t really go anywhere. One interesting quirk of an event like this with so many people is that a lot of conversations feel very ephemeral with very quick turnaround times if you’re not vibing. I guess the emphasis here is on vibing quick and early which I’m not always sure is realistic. Usually on a first date at people generally have at least an hour to get to know each other but oh well.

During one of my conversations I brought up my weekly newsletter and she said “Do your friends actually read it?”. I was a bit taken aback and said yes and she said “Oh thats really sweet of them.”. I’ll admit that definitely threw me for a loop. Its rare to meet someone so openly condescending towards a hobby of mine. Usually people would at least keep that thought to themselves and say some sort of empty phrase peppered with a hint of plausible deniabilty if they really didn’t care for it. I’m not sure whether to be impressed by the honesty or put off by it. Suffice to say that conversation didn’t really go anywhere. As I left the conversation I was more on the taken aback side and silently hoped the middle school students she taught would be extra annoying the next day.

Interestingly I meet as many current coworkers as former at this event with both of those numbers being greater than 0 which reminds me just how small a world this truly is.

After 40 minutes of this we finally get the AI matches. We’re told there are envelopes in the back and we need to go to the table thats been designated for us. I match with one “Molly M” at one “Table 53”. Thats all well and good except for the fact that I don’t see a Table 53 as the tables only go up to 32. I pace back and forth throughout the bar looking for this supposed Table 53 only to find that its as real as me not committing to the bit. Eventually the organizers send out a text blast saying they messed up and anyone with a higher table number should congregate over by the pool table.

While on my way there I trip over a metal grate and quickly grab a woman’s shoulder instinctively to balance myself. I always know how to make myself look good.

Eventually after a while I do wind up meeting this “Molly M” and we chat for a few minutes. Shes a genetic councilor specifically in the area of cancer. I didn’t know that certain kinds of cancer were hereditary so that was interesting to learn. She seemed fun but I guess she didn’t feel the same way when I got the “it was nice to meet you” just 3 minutes in. I’ve learned that “its nice to meet you” is not the same thing as “its nice to talk to you”

I decide to head out shortly after this and go to bar night with “The Lad’s”. We go to the usual spot of Fermentation Lab and just chill for a while. I talk with “The Mosher”, “The Orchestra captain”, “The juggler”, and “Thats nuts”. It was a a fun time but nothing overly memorable.

Friday

Today after work I host a board game night over at The Humza cave. I’m joined by “The organizer”, “The Doctor”, “The Mosher”, “The Boxer”, and “John Wick”. Interestingly “The Mosher” and “John Wick” have the same actual first name so I do wind up using these character names to help distinguish them.

We play a few rounds of Secret Hitler where the fascists get their butts kicked all 3 times without it even being close (I trust you all to make your own jokes about how this relates to the current political climate and insert it in here). Naturally of course I’m also the only person who was fascist all 3 times as well.

We also play Coup where I introduce a new house rule of not teaching new people how to play and having them figure it out for themselves which leads to some pretty funny results.

Saturday

Today starts off with Trash Pickup over in The Mission. The crowd is smaller than usual (likely due to Memorial day weekend) but its no less fun. While there me and “The writer” meet a guy who is *REALLY* good at impressions. He can do all sorts from “Thor”, to different nationality accents, and a lot more. It was super interesting seeing him do all these impressions.

After trash pickup its time for my party in GGP! We start off over by The Conservatory of Flowers and dance our way through GGP. We make our way from the Conservatory of Flowers roughly to the rollerskating ring and then back. Huge thanks to everyone coming to dance with me! I know many of y’all weren’t comfortable dancing but even just walking with me while I danced was still appreciated. Props to “The fisherman” and “The Doctor” for your killer moves.

One especially nice touch is that some of my friends got a bunch of toy unicorn hats from Amazon like this and everyone wore them which was very touching!

We then picknicked in GGP for a while and did presents. A bunch of y’all contributed to some pretty awesome group gifts! I’m really touched by the gifts, they’re incredibly funny!

I can’t wait to start going through the bit jar and doing some of these bits! And to wear my new shirt! :D

We picknicked in GGP for a while and enjoyed the nice day. It was really great getting to just hang out and catch up with a bunch of friends especially those who I don’t get to see too often these days.

After the picnic we went to Volcano Curry over in Richmond for dinner. Its one of my favorite restaurants here, the beef tendon curry is really good!

The night ends with karaoke over at K-Box. We have some really good singers in the group espeically “The Raja of Rhythm” and “Farz”. Its a really grand time! The one downside is that they don’t quite have every song we want. It’d be nice if these karaoke places let us connect our phones or something so we can pick out different songs. The night ends with me doing Rap God because you know, I gotta do Rap God. Unfortunately it wasn’t on the K-Box’s list of songs so we hack our away around it by connecting my phone to the speaker that I brought for dancing and use one of the mics as an amp. It ain’t much but its an honest song.

Huge thanks again to everyone who came today! Y’all are what make SF such a special place to me!!

Sunday

Today begins with Trash pickup as per usual. At the same time there is also Carnivale happening which is pretty fun to see! “Olives” is Jamaica so we wanted to find any Jamaican representation but unfortunately we couldn’t see any. Interestingly Jamaican Carnivale is actually bigger than South American Carnivale but thats not well advertised here. Incidentally at the trash pickup I wind up meeting someone who looks just like the person from Thursday who commented on the Newsletter. She has the exact same as well but when I ask if she’d seen me somewhere before or if she’s a middle school teacher she says no to both of those.

After all the festivities I head over to a birthday party for one of my old coworkers. I catch up with a few of my old coworkers there and what I hear doesn’t exactly make me regret my decision to leave. Interestingly this old coworkers is about to leave herself to become a full time LinkedIn influencer. I’ve never heard of anyone trying to be an influencer over there and while I have a general disdain for LinkedIN as a platform in general I wish her luck and I respect her conviction to go full time on something like this. For whatever reason at this party in particular I get bored of introducing myself to a whole bunch of people and decide to leave a bit early which is pretty unusual for me.

From there I go to dinner with my family. We do a joint birthday dinner as both me and my younger sister had birthdays recently. For a while I didn’t really tell my parents about me doing trash pickup because in the past they expressed their disapproval of it but I figured I may as well tell them. This was inspired by what happened back in episode 16 when I realized that in order to feel closer to my family I should tell them more about the kinds of things that I do.

My Mom wasn’t the happiest at first but it wasn’t as bad as I thought it would be. My family all found it funny when I told them my “Thank the courts” joke and the fact that I was able to get us 30% off on a pretty big meal at Anatolian table didn’t hurt either. From there I head home. Unfortunately I have a bad headache at this point so I’m not able to finish up the newsletter quite on time
