Monday

Today was a pretty chill day. I enjoyed the nice weather and walked around. I didn’t go anywhere or do anything of particular note. Perhaps the best part was when I went to Tank Hill early in the morning and enjoyed one of my favorite views overlooking the city.

Tuesday

Today after meandering for a bit in the morning I thought it would be fun to spontaneously do The Double Cross trail. I start off around Pier 23 and make my way towards Lake Merced.

I make it a goal to go as aggressively as I can without running so I wind up going at about a 16 min/mile or 3.8 mph pace

On the way to Coit Tower

It was a fun hike but overall nothing particularly notable. Between this and the regular Crosstown trail I think I like the regular one more but it's pretty close. At this point I had done hikes of this distance enough that I didn’t feel like I was breaking new frontiers here.

Perhaps the most interesting part was towards the end when I was at Stern Grove and a bunch of dogs came up and barked at me. One of the dog owners informed me I was at a dog park and I just sighed and moved on. I think at that point 5 or 6 different dogs were barking at me. Maybe it was because I was walking fast, maybe they didn’t like the cut of my jib, maybe they sensed some demon aura inside me. Who knows. It certainly did dampen the walk for all of 3 minutes.

From then on I got a bit spooked whenever I saw a dog on the walk. The same thing happened when I finished the walk and was headed back. At that point only one dog was barking at me but it was doing so quite aggressively and blocked my main way down since I couldn’t overtake the dog and its owner. I entered a Mexican standoff as I stared down the mutt and shot it my fiercest look of annoyance. After a minute I backed down and waited for the scamp to walk on by which it eventually did as I wished for the dog equivalent of stepping in gum to happen to it.

On my way home I took the bus; going through the south west part of SF I shared the bus with a bunch of teenyboppers who talked about classes and clubs they were doing and it took me back to my time in high school. It was kinda amusing reminiscing on my time taking classes and doing clubs like that. I think in my time living here I never had a bus with so many kids on it. Sometimes you have a few but never a whole bus full of them.

Afterwards I went to a Krav Maga class which was fun. This one taught a different style from the one I’m used to so the class was mainly reviewing fundamentals but with slight twists. It was a good time.

Wednesday

Today was another chill day. I walked around and laid down in Duboce Park for a while. Then I went to Midnight Runners with “The Quant”.

Thursday

I started off by doing Almost Friday Run Club. I tried to make it a bit harder by carrying my air purifier with me. Back in episode 24 I did this at Midnight Runners and actually succeeded. Unfortunately this club is smaller and tends to run at a more homogenous (and faster) pace which I couldn’t keep up with so I just ran by myself for a while while carrying the air purifier and caught up with everyone afterwards.

Sadly after the run club I received some horrible news that one of my friends “The Climber” had passed away. When I first read the message I was shocked. It seemed so surreal. He was pretty much my age. How the hell does something like this happen? To be perfectly honest I wasn’t going to write about this given the subject matter but for reasons I’ll get into later today I ultimately figured this was the right thing to do.

I felt a sense of despair that hung over me for quite a while. Throughout the day I imagined him next to me as I shuffled along. Conversations we had would flood back to me. I had to resist the urge to cry in a Whole Foods as I reminisced. I didn’t really leave my place much after that.

Later in the day I got myself up and met with “The Hombre” for gelato in Mission Dolores. He would be moving away to the UK for a job and I wanted to make sure I saw him before he left. Best of luck man, I know you’ll kill it in your new role!

From there I went to a service for “The Climber” at his old apartment. As part of the service people brought things for an apartment shrine that was being set up. I wasn’t sure what I should bring for the shrine. Flowers seemed like a standard choice but when I got the message that he passed I was told “he really loved your newsletter and sense of humor” so I knew there had to be something more personal I could leave. I thought about it for a while and what I ultimately landed on was leaving some of my six of hearts playing cards along with some flowers. For those unaware I have a “magic trick” I like to do in which I ask someone to pick a card and I try to retrieve it after shuffling the deck. The joke of this trick is they are all 6 of hearts and thus it is very easy to pick. Its the easiest way to have a tangible form of a joke and I never got to do the trick with him unfortunately so I wanted to leave a little bit of it behind because this is one of my personal favorite jokes that I do, while the cards themselves aren’t special in general they are special to me and I want to give a little something to him, and finally to wish him six-cess in the after life. I am Muslim and believe in an afterlife afterall (I prayed for him before this as well but that's separate).

The service itself starts off understandably pretty somber as we all gather around and share some of our favorite stories about “The Climber”. Throughout the event it's heartwarming to get reminders of what I already knew: How kind he was, how funny he was, how down he was to do anything, how he was really good with kids. I met his mom, sister, and brother at this event. To be honest I can’t imagine what they are going through with all this.

As the event goes on the mood becomes a bit lighter as the ice breaks and we share more stories about him and even start laughing a bit. One of his apartment mates makes a toast and reminds us that he wouldn’t want us to feel sad and we toast to him with a bit of a smile.

Throughout all this I’m reminded of a quote from one of my favorite mangas called One Piece

When does a man die? When he is hit by a bullet? No! When he suffers a disease? No! When he ate a soup made out of a poisonous mushroom? No! When his heart stops? No! A man dies when he is forgotten!

I won’t forget you “The Climber’.

As part of the event we all went around and shared some of our favorite stories with him. Unfortunately most of y’all reading this newsletter never got to know him, or what an amazing person he was so I want to share some stories here. Some of these may be in previous newsletters but I want to write them all out here anyway.

Climber Story 1

One time I did Midnight Runners with “The Climber,” “Sergeant Sassy,” “La Taqueria Royalty,” and one of our other friends and we went to a restaurant afterwards for dinner. While there we were talking about hobbies and I mention that I’ve done improv in the past and “The Climber” and I do some impromptu improv for a while centering around a white sauce condiment we see on the table and a fictitious duck that we co parent after our divorce. A lot of times people will be interested when I say I do improv but very rarely will people be so down to do it with me spontaneously. Afterwards we got into a discussion regarding titty milk and answering the age old question of whether you would drink the titty milk of someone rich with really good genes. To be perfectly honest even amongst all my friends there are very few who I think I can truly go full unhinged humor on. He was one of them. I could take the conversation in any unhinged place and he could match me titty milk for tatty milk.

Climber Story 2

One time after Midnight Runners me, him, and another friend were sitting around a table and discussing our biggest dating failures. At one point I bring up the dating posters I made

He found this so amusing that he asked everyone around what they thought about it which led to a whole bunch of interesting conversations. He had this childlike enthusiasm, energy, and excitement that felt downright infectious. I couldn’t help but laugh as he eagerly showed everyone this poster.

I mentioned this earlier but originally I wasn’t going to write about this. In part because I was worried it was wrong to do so, and in part because I wasn’t sure I wanted to write about this myself. Ultimately what convinced me to do so was as the event was ending I talked with "Sergeant Sassy” and she asked me if I was going to write about it. Originally I responded “I don’t know” because I wasn’t quite sure at the time. She said I should as he would have wanted it. I thought back to how excited he was to first read one of these newsletters and realized I should do so for him. As I said I believe in an afterlife so if somehow you’re able to read this up there I hope you like this “The Climber”. I really do. You truly were an amazing friend, there were so many more adventures I wanted to have with you. You had authentic, radiant energy. You had one of the best smiles and laughs. Rest in Peace.

When the event ended I kinda wanted to just go home and chill. There was a bar night planned but at first I wasn’t feeling in the right head space to attend. Ultimately I decided to go because I remembered that he was the kinda guy who would always be down for something and always bring positive energy and the vibes. If I truly want to remember him, if I truly want to keep him with me, that's what I need to do now. Of course I’m still quite sad but I need to be there for the people I have in my life right now and so I decide to go.

We go to a bar we don’t usually go to over near Union Square. It's not as homey as The Fermentation Lab that we usually go to but it's a nice change of pace. As usual I don’t drink but I did get a raspberry from the collection of raspberries that they top some of their drinks with. The owner said he wanted to charge me but one of the other bartenders slipped it my way. Apparently they throw out most of their raspberries at the end of the night so really I’m doing them a favor.

Before we conclude this section I want to say that given the recency of what happened I still think about “The Climber” a lot this week but I won’t be writing about it any more. Both because there isn’t really anything I could say that I haven’t already, and because in the spirit of what I can best do I want to focus on what I’m doing for everyone I have in my life right now.

And finally I want to thank all of you who are reading this right now. I’m grateful to have so many amazing friends in my life. Another thing I was reminded of throughout the day was this xkcd comic about saying final words. Of course, there was no way I could have known that the last time I saw him would be the last but it did come to mind.

Friday

The day begins with a walk followed by another Krav Maga class. In this class I learn a lot about how to escape from a chokehold if someone wants to do a head choke so I’m that much more ready for the streets of SF. Or sex. Either one really.

Afterwards I do a pilates class which I thought would be pretty easy but winds up kicking my tooshie. The whole class I have to run back and forth on this stupid machine doing plancks, squats with a leg extended, and various other exercises which suck. The class is also incredibly fast paced. Its “do this random exercise”, “ do that random exercise”. Does the instructor ever explain what they are? No. Also I have to take these red and yellow pins and alternatingly place them in the machine for various reasons I don’t quite understand.

My glutes are still feeling it a bit as I’m writing this. I can’t say I really loved it but I guess I’m glad I did it. Perhaps I’ll do it again at some point.

Afterwards I met up with “The Quant” and “Mirandom thoughts” and we walked around and got dessert at a cafe over in Inner Richmond called Sweet Mango Dessert cafe. According to “The Quant” apparently the place ripped off of some place in Hong Kong down to having the same menu items. I had the nothing which is as delicious as it always is and I left a yelp review here

Saturday

The day started off with trash pickup which was a good time.

From there I go to a bbq hosted by “Granola Girlie” which is a great time. I tried a grilled carrot for the first time which was better than I thought it would be. Not quite as good as grilled beef but still this could do in a pinch! Her house has a narrow spiral staircase which reminds me of going up some old towers in Europe. Not the better parts of going up the old towers in Europe but part of that experience nevertheless.

Afterwards one of my other trash friends has a 30th birthday in Dolo that I go to which is good fun.

Finally I get dinner with a friend over by El Farlito and we go on a walk from Mission down to Oceanview over in Southwest SF. While in Mission we pass by the low rider festival thats happening

As we walk through Oceanview I see some discounted Painted Ladies

Are these the painted ladies we have at home?

Sunday

Another trash pickup another fictitious dollar. After trash pickup I get brunch over at Cracked and Battered with “The Taco,” “The Plower,” “The Sparkle,” “The Quant,” “Mirandom thoughts,” “The Mushroom hunter,” “The pickleballer,” and a couple other folks. I get my usual which is as scrumptious as one can hope for. Review here. I’m wondering if now is finally the time I try to apply for Yelp elite. Can I finally crack through the gates of Valhalla?

From there we go back to the Humza cave and play some board games. We play a game called “Trial by Trolley” from the people who made Cyanide and Happiness. This game centers around two teams creating absurd trolley problem scenarios and a judge picking which group of people to run over. You can put people that you don’t want the trolley to run over (a group of kids, someone who made the cure for cancer), and people the trolley should run over (someone who spoils movies and tv shows, corrupt pharma executives). Generally people try to judge based on what would save lives but when its my turn to judge I give everyone a little surprise by judging based on what would kill the most and I decide to save the group of people that has an assassin on it as a form of future investment.

Then we play Secret Hitler in which I manage to get trounced in every game. For whatever reason everyone thinks I’m always suspicious no matter what I do. Completely baseless naturally but what can you do? Afterwards I write up the newsletter.
