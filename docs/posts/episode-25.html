Shoutouts

I’m starting a new section called Shout outs. The inspiration for this is someone once likened this newsletter to a social network where only I post. Given that I thought it would be great to shout out people who have done cool / interesting stuff this week so I can brag about how awesome y’all are to my other friends.

Starting off we have “The Raja of Rhythm” who is doing a class this weekend to get his Motorcycle license. I think thats pretty sick and deserves a shoutout.

Monday

For Memorial Day I did the “Breakers to Bay” walk. This is essentially doing the reverse of “Bay to Breakers” where I walked from Lands End to the Ferry Building. I was joined on this endeavor by “The Quant” and “The Raja of Rhythm”. It was a beautiful day out and really nice to go for a long walk like this. Afterwards a long walk like that we go to Kogi Gogi to get some good meat in and as always it meats if not exceeds expectations.

As we were waiting outside before we got our table we struck up conversation with someone painting a building nearby. He told us about how he came to the U.S from Mexico when he was 19 and has been painting buildings for over 15 years. I don’t talk to a lot of people like this generally so it was quite unique. He’s planning to go back to Mexico soon and do the same thing over there where the money he has saved up goes a lot further.

Tuesday

Today during work I make a spontaneous decision to throw away my unicorn hat, also affectionately dubbed “The Chastity Hat”.

I like the hat and think its fun but if I want to reach my goal of being more fashionable sacrifices do need to be made and this is one of them. Goodbye chastity hat, you were great fun and I’m wishing you all the best on your new travels in trashland but its time for improvement. Onwards and upwards!

After work I keep my momentum from above and go shopping.I start off with socks as I figure that would be low hanging fruit. I actually quite enjoy getting fun socks and it requires less effort than the other parts of the wardrobe. I go to “Sockshop Haight Street” and pickup quite the haul

Afterwards I go to Buffalo Exchange to try to pick up jackets only to be met with abject failure. Here my lack of fashion sense means that I need to recruit help from one of the group chats with “The Doctor”, “The Plower”, “The Quant”, and “The Taco” on hand to provide advice on the items I pick. And it turns out everything I pick sucked. Heres a selection in case y’all want to roast my terrible ability to pick clothes as well. Fair warning since this involves many pictures of me I recommend having some ibuprofen on hand if you don’t have any before looking at the next section. You’ve been warned.

The feedback I got on every outfit basically boiled down to “No”. It was incredibly exhausting trying on outfit after outfit, sending it to the groupchat, and being met with a no. I generally pride myself on having high energy levels but 20 minutes of this broke me. I’ve walked 45 miles over 19 continuous hours, ran 5 miles with an air purifier , and wore down Garden Creamery until they brought back the Mujicha ice cream flavor. At this point I thought I could face any challenge that came my way with a smile on my face. I had no idea how wrong I was. I felt crushed mentally and spiritually. I had flashbacks to when I was a kid going to the outlet malls with my family taking solace in the PSP that I brought to keep myself entertained while everyone else went to slay the shopping beast. But now I have to try to slay the beast myself and I failed. It was clear to me that the next time I do this I can’t go alone and need reinforcements.

I somberly walk out of the thrift store and try my best to recoup my morale by playing some spikeball over in PanHandle. It helps but I was definitely off my game today due to the damage I incurred.

Wednesday

Today after work I go to Midnight Runners. I must have rolled a d20 before coming to run club today because 3 rare Pokemon came to run club being “The Dentist”, “La Taqueria Royalty”, and rarest of all “The Enabler”. I wonder if this is what it feels like to get that super rare Charizard Pokemon card. Of course “The Quant” is there as reliably as usual :D.

Without an air purifier to carry today the run was pretty straightforward. Afterwards we go to Tony’s Pizza which is great. In case anyone is interested I wrote a full yelp review of it here as part of my quest to become Yelp Elite.

Thursday

Today at work I learn that one of my coworkers is going on a yacht party that her and her boyfriend were invited to and the boyfriend unfortunately couldn’t make it. I think it would be hilarious if I pretend to be her boyfriend and go but sadly she shoots me down. I’m not even really that interested in a yacht party, I just think the idea of pretending to be someone else so I can crash a yacht party would be fun. The main reason I bring this up is I told her that in response I’d mention it in the newsletter and I am nothing if not a man of my word.

Instead of that I meet up with “The Raja of Rhythm” and one of his friends over at Fermentation Lab and have a chill night. While we’re talking she mentions wanting to get a tattoo of a big cat on her back. I was hoping it would be a Cougar so I could make a Cougar joke but it winds up being a Jaguar.

Friday

Today after work I do some emergency shopping. I’m trying to convince someone that I have a twin brother by the name of Imran so I quickly cobble together a Goodwill outfit thats different from what I would wear. Thankfully since I’m not worried about this looking good I can just by stuff quickly and circumvent facing the shopping beast. This is the outfit I wind up with

I give the outfit a test drive as I go to poker. “The filmmaker” is hosting it and I’m joined by “John Wick”, “The Raja of Rhythm”, and many others. Originally I walk into this game thinking that I’ll be charitably donating my $5 buy in from the less fortunate to the more fortunate. I had absolutely zero expectations that I’d win.

Surprisingly lady luck actually smiled on me more than I thought. My golden rule for playing poker was to not focus on winning or losing but instead doing whatever I found interesting. For example I would not look at my cards and instead opt to go based on vibes. I reasoned that winning or losing a few bucks pales in comparison to the fun of just assuming I had really good cards sometimes and play accordingly. It was pretty funny whether I won or lost this way and sometimes I won big with multiple straights, and 3s of a kind that I only knew about after I had to reveal.

In the end I wound up winning $16 which means I’ve now officially entered the 1%. So you know, bow down peons. The nice thing was that everyone left with chips in the end. Some left with chips on their pockets while others left with chips on their shoulders but hey, a chip is a chip. “Enabler”, if you’re reading this I know you play poker sometimes so lets play together. Also “The Philosopher”, next time I come to poker night I’ll actually play.

Saturday

The day begins with Trash Pickup. I mentioned before I’m trying to convince “The Ravenkeeper” I have a twin and this is where I put it to the test. I go to trash pickup not as Humza but as “Imran” my twin brother. Humza was supposed to come but he “was out drinking last night and wound up sleeping in til 11:30”. I’m pretty sure she doesn’t believe me one bit throughout this but as someone who commits to the bit I can’t just back down. I keep the charade up for about 2 hours. It gets tricky as she asks me more and more questions like “Whats something about Humza he would never tell us”. I have to create a whole fictitious backstory while keeping in mind everything I had already made up in the past. Essentially it was the longest and trickiest improv I had ever done. Supposedly Imran works at Google and she asked for a referral. I came up with a Google Voice number just in case she ever asked for a phone number but she wanted to tap phones instead. Truly sneaky indeed. Due to various circumstances the backstory wound up involving me pushing “Imran” down some stairs when we were 7 giving him a scar that I still make fun of to this day. He also lives in the Marina, hates trash, and loves playing soccer. He wasn’t even going to come to trash pickup except for the fact that he wanted to prove his existence and that his friends cancelled on him for the usual post hangover Brunch.

Eventually we part ways after Imran promising to refer her to Google ..,. Somehow. I learn from “Granola Girlie” that she reads my newsletter with breakfast so if anyone ever asks about I can now say that the newsletter is a key part of a balanced breakfast. From there I go to Chinatown. As part of my birthday present I got a jar of bits to try and achieve and one of them is the following

For the item to be mysterious I set the rule that I should have no idea what it is because the label is written only in Chinese. I walk through multiple shops and eventually I find this

It looks about as good as any other mystery item I could find so I decide to buy some and eat it.

I buy roughly a quarter of a pound of the stuff only to find that it tastes incredibly mulchy and I give up after a couple of bites. My mouth feels dirty after eating this and I wouldn’t recommend it to almost anyone (except one person who we will talk about soon).

After throwing the rest of “mystery item” away I learn from a Chinese friend I ran into that this is apparently called Polygonatum and its used to make soups. Apparently its also toxic to consume in large quantities. Guess its a good thing I didn’t eat the whole quarter of a pound. I’m sure the slight queasiness I felt in my stomach immediately after eating it was probably unrelated.

I go to Dolores Park to try another bit that I got

I went around shouting “Life advice … 25 cents” and “A quarter now is worth a quarter life crisis”. As expected nobody took me up on it. To be fair I didn’t adequately prepare this bit. I just did it because I happened to be nearby. In the future when I make a more solid attempt of it I’m gonna get a table and make a sign advertising my life advice services. Maybe somebody will come by, probably nobody will but at least then I can say I truly tried.

Afterwards I meet up with “The Doctor” and we play Gomoku in Dolores for a while. The man is truly a god of Gomoku. For those unaware Gomoku is a game where you try to place 5 tiles of the same color in a row on a Go board before your opponent does. I play multiple rounds and lose against him every time. I do improve as the games progress though. Our last game is a six minute long battle of pure intensity. Its pitch silent as we’re both concentrating on our moves. My brows inch deeper and deeper down with each passing move as I try my best to block his relentless onslaught. I try to give myself mental strength by mentally replaying intense fights I’ve seen in Shounen anime as the protagonist boldly tries to beat the overpowered Antagonist. This was it. This was gonna be my moment of glory. Sadly towards the end my greed gets the best of me and I think I have an opportunity to go for 5 in a row only to play right into his hands. I immediately crash onto my back and sigh in defeat.

From there we meet up with “The Plower” and play Dune Imperium. After a few months of not playing its refreshing to play again. I was worried I would still be burnt out after all the online Dune we played while “The Doctor” was travelling but as the game goes on the old joy I used to feel slowly comes back. I’m still not as into the game as I used to be but it nevertheless feels a bit like the good ol days of Dune.

Sunday

The day begins with trash pickup. There are surprisingly few people here this time. I wind up pairing up with someone I don’t know called “The Shadow Brigade”. She tells me shes in the Army reserves doing signal processing transmitting signals and intercepting enemy signals which is super cool. I almost decide to give her the nickname “Mixed Signals” but realize thats easy to misinterpret so “The Shadow Brigade” it is. Rarely do I make friends with / vibe with someone so quickly, it reminds me of when I met “The Enabler”, and “The Dentist. Also, its a good thing that shes in the Army as well because I need all that strength for the upcoming fight ahead.

After trash pickup we along with “The Plower”, “The Doctor”, and “The Quant” go to Stonestown to face the shopping beast once more. I learned from Tuesday that I can’t face it alone so I bring a whole squad to take down the goliath. My objective here is to get as many things I can that look good and that will help me face an upcoming trial that will be discussed next week. I hope y’all still have the ibuprofen handy as there will be a lot more pictures of SF’s ugliest shopper inbound.

We first start with Zara where I have surprising success buying a jacket and shirt that the group likes

I leave Zaras feeling a bit tired but still energized and ready for the next fight. Little do I know how bad this would be.

We then go to the main boss Uniqlo. To say Uniqlo was overwhelming was an understatement. It really forced me to use every bit of willpower I had left.

Everyone in the group presented me a bunch of different options to try on. Each option was both a blessing and a curse as I had to go through the ordeal of changing, sending a picture, and asking for feedback. It was like Tuesday but much worse. Of course that all assumes I can even get to the changing room in the first place. I first have to deal with a 10 minute line which already provides significant strain. Whoever made Uniqlo so popular that I have to wait in line for 10 FLIPPIN MINUTES TO CHANGE deserves a whole bunch of Polygonatum down the throat. Eventually I make it to the changing room and try on a whole bunch of things.

After all these outfit changes I feel like a broken husk of a human being. Barely being able to think about more than the next step I take. Thankfully we find some stuff thats good. The pants from the third photo and the jacket from the 5th photo wind up being winners. I get one more jacket like that but in black. Below are some outfits I made with what I bought

Theres one last stop before calling it done and thats shoes. Thankfully “The quant” really takes over here and just gives me shoes to try on and we see what we all like while I’m disassociating mentally. Eventually we decide on these

And the outfit is complete.

Mission accomplished! Thanks to everyone involved who helped me take down the goliath. Especially “The Shadow Brigade” who I only just met that day but managed to rope into such a perilous undertaking. I really hope I don’t have to face this gauntlet of horrors again for a while. AFterwards we all head to “The Doctor”’s place to play some board games. And then I go home and write up the newsletter.
