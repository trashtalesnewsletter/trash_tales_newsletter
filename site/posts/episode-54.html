Thursday

Originally I wanted to go to Almost Friday Run Club but I came down with a bad case of not waking up in time having woken up at 7:14. I really hope they come out with a vaccine for that someday but the day is not today. I went to the post run hang in Flywheel which was still something though.

I made my way through more of ground school today and later I went to meetup with “The Bartender” at Last Rites. For those unaware its a charming Tiki Bar over in The Castro with airplane seats being used for the bar seats.

We hung out for a while and he told me some interesting tidbits on Brazilian history. One thing I didn’t know was that in the 19th century a lot of Syrian and other Middle Eastern immigrants came to Brazil followed by a second wave during the collapse of the Ottoman empire. Apparently Brazilians liked them because they looked “white passing” (note these were his words not mine). As a result there is actually a reasonably large Muslim population in Brazil which I didn’t know until now. He also told me growing up that he played with both Muslim and Jewish kids playing soccer in a 3 school soccer league featuring the Muslim school, the Jewish school, and his (the Catholic school).

Also interestingly Brazilians think every Muslim person there is Lebanese by default which is interesting since Lebanon has quite a sizeable non Muslim population (looking at you “The Juggler”).

He also told me more about the difference between Knitting, Crocheting, and Quilting. Basically knitting is chaining fabrics linearly by knots. Crocheting is more non linear with the chaining and quilting is more about adhering things together via a loom. They are kinda like different building blocks for building similar things. I also learned that he knits and even made the sweater that he was wearing that day. I think knitting could be an interesting hobby as something to do while on the bus or just going around and I need something to keep my hands occupied. They said idle hands are the devils workshop after all and maybe my hands can be more of the angels playground instead.

Later we went to the usual Thursday night bar and met with “That’s nuts” and some of the other usual Canadians.

By the time I got back to SF proper I learned there was a huge blackout that affected a lot of the city. My neighborhood was among those affected meaning my apartment and everything around it had no power. A bunch of grocery stores were closed, all the lights were off and everything was dark. Thankfully I wasn’t super impacted as I could just hole up and hotspot and more or less chill just like normal except in the dark. I am grateful the outage only lasted a few hours for me considering other neighborhoods like The Richmond fared much worse.

Sunday

Today we had the smallest Sunday trash pickup in history with only 22 people showing up. The holidays really did a number here but the people I did meet there were great!

I met a high school German exchange student who is wrapping up next week. She had come for about 5 months from a small town of 20K people about 3 hours away from Berlin. She really liked it here which I’m glad for! Apparently the part that surprised her the most was how friendly the relationship can be between students and teachers. At her exchange school (Mission High school) the precalc teacher is super friendly and best friends with a bunch of her students. On the other hand she described German teachers as having a much more professional relationship with their students which definitely sounds less fun by comparison.

I also met a couple people who work at an electric plane company where they are designing an electric airplane and trying to get FAA approval to sell it. Who knows, maybe once I get my pilots license I could try flying it. They currently have test pilots but they are all people with 20+ years of experience who have flown F16s.

Afterwards I had a group call with “The Professor,” “The Taco,” “The Game Master,” “The Sparkle,” and “The K-pop dancer” to catch up. “The Professor” has been thriving in Vienna which we love to see!

I got home and kinda just chilled. Later I had a call with my Mom who told me I should do more father son things with my dad. I suspect that it's because growing up there was never really a hobby we both did that we could keep doing together but no better time than the present to start changing that.

I thought for a bit about what kind of things make sense. He’s not as active as I am and can’t really run, somehow I don't think he would be that into trash pickup, and then it hit me! KBBQ!

I called and suggested we go to Kogi Gogi and walk around in GGP and he was quite excited! This could become a good father son ritual for us! On a more serious note, I have heard that people regret not spending time with their parents and such when they are around my age and regardless of past circumstances I’m the one who can choose to change things now.
