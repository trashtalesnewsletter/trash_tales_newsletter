Monday

Today starts off pretty chill. I write up last weeks newsletter in GGP, do some personal work and then walk around for a while. As I’m walking I come to the realization that I don’t really have a lot of interesting indoor hobbies. I mean sure if I’m relaxing I may watch TV, youtube or whatever but nothing really fulfilling or newsletter worthy. Previously this was never really an issue for me as when I was working after work I’d either have my other hobbies like improv or running and on the nights I didn’t I’d usually be tired enough that I’d just want to chill.

Now with this time the question becomes, what should I do? Ideally I want something that isn’t just consumption. I guess writing the newsletter itself could count but what else could I add? Maybe I could take some online classes? Maybe I could sew or crochet? The possibilities are endless in theory but in practice there isn’t anything that really sticks out to me.

After I think about this while walking I go to a new run club in the Mission appropriately called Mission Run Club. I heard about this from “The Raja of Rhythm” who mentioned that he started going on Mondays. I figured I’d check it out and honestly it was quite good!

The run itself was alright. There was a reasonable enough spread of paces that I could have taken it chill if I wanted to. Unfortunately it turns out one of “The Raja of Rhythm”’s friends is really good at running and I made it a goal to keep up with him for the whole run which meant I actually had to try. Don’t get me wrong I could do it just fine and I wound up beating him but it did mean I actually had to put in effort.

After the run we all went back to a bar and hung out. I learned that this is where the Mission Run Club really shines. The post run vibes were some of the best I had seen in a while. It's much smaller than Midnight Runners which makes it much easier to talk to people. For people who do trash pickup imagine the difference between Sisters and Mannys. I met some pretty cool people there and it cemented Mission Run Club as my second favorite run club in the city. Here’s the current rankings

Tuesday

Today I flew a plane for the first time! I got inspired because “The Juggler” is learning how to fly a plane and I thought it would be a cool thing to try. There's a flight school over in San Carlos where you can learn to do this so I went and checked it out.

When I got there I met with my flight instructor who got me situated in the plane and explained some of the different controls and panels.

So many things. Such fancy.

One thing I took for granted was how much pre flight work there was before the flight itself. Not just the taxi and take off but continually radioing into the control tower to make sure you are cleared for flight, checking the fuel, and a bunch of other preflight checks I have a hard time remembering.

Once all that was done and we were in the air that was when I got to take over. Let me tell you flying a plane was SUPER SICK! I remember as a kid I used to play a bunch of videogames and I’d always love the ones where I got to fly in all directions and this is basically a real life version of that! I got to go up, down, and even turn! Sadly I couldn’t quite do a barrel roll but maybe someday.

Thankfully I picked a sunny day to go out and do this so the views were great!

Once the flight time was over the flight instructor took over and we landed back at the airport. An interesting detail I didn’t expect was that the controls for steering an airplane on the ground are different from in the air. In the air you use the thing I’m holding in the picture above to steer the plane. Kinda like a steering wheel for a car. On the ground you use pedals to move the plane left or right. After landing I took the plane back to its original position and found myself confused when I wanted to default to using the handle but needed to pedal, a confusion the instructor said is pretty common.

Honestly this flight does make me want to sign up for more lessons and try flying again. Gosh darn was this fun!

Upon coming back to SF I originally went to check out Tuesday Spikeball over in the Panhandle only to find nobody there. On a whim I decided to fill the spikeball shaped void in my heart with another run club in the form of Fat Boys run club. Naturally anyone who ever met me wouldn’t question my qualifications for this particular run club.

This run club meets in the area between Panhandle and GGP and runs along GGP for a while and then the perimeter of the Panhandle. I’d rank this run club solidly below Mission Run Club based on the vibes. There was a bit of socialization in the beginning but didn’t quite seem as fun. The run itself was alright I suppose. As the run was finishing up in the Panhandle I saw the spikeballers playing in the distance and decided to ditch the rest of the run and join them. I only had a couple blocks left anyway. Saturday

The day begins with trash pickup. It was raining so we didn’t get a huge attendance but it was still pretty sizable. “The Shadow Brigade” was in town this weekend so she stopped by as a bit of a guest appearance to the pickup.

After the pickup the two of us went to Daly City to go ride horses. I had never done it before but she suggested it and I thought it could be fun for both me and you my dear reader.

It turns out it was quite fun. Riding a horse (at least a well trained one), is a bit like playing a video game where you move a character using some reins. The video game is a bit laggy though so you may need to pull the reins multiple times or occasionally course correct. Oh and sometimes the character randomly wants to drink water by the beach so you need to pull it back.

In all seriousness though it was pretty interesting seeing what horses react to. One of the horses got spooked by a paper bag and needed to be taken back to the stable. Going downhill was a bit scary at first but once I got used to it it was alright. I just need to remember to lean back on the downhill and lean forward on the uphill.

Sunday

Today I spent the whole day doing The Roundabout trail (a trail meant to be the perimeter of SF) with “The Shadow Brigade”.

About a year ago I walked roughly the perimeter of SF with “The Quant” and “The Philosopher”. “The Shadow Brigade” mentioned wanting to do a long walk of some sort and I suggested the one above which clocks in at about 38 miles. To be honest I didn’t think she’d actually say yes but she did and I thought why not right. It turns out when I did the perimeter a year ago we cut a lot of corners on the south east part of the city so I figured now would be a good chance to go and fix that.

In honor of the walk I wore my lambrofeeti socks that I got from “The Quant”

Ferry Building -> Candlestick park

We start at 9AM at the Ferry Building. As with many of these long walks part 1 is always the time when we are the most optimistic and upbeat. We think we can conquer anything and are ready for everything. In my opinion the first part has the most sketchy areas considering we have to go through Bayview, Hunters point, and Candlestick park so I decided to get that out of the way during daylight hours. This part is alright. Nothing too crazy really. The sketchy parts are predictably sketchy but we remain in good spirits.

Along the way we stop at a slide and slide down.

Candlestick park -> Los Trinos

Now that we’ve gone as far south east as we possibly can we head westward. We go through Visitacion Valley, Crocker Amazon, Excelsior, and get close to Daly CIty. Energy wise we’re both fine here but as we pass through the “purgatory of houses” as Shadow put it it does get a bit boring. This isn’t helped by the fact that it starts raining here. It's raining, it's cloudy, and my spirits are starting to go here. We are no longer in the optimistic phase 1, now we are solidly in phase 2 where we start to realize just what we signed ourselves up for. Every long walk this always happens to me. Eventually I get hungry and so we stop and get lunch at an El Salvadorean place.

While we’re getting lunch we take a phone break and “The Shadow Brigade” said “Are you reading manga you FUCKING WEEB?!”. At the moment I was not but I read some afterwards since she got the idea in me.

To be honest between the cold and the rain and the boredom I was tempted to give up.

Los Trinos -> Ocean Beach

Thankfully after lunch the rain subsided and my spirits came back. At this point we were joined by “The Quant” who tried his best to sneak on us but I thwarted such chicanery.

We were still in house purgatory around here which subsided right around the time we got to Ocean Beach.

Ocean Beach -> Geary St

Sadly “The Quant” left us to go back home. This sucked especially for “The Shadow Brigade” since it meant she was stuck with me again for a while. We walked through Ocean Beach and got to see some of the sunset. There were hammocks along the way which looked kinda nice. This was pretty uneventful for the most part until we got to Lands End.

Unfortunately the sun had set at this point so we were doing a straight night time hike. Apparently “The Shadow Brigade” has really good night time vision as she didn’t need a flash light to navigate Lands End. Sadly what I have in walking skills I lack in night vision so I definitely needed one. To be honest now we’re getting into the part of the perimeter trail that I really dreaded because doing this at night is definitely suboptimal.

Originally after Lands End we would go up the Presidio by Baker beach and go to Golden Gate Bridge. However I decided against that because we get quite close to cars and I’ll be honest I really don’t want to get right next to cars driving by when it's night time. So I organized a strategic pivot to have us instead walk along Geary St and get to Ferry building and go that way

Geary St -> Tenderloin

At this point I’m honestly pretty bored. One thing I realized about myself doing long walks is past a certain point. It's less because it's fun and more about achieving a goal. For example when I did the 45 mile walk I would not have persisted as long as I did solely because I thought it was fun. I did it because I wanted to achieve my 45 mile goal. But I’ve already walked to the Ferry building before. I’ve done the perimeter trail. There is nothing really new for me to achieve here.

With that I suggested to “The Shadow Brigade” that we stop the walk and take pride in what we achieved so far. “The Shadow Brigade” was not having it though. She was adamant that we finish. I think because she hasn’t done this before she was still chasing the novelty of achieving the goal. She also reminded me (multiple times) that it was my fault for suggesting this in the first place. I’ll admit she has a point. If we did the double cross like she originally suggested we would have been done many hours ago.

But the past is in the past so it was time to persist. As we walked along Geary St we first set an intermediary goal of getting her boba like she wanted. By this point it was 8PM on a Sunday so many places were closed. I found Little Sweet was still open so eventually we made it there but sadly it didn’t meet her particular boba standards (i.e. that the place has seats).

One interesting thing to note here is that while she had more mental energy than I did I certainly had more physical energy. We reached the point where she wanted to sit down roughly once every 10 minutes or so. Most of these sitdowns were at bus stops where I tried to not so subtly suggest we stop to which she with as much subtly reminded me of who it was that suggested this walk.

After a certain point we get to where Geary st meets the Tenderloin which brings us to …

Tenderloin -> The Ferry Building

This is the final part of the walk. Originally “The Shadow Brigade” didn’t want to walk through The TL so the plan would have been to stop right before. However just as we were getting to The Tenderloin she wanted it on the record that I gave up before she did. I refused to admit such things and said I”d be down to go to The Ferry Building if she is. And with that our battle of pride led us to walk straight through and finish.

To be honest this was the best thing that could have happened for me. I didn’t have a clear motivation for this last part but a battle of pride was exactly what I needed! With that I was back to almost prime form. I could feel a clear boost as I was determined to finish what we started.

We walked straight through TL dodging many cases of vomit on the street and eventually made it onto Market street. Honestly even 30+ miles in I feel like I could have run there. It's like I got a second wind!

Eventually we made it and finished our walk.
