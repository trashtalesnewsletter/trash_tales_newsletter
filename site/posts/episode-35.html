Monday

Today I mainly commune with the code and go home and chill.

Tuesday

Today after my daily code whispering I go to they gym with “The Quant” and “The Professor”. I manage to increase my plank PR from 1:40 to 2 minutes. I think I probably go even better next week. I do some back workouts which I’m surprisingly good at relative to the other workouts. I suspect this is because I use a machine for these as opposed to free weights which give me a greater margin of error without having to work on my form.

When I do the free weights like dumbbell presses I’m never quite able to get the form right. I either forget to tighten my abs, keep my elbows right, or something else like that. I think I’m slowly getting better but still far from perfect.

Wednesday

Today after delving into the code I go to run club. The run itself is relatively straight forward. I was worried after skipping for two weeks post marathon I wouldn’t be able to run but I was happy to see that was false.

After the run I hang out with “La Tacuqueria Royalty”, “The Climber”, and “Sergant Sassy”. We go to The Port of Peri Peri which has some great Portugese food. You can tell its delicious because I left a Yelp Review for it.

We wound up spending pretty much the whole dinner talking about a variety of unhinged topics. Originally I wanted to go into detail but after telling some of my other friends IRL I decided not to here based on their reactions. Just know it was very amusing though and everyone there was a G. The one thing I can say though is that me and “The Climber” did some great improv around me having a pet duck by the name of Charles which was amazing. Also chunky milk is apparently more nutritious than non chunky from what I heard. The more you know right.

Thursday

Today before work I decide to go to Thursday morning run club. Generally I don’t actually run at this run club, instead I just show up for the post run hangout. However last week I promised tow of the people that I was running with there that I would run so I decided to this time.I was a bit nervous when I woke up because I felt some knee pain. Originally I told myself that it was nothing but about a quarter of a mile into the run I quickly realized it was not in fact nothing. My knee was searing and I had to call it quits. Thankfully this is the run club that I’m far less invested in but it was still annoying. It also turned out the people I made that promise to didn’t even show up so it was all for naught. Ah well. At this run club I meet someone who was friends with one of my college roomates. Truly a small world.

After another day going to war with my code I go to bar night over at The Lark. I’m joined by “The Philosopher," “The Juggler” and “The Bartender” as per usual but also “Always causing a Racquet". Its his birthday today so I thought it would be fun to invite him! Bar night is fun as per usual.

I do get an interesting piece of feedback that I tend to repeat jokes to the point of it being annoying. As someone who thinks my telling jokes is a key part of the value I bring I do spend some time ruminating on how I can fix this. One example which was brought up was that I tend to ask a common ice breaker question a lot “Whats something cool or interesting about you that people wouldn’t expect?” When I got home I spent some time thinking of other fun ice breakers I could use to break the monotony. I came up with some fun examples which I”m looking forward to testing out.

Another consequence is I may need to retire some of my more common jokes that I use like my joke that goes something along the lines of “My jokes tend to bomb. Its ok though because I’m Muslim”. I do really like the joke but I guess if I’ve been telling it to the point of it being stale I may need to sunset it. I’m sure I’ll come up with new material but I was a bit bummed to hear that.

Friday

Today after wrestling my code into a chokehold I go to GGP and briefly hangout with “The Professor” and “The Pickleballer” at The Conservatory of Flowers. Unfortunately due to upcoming KBBQ plans I don’t spend a long time here but it was nice to chat amidst the background music of Outside Lands.

On my way between Conservatory of Flowers and Kogi Gogi I get jumped by 3 small dogs. They don’t actually bite me but they all get very close. I was never a fan of small dogs and this definitely did not help. I audibly screamed for them to back off and tried to stand my ground. It really was a good thing those small rat bastards ar all bark and no bite. For completely unrelated reasons I briefly thought about the movie Old Yeller.

Once that crisis was averted I make my way over to KBBQ where I meet up with “The Boxer,” “The Pasta Prince,” and “The Quant”. Kogi Gogi is great as usual. It turns out that a while back one of the waiters there who I know on a first name basis bought some Pokemon cards from “The Quant”. I was hoping to see him and ask how he was liking them but he wasn’t working there today. The KBBQ slapped as per usual. I learned that “The Pasta Prince’s” dad worked in Pakistan for a while and had an assistant by the name of Iqbal which I thought was pretty funny.

Saturday

The day begins with trash pickup as per usual. Nothing particularly surprising here. I would have liked to stay longer but I had a belated birthday party to go to for “The Plower!”.

For this surprise party I do something a little sneaky. One of the interesting benefits to writing this newsletter is it gets me into the habit of sending a weekly text to everyone who gets it which proves useful for keeping up with friends who I may not see all the time. One such friend is “The Kpop dancer” who moved down to L.A a while ago. She told me she was planning to come up in Sepetember. Since “The Professor” will be leaving the Bay soon I convince her to move up her trip. Nobody knew she was coming other than me. I tell her to come to “The Plower”’s birthday party as a surprise to everyone. It was extra funny because she assumed I would have told everyone she was coming only to learn shortly before arriving that I did not in fact tell anyone as evidenced by this text exchange

As I expected the surprise went great and everyone was excited to see her! When I catch up with her it was cool to see that she already knew a lot both about what I was up to and our friend group through my newsletter.

We start off the birthday at Plow as would be expected for “The Plower’s” birthday party. When we get there we learn that the wait time will be 2 hours. I try my best to bribe the waiters with some Thai currency worth about $3 USD but sadly it doesn’t seem they are that interested in moving on up in the world as I don’t take my Thai money. I briefly contemplated bribing them with my fake money used solely as a movie prop but decided against it since anyone uncultured enough to decline Thai currency probably wouldn’t be swayed by this either. The old saying of casting pearls before swine is truly applicable here.

We have a pretty large party so we don’t all get to sit at the same table. Since I wind up not sitting with

After Plow we go to my place to play some Secret Hitler. Huge props to “The Quant” for helping out the fascists as a liberal by being an agent of chaos. I’m especially proud because that's exactly the sort of thing I’d do as a liberal.

From there we all go down to Prime BBQ over in San Mateo. I went to this place before and while the meat was good it was overpriced as heck. Because of that I made a commitment to not eat there and instead write a Yelp Review.

Since The Plower is into post cards I’ve been following a tradition for the last 3 birthdays of making one for her with photos of us from the time since her last birthday. This is this years!

After a good hearty meal that I didn’t eat we go to the Hillsdale mall and enjoy the finest scenery one could find there which in all fairness was better than the Westfield mall over in SF. I felt like a teenybopper going to the Lego store and then the food court.

Sunday

Before the usual Sunday street sweep I have a call with my Mom. To be honest amidst all the stuff I’ve been doing I haven’t been calling home as much as I should. I generally call about once a week but because work got really busy that wound up being less. I feel bad because I do like talking to my Mom and its important to keep family close so I am going to make sure to make that more of a priority going forward.

My Mom did say she missed my calls which was what prompted the call this morning and a goal to be more consistent with this.

After that I go to trash pickup. I catch up with “The Hombre” and “The Yogi”. This also winds up being a quick trash pickup as I go to get brunch with “The Professor,” “The Kpop dancer,” “The Pickleballer,” “The Game master,” “The Attorney,” and “High Entropy” over in Duboce park. Between yesterday and today this weekend definitely takes me back to a couple years ago when this group of friends was my primary group of friends. It feels interesting looking back then vs now to see how things have changed. Due to life and all that we don’t all get to see each other as often as we’d like so I’m glad we got this chance today.

We spend a while soaking up the sun over in Duboce. Part way into it we play Gomoku which in case y’all aren’t aware is kinda like Chinese connect 4 except its 5 in a row and you can connect diagonally as well as vertically and horizontally. I’m generally not very good at this game and quickly lose to “The Professor”. I then wind up playing against “The K-pop dancer” in a 15-20 minute long battle royale which I wind up eking out a win in. We then play a second game where she manages to snatch defeat from the jaws of victory. For context she was about to setup a 4 in a row that I couldn’t block but was just missing a single piece. This was explained to me and I was told I should concede. I decide not to and drag the game out and go for a 4 in a row that she could have blocked. Instead of blocking she decided to complete the 4 in a row not realizing I just needed to put my 5th piece and win the game. Had she blocked me she would have eventually been able to complete that 4 in a row I couldn’t block and win. She jokingly mentioned that she didn’t want to shame her Chinese ancestors and unfortunately that might have just done it. Considering my Paki ancestors never played the game I don’t think they really cared about my win.

After hanging out more in the park we get dinner over at Nakama sushi which I decide to cheat on my diet and eat at. Afterwards I go home and write up the newsletter.
