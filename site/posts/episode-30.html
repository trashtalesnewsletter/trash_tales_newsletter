Monday

Today is mainly work. After last week we restructured the project and moved things around to account for the fact that we weren’t gonna meet the original deadlines. We still want to get stuff out so will be doing my best of course but now I won’t have the last thought when I go to sleep and the first one when I wake up be this project. I was debating going to chess club but opted not to because of something else I’m planning (stay tuned to find out what that is).

Tuesday

Same as Monday, no real change here except for the fact that I’m another day closer to the great sleep.

Wednesday

Today after work I *finally* go to Midnight Runners after the first time in what feels like centuries. Today at this run I’m joined by “The Quant” as is often the case but today I see 2 rare Pokemon show up being “The Enabler” and “The Dentist” and one less rare Pokemon “La Tacqueria Royalty” (in other words “The Enabler” squad). Its great to see them all but this run presents an extra challenge as they all run faster than I usually do. I manage to mostly keep up with them somehow but while they are casually strolling through the run I have to put in reasonable effort to keep up with those speed demons.

Between the miles we stop and do bootcamp activities like burpees, squats, and so on. To make sure everyone else is doing the activities properly I take on a supervisor role and oversee people rather than do the activities myself. I employ such motiativational tools as telling people I’m docking their pay if I ever see them slacking.

After the run we hang out at Underdogs where I see “The Pasta Prince” working. When we’re taking the MUNI back I ask “The Enabler” how his friends would describe him in 3 words. Astute readers may notice the callback to a few weeks ago where my Dad asked me the same question. I got a couple responses from my friends which included (“Eccentric”, “Thoughtful”, “Committed”) and (“Contrarian (in a good way)”, “Relentless”, “Inquisitive”). Sadly I only asked this question when we had one MUNI stop left and there wasn’t enough time to answer. I did tell him I’d ask him again when we next see each other which looks like next week so I look forward to getting an answer then.

Thursday

Work is unusually quiet today. I suspect because many people took the day off or are working remotely its unusually quiet and I get into the zone much more than usual. After an above average day of productivity I go to Downtown First Thursday.

When I get there the first thing I do is watch people do a hot dog contest where they have to eat a hotdog while wearing a blindfold and not using their hands. Its truly a sight to behold. My first impression is that these people were pre selected to go which is corroborated by the fact that the host references one of them as a previous champion.

I find to my surprise though that after the first round they are doing another one for volunteers. As soon as the host says that my hand shoots up. Do I think I’ll win? Of course not. Does it sound like fun to try, heck yeah!

I get up on stage and partake in the festivities. Manny the main organizer of both Downtown First Thursdays and Trash Pickup is in the audience so he records a video of me which he shares to a group chat. Unfortunately its hard to share videos in a newsletter so heres a screenshot I took when I uploaded the video to insta.

To clear up a common misconception this isn’t who can eat the most hotdogs, this is who can eat a single hot dog the fastest.

I do terribly as expected only being able to eat half the weiner in the time it takes for 1st place to finish but it was still quite fun. Apparently if you are able to eat weiner really fast you get a special invite to The Castro but thats at a level far above me.

After this I meet up with “The Raja of Rhythm”, one of his friends from his Motorcycle class, and someone they just met at the fair. The person they just met is only gonna be in the Bay Area for a short time (a month). Between her and “The Shadow Brigade” thats two separate people I spontaneously met at an event run by The Civic Joy Fund that grew up in the Bay, moved away, and are only gonna be here for a few weeks. Seriously, what are the odds? I was waiting for this girl to say she is also in the army reserves or plays the harp or something to add another layer to the coincidences but sadly that didn’t come. (I later realized I actually saw this girl on Hinge like a year and a half ago or so, small world am I right?)

Admittedly nothing at Downtown First Thursdays can top the wiener fest above but its still a great time. We check out an Orchestra playing jazz which is great (and also makes me think of “The Shadow Brigade”).

We then go to a bar / music venue that has a bunch of skateboards on the wall as a form of art.

After admiring these I note to myself that the situation calls for a bit of a change of pace and as if on queue we see “The Dentist”, “La Tacqueria Royalty”, and a couple of “The Dentist”’s friends. We all go and check out some of the live music happening outside skateboard bar which is more party vibes than the orchestra music we saw earlier.

Unfortunately there is a party dynamic known as “Humza’s law” which states that a group of sufficiently large size that doesn’t all know each other at a party is gonna break away into smaller groups. This happened and as a result I spent a good while ping ponging between “The Raja of Rhythm group” and “The Enabler” group. The former mainly hung out at the Orchestra and the latter at one of the live music stages. Apparently the live stage had a DJ called “Washed out” playing that “The Dentist” is a big fan of.

After a while “The Raja of Rhythm group” all wound up going home which was bad news for “The Enabler” group because now they were stuck with me for the rest of the night. We hung around the live stage for a while before checking out some of the live dancing they had. They had a DJ playing throwbacks like “Soulja boy” and “I know you want me” while we all danced to them. Once this ended the festival was basically over.

I met a college student repping an Attack on Titan jacket who I recommended various things in SF to and some guy who looked like he had too much to drink lamenting the fact that he didn’t invest in NVIDIA back in 2022.

While I’m no “The Quant” I tried my best to give the guy some investment advice regarding thinking about what he believes is likely to go up over a long time horizon and ignore short term market fluctuations. I was about a quarter of the way through my spiel when “The Dentist” and some of her friends said it was time to go. She said me and “La Tacuqeria royalty” were being too friendly talking to random strangers like this which may be true but you know there is a reason southern hospitality isn’t called Bay Area hospitality and I kinda wanna change that :D .

As we are leaving the festival we run into some of “La Tacqueria royalty”’s friends who want to go to some bars. I’m the only one from our group who joins them while everyone else heads home. Hmm I should probably come up with a name for the people I’m with right? Well I don’t know them well enough to give em a nickname so lets call them Aditya, Maggie, Angelo, and Will because those are their real names. We all go to a tiki bar called Zombievillage where everyone except me and Maggie gets drinks. I learn here that Maggie apparently knows some of my trash friends from the Saturday trash pickup so I try getting her to come. She even says she may come this Saturday. Stay tuned to find out if she does. After a while I get tired and decide to make a last minute hot date with my bed.

Friday

I start the day off with a nice long walk through GGP and down to Embarcadero and eventually over to “The Professor”’s place over in Mission Bay to play board games with “The pickeballer”, “The Quant”, “The Plower”, and some of our other friends. We play One Night werewolf where me and “The Quant” manage to snatch victory from the jaws of defeat in a truly stunning Werewolf victory. It was truly a work of art that I can’t properly convey in a newsletter like this. I even tried warning everyone by proudly proclaiming I was a Werewolf but our genius was far too much for a mere warning like that to get in the way of our victory. I think we played some other games as well here but after that Werewolf victory thats all a footnote.

Most other people head off to a BBQ place in San Mateo and I would have loved to go with them but I have something far grander to go to, “The notetaker”’s 4th of July party! This party is a DEI flag party where we all have to wear an outfit inspired by a flag that isn’t just red, white, and blue (in other words no USA, Britain, France, etc.) I decide to go with a flag that doesn’t have red, white, or blue and Jamaica is one of the only flags like that :D.

As part of this we were supposed to come with some fun facts about the country in question so here are some for Jamacia

Ian Fleming the author of the James Bond series wrote them all in Jamacia

Jamacia was the first caribbean country to have a website launching http://jamaicatravel.com in 1994.

1/3rd of the people in Jamaica live in Kingston.

Originally I wanted to go with Djibouti so I could wear a shirt like this

But I couldn't get the shirt delivered on time.

As an extra challenge I decided to speak in a Jamaican accent for the entire party. To help you understand what thats like I’ll write out the rest of the party in a similar tone to how I spoke.

Mon that was a really fun party mon. I got greeted by so many of the old friends mon I got to see “The Raja of Rhythm”, “The Yogi”, “Granola Girlie”, “The Pasta Prince”, and of course “The notetaker” mon. Speaking this way mon, it was really fun for an hour or two mon. The challenges mon they came up after that mon. My tongue was sputtering mon. It was really tiring mon. As I got to the 5.5 hour mark mon, my mouth was crying out for the mercy mon. We went up to the roof to see fireworks mon. The cold mon she lunged upon us mon. To fight the cold we all huddled penguin style mon. Apparently the party went to 3am mon. I would have loved to stay mon. I was tired mon. And I couldn’t keep up the accent mon. I went to bed at 1 mon but I feel like an old mon by comparison mon.

Man even just writing that out was tiring!

Saturday

The day begins with trash pickup as per usual. We have a low of about 13 people which is below what we usually get these days. Its likely because people are either hung over, out of town, or both. For all of you who were waiting on the edge of your seats to find out if Maggie came to trash pickup … she didn’t. We’ll get her next time though!

After trash pickup I go to prep for a board game night at my place and walk through GGP. Board game night starts and … surprise! Its not a board game night! Its a surprise party for “The Professor”! Ever since I knew that “The Professor” got the job I’ve been planning for the last several weeks to throw him a surprise party and tonight was finally the night! I employed his younger sister “The Pickeballer” to stall him until we were all ready, I got everyone in my apartment, and when he opened the door to come in we all yelled “Congrats Professor!”.

I decorated my apartment for it as well.

This was the first surprise party I ever threw for someone and in his words he said he was surprised and liked the gesture so we are calling it a success!

We played Secret Hitler in which “The Professor” pulled off a miraculous gamble that won us the first round for the fascists. In the second round where I was also a fascist I apparently had really bad acting which lost us the game. Overall a big win but a little bit of a big sad.

I came up with an idea for the party that everyone dress in a labcoat and a beanie to mimic his style which works great … for everyone who remembered to do it. Sadly I am not in the group that remembered to do it but since I organized the party I’m gonna honorarily say that I did it. So good job me!

Today I also learn that the professor is leaving the Bay on August 18. The iminence of the deadline really hits me as he is one of my closest friends here. This now means I need to accelerate some plans. The two of us were already planning to organize a scavenger hunt together but now we need to do that ASAP. Also I have another event I want to organize. This will be one of the biggest things I’ve ever tried to pull off and I really want him to be here for this so I gotta make haste and move, make the event ,advertise, and get all my ducks in a row for it to be ready to go. Lets do it!

Sunday

The day begins with me going to a pilates class in the Marina with a pumpkin spice latte in hand! Nah I’m just messing with ya, it begins with trash pickup. Today is a momentous occasion as I finally get “The Professor” to come after 2+ years of trying to convince him. Sure he may have come a half hour late but this is progress. If I’m lucky I may be able to get a full pickup out of him before he leaves the Bay.

During this trash pickup I officially signed up for the SF half marathon at the end of July. I haven’t really trained and don’t plan on either except for Midnight runners but it’ll be fine I think. I do expect to get my butt kicked by “La Tacqueria royalty” and “The Dentist” but thats expected.

After trash pickup we do the usual post trash pickup hang. We hang out in Dolo where we see a magic show. Not gonna lie the magic show wasn’t that great. The performer made *way* too many jokes that weren’t that great and the actual trick was pretty easy to see if you know what to look for. Essentially he took a card that I signed and gave it back to me. He did a bit of a fakeout where he pretended he lost it and “switched” it but you could see him do a last minute swap.

Also the sharpie I used to sign the card may have been in his mouth at first … I made sure to wash my hands after that.

From there I hung out more with “The Raja of Rhythm” and “Always causing a racquet”. After that I went home and wrote up the newsletter.
