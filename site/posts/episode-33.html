Monday

Today begins with another day in the Python mines. The big project I’m working on looks like it might finally be wrapping up. Its been a hard fought battle with many ups and downs, but fingers crossed we may finally have something we’re happy with.

After work I go to the gym with “The Professor” and “The Quant”. Now that I’ve improved my fashion to a degree the next step in making me less ugly is going to the gym. To be honest this was MUCH harder than I thought it would be for a variety of reasons.

One of the first things was that a lot of the time spent at the gym was focusing on form. I tried squats, plancks, dumbbell curls. The biggest thing those have in common is that my form sucks for all of them. A lot of it was small but admittedly important things like keeping my back straight, my feet shoulder with apart and other such things. I didn’t really get to lift at my maximum potential and worse I didn’t get to use what I think is one of my stronger muscles. My sheer will. I was able to take on the Humza trail. I was able to do something else we will cover later in the newsletter. And yet I wasn’t able to do more than 10 squats. This was mainly because my form started deteriorating and I was told to stop but even still it felt frustrating to me.

Interestingly I don’t often feel frustrated when trying to learn or do something new. Generally I have no fear of embarrassing myself in public (something many of you should be well familiar with). I think its because I feel like (absence of form), I should be lifting or doing more than I was. And the fact that I wasn’t sore the next day probably means I was right at least as far as pushing my actual physical limits. But even still it did feel bad nonetheless.

This is a case where my pride definitely got the better of me. And as much as I hate to admit it I think I’m exactly the sort of person who is susceptible to ego lifting. Rationally the correct way of thinking here is to realize this is the start of my journey and it doesn’t really matter. So long as I take things one step, squat, or other repetition at a time and focus on form thats what really matters. Sadly the monkey in my brain strongly disagrees. Big thanks to “The Professor” and “The Quant” for very patiently teaching me all these things.

“The professor” brought up an excellent suggestion that I hire a personal trainer to help me learn form. I think I’ll start looking into that.

Tuesday

Today is just an ordinary day in the python mines. Progress is made towards my project chipping away at it and cracks of light start to emerge.

Wednesday

V0 of the project looks like its basically done. There are some last items here but we have a version we feel ready to start using.

After work I head to Midnight Runners. The run itself was pretty normal. I met a guy who lives in Napa and comes all the way to SF just for this run club. Thats some dedication right there.

Once I have sufficiently shuffled my feet I catch up with some of my friends. Sergeant Sassy’s date from last week went well! Hooray! I also meet a guy who owns his own nightclub. Way back in episode 15 I went to a night club as part of a Midnight Runners party and it turns out its the same club! That was pretty cool!

I also got to put my shuffleboard skills to the test. There was a beer company offering shuffleboard with the person who scored the highest getting a free trip to Belgium. The score to beat was 9. I put my best foot back, best hand forward and mustered all my skills to score … 4. Sadly it seems all my effort was 4 naught. Ah well it was still fun!

Thursday

Before work I go the Almost Friday run club. More specifically I go to the post run hang over at Flywheel. I could have done the run but I’ve crashed the post run hang a few times without running and I’m starting to get known as the guy who does that. I can’t besmirch such a hallowed reputation by doing something like running. I talk with a number of people and inevitably the topic of the marathon comes up. They’re quite surprised when I tell them I plan on rawdogging it. I’m pretty sure they’re more stressed about it than I am :D. One of the folks mentions that she's nervous for the half and another person tells her “Think of this as the celebration of all the training that you’ve done. Theres no reason to be nervous”. A great line! She then turns to me and says “I don’t know what that means for you though.” If i finish this I can’t wait to go back there and tell them!

Today while I was at work I came across an interesting tweet that got me thinking about the question “what are you trying to avoid feeling by doing what you do”

I quite liked the honesty in the answer in the tweet above and thought I’d give it a thought about myself.

One obvious one for me is that I hate feeling bored so I constantly seek out different activities and trying different things. If I find myself on a weekend with nothing to do I’ll try to either find some activity or rally around friends to do something. Overall I think its been a net positive for me because I love trying new things and avoiding the feeling of boredom has been a good motivator for me to continually try to push my comfort zone. That being said though I do sometimes wish I was better at being bored. One thing I want to try but am a bit scared to is purposefully declining social plans and just sitting in my apartment and not really doing anything just to build muscle. Its not fun but maybe it might be good for me.

On a related note I can be bad with FOMO. If theres an event happening without me or I need to leave early I always wonder what I might miss out on. Maybe theres some really cool story, maybe I miss the chance to learn something really interesting about one of my friends. Perhaps even some after event where the people there get to bond closely about something. To that end I have a very hard time leaving things early. There are times especially late at night where I do kinda want to go home and sleep or chill but I’m worried something really cool will happen. This isn’t rational but it is a feeling of mine.

Finally for a more minor one I really hate making others wait for me so I really hate being late. Occasionally when I’m running late or close to, I flash back to the 5th grade when my teacher told me that if you're late it means you're disrespecting someone's time. Thanks Mrs. Shern, glad you’re still with me 20+ years later 😅.

After work I head over for bar night with the lads. I saw “The Philosopher,” “That's nuts,” “The Mosher,” “The Juggler,” as per usual, but what I didn’t expect was seeing “Granola Girlie” and “The Writer”. It was cool to see all these people in one place. I also learned that the bartender at the bar we usually go to is a fellow One Piece fan! Sadly he's not up to date on the manga but its still sick to learn.

As the night goes on I find myself getting really tired around 10pm or so. This is one case where I probably should have gone home and slept but I did have some FOMO and wanted to stay. By the time 11 rolled around I had had enough and decided to just sleep regardless. It was still a super fun time though!

Friday

The last necessary pieces I need to do for the project are done. There is still room for improvement but we’re at a state where its good enough and we have bigger fish to fry. My project is ultimately a small part in a bigger project we need to get soon so the battle still very much rages on. Nevertheless I appreciate this victory and will savor it to the best of my abilities.

I also happened to find another interesting tweet at work that got me thinking.

I realize that I am very much a # 2. While I’m not someone who necessarily has grand dreams of changing the world I think I am someone who tries to be pretty impactful in my local community and the part I don’t always spell out is because I want to be someone who matters to the people that I care about not just because I want to help the people I care about (of course that is relevant too). Theres no real followup to this I just thought it was an interesting tweet.

After work I host a board game night with “Always causing a racquet,” “The boxer,” “The Plower,” “The Juggler,” and “The pasta prince”. We play secret Hitler. Nothing particularly unusual here but someone did drop the great line “I’m excited to be Hitler in the most socially acceptable context”.

Saturday

I make a brief trip to the slums known as the Marina to pick up my bib for the half marathon. I see a whole bunch of people crowded around some race expo thing a ma jig and worry this will be the line for bib pickup and I’ll need to miss trash pickup. Such worries are quickly assuaged when I find that bib pickup only takes about 5 minutes.

From there I head to trash pickup. We have a pretty solid turnout today with 27 people and between “The Philosopher,” “The Politician," “The Grandma” and more its quite the party.

After trash pickup I head home to deep clean my apartment. Actually scratch that a more accurate version is I head home to supervise people I pay to deep clean my apartment. It had been a while since my apartment had gotten one and given my utter laziness at this stuff I decide to utilize my software engineer powers and throw money at problems I don’t want to solve myself. While this is happening I play online Dune with “The Quant,” “The Professor,” and “The Taco”.

Once my apartment gets the TLC it so desperately needs I wander around for a while before going to “The Writer’s” housewarming party.

At this party I heavily put my improv skills to the test. When someone asks what I do for fun I mention trash pickup and when they ask how I got into it I mention that my parole officer got me into it. This leads to them asking how I wound up in prison and I say that 5 years ago I did something which combines the best parts of arson with the worst parts of insider trading and follow up by insisting that whoever is listening is a smart cookie that can figure it out. When they inevitably can’t I tell them that 5 years ago I was really into investing and thought for sure Apple would do poorly so I decided to short it. When they didn’t I wanted to make my short calls a reality by trying to burn down the Cupertino office. I had staked it out and done my research however things went wrong when the security guard who usually covers a previous shift showed up at a time when there wasn’t supposed to be any personnel. The reason he showed up was because he was having marriage problems at home and wanted time away from what he described as “The old nail and coffin”. While in prison I supposedly learned to make prison wine and got really good at not dropping the soap. I dropped various hints like how I was supposed to be released after 500 days but due to good behavior I got out after 420.

Most people naturally didn’t believe me but their reactions were great. One group tried to look me up and couldn’t find my case. Another definitely didn’t believe me but I think they were impressed with my ability to come up with stuff on the spot and kept asking me follow up questions which was really fun. I felt a bit bad for the third group where one person actually did believe me and said “woah I never met someone who went to prison before”. When it was explained she was genuinely surprised and thought I actually went to prison! I guess there a lesson in there somewhere but like many math textbooks I’ll leave it as an exercsise for the reader.

Sadly I do actually need to leave the party early (around 11:30) because I have to get up at 5 for the half marathon. It sucked because I was feeling wide awake and was having a really good time! I also got this picture of me that might be Hinge worthy.

Sunday

I wake up at 5AM to head out for the half. On the way down I take the N-Owl which I was expecting to be pretty sketchy but sure enough it was full of runners and more lively than a bus at 5AM had any right to be.

I get to the Ferry building and am greeted by a flurry of folks

I spend half an hour milling around before getting into position. The way this works is everyone is in a different corral and the start times are a bit staggered. Originally my goal is to finish within 3 hours so I go in the last corral. Once the event officially starts it feels like forever until my corral can actually go. Eventually the moment of truth comes and I set off. I’ll break this down into a few parts.

The first 5 minutes

Whenever I start running I always need some time to warm up. Subsequently the first part of any run tends to feel sluggish. Initially I run without any music which lasts for all of 5 minutes before I realize I need the music to focus. It truly is incredible just how well music can help with that.

The first 8 miles

Whenever I do something like this I tend to play only a few songs on repeat and switch whenever I get tired of them. To kick things off I play one of my favorite feel good songs “Staying Alive”. As its playing I clear the first 4 or so miles. Once I get past the initial slowness things start going alright. I’m able to maintain a consistent 9:38 pace or so for the first many miles. If the half marathon is a video game we reach our first miniboss once we get through Fisherman’s Wharf and get to the hill near Fort Mason.

This hill isn’t actually too bad and I manage to run up it without too much difficulty. After clearing the hill I decide to switch to my second song that I’ll put on repeat, the Aqua Teen Hunger Force first opening song. From Fort Mason things go pretty smoothly for a while. There is a bit of uphill when we get to the bridge but its manageable. From there we get to The Golden Gate Bridge and our beloved Karl the Fog.

Around here I switch the song to “Moves like Jagger”.

Miles 8 to 11

After crossing the bridge I’m starting to feel it. My knees and legs hurting. My morale starts taking a bit of a dip. Of course I’ve made it this far and its not like I’m gonna quit but I definitely feel myself slowing down. To help myself out I imagine different friends cheering me on in my head, or in the case of “The Philosopher” saying this will turn out like the bean challenge if I don’t finish. I switch things up to “Not Like Us” for the next little while. Once we cross the bridge we go down close to Sausalito near some of the forts. This part wasn’t too bad but a lurch started developing within me. Since we were going downhill that meant we needed to go back up hill. Every step down was another step up. Every pant for air going down was 3 pants for air going back up. We see the bridge above us and a hill which brings us to

THE FREAKIN HILL

This is the true boss of the half marathon.

I won’t mince my words here. THIS PART SUCKED. I HATED IT. I had to muster everything I had to keep myself in the fight here. It was truly an ordeal. Eventually though I made it through. Make no mistake though I did bear hill will towards this boss.

Finishing up

Now it was time to cross the bridge and go back into SF

This is my first time crossing the bridge on this side since the time I accidentally did it back in 2022 leading to a bridge security guard to think I was suicidal, but thats a story for another time. With the hill finished I was in high spirits and switched back to “Staying Alive” to really give myself the boost I needed. I’m starting to feel myself pick up and I start singing aloud a little as I go across the bridge. Once I cross the bridge I find that there is a last bit of uphill. This is certainly not boss level not even mini boss level but it feels worse than it is because I was expecting this to end once I crossed the bridge.

Interestingly the last bit of the race can feel like one of the most arduous because mentally I feel like I’ve already finished but I haven’t physically. Originally I wanted the first One Piece opening “We are” to be the last song I had for this half but I fat fingered it and went with Aqua Teen Hugner Force again and was too lazy to change it. With the finish line in sight its finally time

I cross the finish line and end it!

Not the best time in the world but considering I didn’t really train for it I consider this a win!

As the quote from above goes “Think of this as the celebration of all the training that you’ve done. Theres no reason to be nervous”. Perhaps in my case the quote should be amended to “Think of this as the celebration of you being willing to commit to the bit no matter how crazy. Theres no reason to be nervous.” I can’t wait to go back to the Almost Friday run club and see the look on their faces when I tell them I finished :D

Soon after crossing the finish line I run into “The Dentist” who did the same half as me. I wind up finishing around 9. Originally my goal was to finish around 9:30 so I could make it to trash pickup at 10. While my finish time was considerably ahead of what I thought it would be the time it takes to leave the half marathon finish line is also considerably longer than I thought it would be. The roads are blocked, the Ubers are $40, and the only other way out is shuttles which takes forever. We were legitamiltey waiting for 30+ minutes to get on a shuttle from The Presidio to The Ferry Building. To all the trashers reading this, sorry I did try to make it but I couldn’t :( :( .

After getting back to The Ferry Building I go to Rise and Set to get brunch with “The Dentist”, “The Enabler”, “Tight Knit”, “La Tacqueria royalty”, and some of our other run friends. It was quite fun debriefing on the half marathon, complaining about the hill and just vibing. Since I completed the half marathon I’m cheating on my diet today and got a Wagyu burger which was delicious!! While talking I learned there are these things called gels that people apparently eat which contain protein. Also in case y’all didn’t know apparently there are small water bottles you can carry around in a belt bag in case you need water. There are a whole bunch of crazy optimizations one can make to make the process easier 🤯.

Once we wrap up brunch we go and get some donuts over at Bob’s donuts. I’m not cheating on my diet *that* much so its more of an I watch folks eat. Sadly on the way there I do wind up making an enemy of a stranger. We were outside the brunch place and since there was a big group of us someone tries to go through us and says “excuse me”. I was feeling playful and decided to respond “Your application for an excusal has been granted and approved”. I meant this in a joking way but the guy did not appreciate it as he shot me a look and said “you must be real funny”. One of my friends “La Tacqueria Royalty” defended me by saying “hes the funniest guy I know”. Thanks man, you’re a real one! Some other folks did concede that what I said could come off as obnoxious, and while they wouldn’t say anything they could see why he would be annoyed. Personally I still think it was funny but it is what it is I guess. Perhaps I should be more careful around strangers.

Once I have my fill of watching people eat donuts I go to the Mission to hang out with “The Professor,” “The Pickleballer,” and “The Taco”. After hanging out in Dolo and getting Mexican food I go home to write the newsletter.
