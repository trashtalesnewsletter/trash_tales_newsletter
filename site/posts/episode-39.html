Episode 39

Tuesday

This issue picks up with me getting into Lyon. Upon arrival we quickly check into our hotel and get some lunch. As we walk around we’re greeted with some gorgeous sites right outside the hotel

At this point we’re pretty hungry and desperate so most of the group goes to an Italian place while I go to a Mickey Ds. This starts a bit of light bullying where the rest of the group gangs up on me for wanting to go to McDonalds. In my defense I only eat McDonalds on vacation since I try to eat healthy when normally so this is my only real chance to do it. But apparently eating it every day is considered “wrong” for reasons that continue to allude me. I did learn an important lesson though that a maple bbq burger does not in fact taste good.

Afterwards me and “The Quant” go up to this big castle pictured above and it looks pretty cool.

We also got a great view of Lyon from the top of the castle

I walk around a bit more both with “The Quant” and by myself before dinner time. We all (minus “The Plower”) go to a french place where I get some steak and fries which is perfectly ok. As we wrap up dinner I ask the waiter for the check and he comments that “The Pickeballer” is still eating to which I respond “So?” The European mind can’t comprehend the American mindset of wanting to get the check so quickly. Generally dining here is a more leisurely experience unlike in America where folks are generally rushed more to finish. The waiter was definitely taken aback by my flippant remark. We then head back to the hotel and sleep.

Wednesday

Today me and “The Driver” go out and get some coffee / pastries before everyone else wakes up. Considering that we get a coffee and a matcha and stay there for about an hour and a half we truly get the European experience of sitting around a cafe for a while which was actually pretty nice. By that point the rest of the gang had woken up and we met them over for brunch at some place where I got both pancakes and waffles. The waitress gave me an interesting look and everyone else piped in and said I was American :P. Yeah once I get back I go back on the diet hard.

Afterwards we go to the station and take the train to Zürich. All in all Lyon was a cute city which we picked at random so we didn’t have a really long train to Zürich but it wound up being a keeper. I do think that one day and one night was the perfect amount of time for it. By this point I had seen pretty much everything worth seeing.

After 4 hours and one 12 minute transfer at Mullhouse we get to Zürich. If you ever find yourself there one of the first things you need to know is that it is EXPENSIVE. And I mean more EXPENSIVE than SF. To give you an idea, a typical meal at a restaurant may wind up costing you 36 USD. Yes you read that right. 36 USD. Never before have I thought to myself “Oh SF prices are pretty reasonable compared to city X”. But I guess that day is today.

While on the train to Zürich “The Professor” tries to get us all to download the app for managing Zürich public transit which brings us to …

Humza reviews foreign public transit Zürich edition

Hey there you numtots! We’re back it for another foreign public transit review. This time taking on the center of Switzerland itself Zürich. As a recap here are the criteria used. 1. Frequency of trains 2. Coverage of transit 3. Ease of paying 4. Reliability of transit

Frequency of trains

This one gets an A. I never found myself needing to wait more than 5 minutes for a train to get me where I needed to go.

Coverage of transit

This one also gets an A. I was able to take public transit all the way out to the feet of mountains I climbed back to downtown and all around.

Ease of Paying

Alright this is by far the most controversial but I’m giving this a B-. In order to pay for public transit here we used an app called SBB Mobile. The reason for this is that if you tried buying a ticket based on where you are and where you want to go you have to calculate zone based fare with a zone map that looks something like this

Imagine yourself frantically trying to find the right zone right as the train approaches

What the SBB app does is you give it your location plus a credit card and you swipe on the app to say you are starting public transit and swipe again when you finish and it takes a look at your start and end location and calculates the fare for you and charges your credit card.

That seems like a pretty fare system except for the fact that almost all our credit cards couldn’t be saved due to an error in the app. I know this technically isn’t the transit system’s fault itself but when we’re all trying for like 40 minutes to connect credit cards and only two people can manage that is really saying something. Shout out to “The Driver” for somehow having his credit card not give some app error which we could all then copy and use for our time here :P.

Reliability of transit

This one gets an A-. Not quite Japan level but pretty solid. Generally I had to wait at most a minute or two after expected time.

Once we got into the city and settled in we got dinner and walked around for a while.

Thursday

I start today off with a solo walk in the morning while everyone else is sleeping. I go to Lindenhof hill and get some really nice views

Once most of everyone else wakes up (me, “The Quant,” “The Professor,” and “The Pickeballer”) we get breakfast and go to the Lindt museum of chocolate. For those who don’t know Lindt is one of the big chocolate makers in Switzerland and they have a whole museum dedicated to the craft.

The museum itself was pretty cool! I had learned some of this before but I always forget how intricate the process of making chocolate is. It's also pretty wild that people were able to figure this out thousands of years ago.

Afterwards we meet up at the hotel and we all split off again. I decide to try going shopping with “The Plower” and “The Driver” to see what its like.

Humza reviews shopping with The Plower

I know from what she has told me that “The Plower” likes to shop a lot but since I generally hate shopping I don’t usually witness it. I thought today I may change that. Going shopping with her is kinda like walking a dog with the intention of going to see a particular squirrel only for said dog to get randomly distracted by random squirrels every 3 seconds and you’re getting aggressively dragged as you hold onto the leash.

We originally intended to go to a sunscreen store (for the Korean stuff) and some other random store I can't remember but along the way got distracted by an Aesop, a book store, a knick knack store (for the magnets), and probably other things I’ve forgotten.

It’s mildly amusing to watch for about 30-45 minutes before I realize I need a break to walk around and see random towers so that's what I do in the middle.

I go to a random tower and climb up it for a view that's pretty reasonable

Windy staircases sure are popular here

After taking a break I resume shopping with “The Plower” watching “The Plower” decide whether to buy a book for 15+ minutes before running into “The Quant” who joins our little show. Sadly at this point it starts raining and pretty badly at that so we beeline for the hotel.

We then go out to dinner at a Singaporean place where I get some of the worst Mongolian beef I ever had. It looked very nice but tasted like ice. The rain is still pretty bad and here I fumble by not renting an umbrella from the hotel. I thought I wouldn’t need it but boy was I wrong. I wound up crowding under an umbrella with “The Plower” which was a miserable experience for all involved. We attempt getting gelato afterwards which the rain really transformed from an outing to an ordeal.

I then go back to the hotel and doordash McDonalds because I was put off from dinner.

Friday

The rain is still bad this morning so in order to get my early morning steps I do the unthinkable and go to the hotel gym.

It was ok, I got some steps but certainly not as fun as walking around. Once “The Quant,” “The Pickleballer," and “The Professor” are up the rain comes back to walkable levels and we go and get some breakfast and walk around. We go shopping for a while but in a way that has more direction and less indecisiveness than yesterday.

“The Quant” wanted to go and get some gold so we went to a gold shop and watched him get a gold coin. While in the shop I see a whole bunch of coins which make sense to me, but in the back I see a Texas license plate. I’m not sure why its there so I ask the guy working there who tells me that apparently someone who brought a bunch of stuff to the shop had it in one of the boxes. I was greatly amused to see a Texas license plate in Switzerland so I decided to buy it for the bit.

Afterwards we got some lunch and I decided to peel off from the group to go on a solo hike to the top of Zürich. Specifically I go to Uetilburg. The views here were some of my favorite from the whole trip. It was seriously stunning!

There was also a wedding happening below me that I caught a glimpse of

Many congratulations!

Some bonus views on the way down

Saturday

Today before everyone else is up I go on an early morning hike to a random view point close to the hotel. This one isn’t as pretty as yesterday but still reasonably nice

Then I meet up with everyone else to go get breakfast. This breakfast place C.R.E.A.M is really good and I wind up getting 2 sets of pancakes and a crispy chicken burger. “The Plower” also really likes the fries at this place.

Originally me and “The Quant” were planning to go on a hike but given we had to wait an hour for the bus to take us to the start we opted not to and instead just relax which was nice in its own way.

We went to the same place we went for breakfast yesterday C.R.E.A.M which “The Plower” likes so much I almost decided to change her nickname to “The Creamer”.
