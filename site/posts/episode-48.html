Monday

The day started off pretty alright I suppose. After some interviews in the morning I tried running with Franklin the air purifier up to the top of Twin Peaks and boy was it painful. The best I could do was go up to Tank Hill before giving up, and even then I stopped for a 20 second break. The moral of the story here is I am out of shape cardio wise and have a long way to go of reaching my goal of running to the top of Twin Peaks with Franklin.

I had the first session in my new Volo Volleyball league 20 minutes after this run was supposed to end so I was booking it in order to make sure I made it back on time. Not only was I the first one but I was well and truly ahead of the pack. Sadly about 0.3 miles into the run I twisted my foot around Shotwell and 23rd street on some uneven terrain. Due to the adrenaline that was coursing through me I didn’t notice how bad it was at the time. Sure it hurt but it was nothing terrible and the run otherwise felt REALLY good. I PRd and got my fastest mile and 2 mile time finally going sub 8 on a mile. Once I finished and said adrenaline wore off the pain hit me HARD.

I could tell based on how I was limping already that I was in no shape to play Volleyball. However since it was only a few blocks away I figured I would at least go and introduce myself to everyone and watch from the sides. I got there and everyone was quite nice but boy was it painful. I couldn’t put any weight on my left foot which was searing out in pain. After about half an hour I couldn’t take it anymore so I went home and iced my foot the only way I could. Using a frozen veggie packet of Squash I had in the fridge.

Tuesday

The pain in my foot was even worse than at the end of yesterday. I woke up in the middle of the night and going to the bathroom felt like stabbing 1000 needles into my foot. Needless to say I would not be moving around a lot today. Perhaps today is the day of the Sloth.

I commemorated Sloth day by staying in bed all day and bedrotting. While I did some work and educational stuff at various points the vast majority of the day was unproductive bedrotting.

Come evening I finally have the energy to leave the house so I go on for a little walk just to get some fresh air and not be a total potato.

Sadly this is the first day in a long time that I walked less than 20K steps a day and it shows :(

Wednesday

Today I can actually start to walk around a bit more and leave my house like a normal person.

While walking around I see this in a window in a shop near my house

This caught my eye since “The Fisherman” has the one on the left so I thought she may like the one on the right to go with it and since it's her birthday this week it's the perfect opportunity for me to get it for her!

As luck would have it she invited me to go clamming with her today so the timing worked out nigh on perfectly.

For anyone like me before this who may not be familiar with clamming it basically involves going to a beach and looking for (small) holes with water coming out of them and digging to find clams. You use an instrument called a clam gun. Despite the cool sounding name you don’t actually shoot clams out of it and instead you use it to help you quickly extract large amounts of sand to get to the clams. I couldn’t stand on my left foot while using the clam gun meaning my pull out game was pretty weak so I focused primarily on getting it in there while “The Fisherman” took it out. Over about 1.5 hours of doing this we managed to get 5 living clams which wasn’t too bad all things considered.

One other thing I didn’t realize with clamming is you needed resilient clothes as some of the holes involve getting in some muddy places up close and personal. Since I don’t own any much less bring any with me I served in more of a supervisory role while “The Fisherman” handled the main extraction.

I’ve done a number of different fishing types with “The Fisherman” and while I enjoyed clamming over all I would say it's my least favorite. The complete list is as follows

Poke pole fishing (Based)

Crabbing by Ferry Building (What she calls crabbing on easy mode)

Sea Urchin hunting (A little of this, a little of that)

Clamming (I mean I guess)

Since I’m not that interested in catching fish what I mainly value is something which lets me hang out and talk with minimal actual work required. Clamming was the exact opposite of that as we were continually digging and refilling holes the whole time which wasn’t quite what I was looking for in a fishing trip. My heart went out for Stanley Yelnats here.

From there we went to Costco and got some hotdogs for dinner. I was debating going to the post MR hang but opted against it when learning some folks I wanted to see weren’t gonna be there. Although in retrospect I learned some folks I did were gonna be there so I should have gone but ah well.

After run club I had some more interviews and then I walked around for a while. While walking I ran into a woman who was putting up posters for some sort of MUNI protest. Apparently she is protesting the idea of being able to force fare evaders of the MUNI. I asked her a bit more about why she was doing this and one time while riding the MUNI the fare officer didn’t realize how her day pass worked based on timing and thought it expired subsequently chucking her off the MUNI. She fought and won later but that prompted her to push back completely against fare evaders being forced off.

Given how amenable she seemed I tried debating her on the matter and highlighting the differences between actual fare evasion and mistakes like what happened with her. It was actually quite an interesting debate and we talked a lot about public transit in general and how to make it work the best. Ultimately we both agreed that in an ideal world it could be free and this wouldn’t be an issue but I was a bit more skeptical that could happen than she was. She mentioned that Luxembourg has free public transit which I didn’t know about until today.

We were about to get into discussing Waymos which she was adamantly against and I wasn’t but she had to go. She did invite me to get coffee with her some other time to continue the discussion which I would be pretty down for as the discussion was entirely civil and I could tell how passionate she was about the issue and as someone who thinks a lot about public transit and different ways of getting around in the city I love to see that!

Later in the day me and “The Quant” went to a Costco dating event. Some or many of y’all may have seen instagram reels about this since I think it became a bit famous after the fact but for those of you who haven’t there was a dating event at a Costco where the group running it gives every participant a pin so people know they are with the event and let them loose in a Costco. The idea was supposed to be that you would go shopping in whatever aisle interested you and you would meet others in the same aisle and start talking. Realistically what wound up happening instead was most of us congregated in the area between the aisles and a bunch of people were all talking at once.

To be honest I was kind of hoping for more 1:1 conversation and while I got some of that most of it was big group conversations. It's not the worst for meeting people in general but harder to see if I should ask out folks. I did try to ask out someone by getting her insta which she deflected by saying she needed to go pick up her friend and then quickly got out of there. I found that particular deflection method kinda funny but I guess in her situation I don’t see how she could have more smoothly extricated herself from the situation.

Interestingly I found someone who reminded me of “The Writer” since she wrote both books and screenplays and has even published a book!

Ironically the only woman’s insta I got out of this event was someone I wasn’t interested in all at romantically but I just went on this whole bit about “The Quant” being China’s Mozart because he was playing the piano and I was basically just messing around which she found amusing. I guess there could be a lesson in that somewhere?

After the event I went to bar night with “The Philosopher,” “The Mosher,” “That’s nuts,” and “The bartender”. When I recapped the event to “The Philosopher” he said it went about as well as he would expect a dating event at a Costco to go which perhaps was true.

Friday

A while back I found out there is a service called HeyThereNeighbor in which you can sign up for 10 minute coffee chats with people in your neighborhood. Since I don’t actually have many friends in Haight Ashbury I thought it would be cool to try and meet some folks. Around lunch I met with a guy who wound up being really cool! He’s gonna be trying improv for the first time tomorrow so I gave him some tips. He’s also trying to meet more people so naturally I advertised trash pickup which he said he would come to next week. He’s another Canadian and my experience with those led me to predict where exactly he was from when he said “Toronto” which he was surprised by.

Later in the day I went to a chicken chase birthday party for “The Slosher”! For the uninitiated a birthday party like this consists of someone donning an animal onesie (usually a chicken or a fox), and going to a random bar from a list of bars and everyone else has to go and find them.

There were 3 birthdays being celebrated at this party so there was a Google Form put up where people could vote on who they wanted to be the chicken. There was “The Slosher,” a guy named Hunter (a former trash pickup leader!!), and a 3rd guy named Angello. Angello is a swell guy but spoiler alert he sadly won’t be relevant for the rest of this story. Initially in the poll “The Slosher” had a reasonable lead and it looked likely she would be the chicken. Personally I was gunning for Hunter because I wanted Hunter to become the hunted and thought the wordplay was too good to ignore.

An under reacted Partiful comment

I did my civic duty and casted my vote and was originally content to leave that alone until “The Slosher” updated the Partiful

So I can vote as many times as I like huh? Well I certainly can’t ignore that now can I? I immediately preceded to go hard and vote 70 times ensuring that Hunter would be chosen. Sadly I didn’t capture the exact breakdown before the party started but Hunter was now comfortably winning with around 60% of the vote.

When I got to the party I was pretty assured of my (err I mean Hunter’s) victory. I told “The Slosher” what I did and she said “That was YOU?!”. For some context the party started at 8 and voting was supposed to close at 9. One of her friends wanted to try closing the gap and making it closer to 50:50. I wasn’t gonna let that happen so I kept voting at the party and he did the same. Thankfully my votes per minute was pretty fast so he gave up once he noticed the gap wasn’t shrinking. At this point my (wait I mean Hunter’s) victory should have been assured. Sadly as the clock struck 9 and things were all set she cheated by changing the rules a bit and pushed the voting time back to 9:30. She also got a bunch of her friends to vote for her multiple times as well. While I probably had the highest votes per minute out of any individual person, the sheer quantity that she employed overwhelmed me and in the end “The Slosher” became the chicken.

In case anyone is interested here is the final breakdown. In total we got 1741 responses which wasn’t bad considering 40 people went to the party.

Once that was settled we all left to go track down “The Slosher”. Throughout this I got a sampling of some of the finest bars North Beach had to offer like Spec's which had a bunch of cool knicknacks and other things inside which was lovely, and there was also Hawaii west which had a bunch of cigarette smokers in there who assaulted my lungs all at once. So you know, you win some you lose some.

Eventually after some searching I tracked her down to a bar called The Showdown which was somewhere in between those two in terms of how cool it was but it worked out well considering our size.

I hung out for a while and chatted with all 3 of the birthday people. At one point I thought “The Slosher” could use some water and given how packed the bar was I was worried it would take a while so I tried going to a corner store and getting her some water but the bouncer wouldn’t let me bring it in (I did try albeit not so well to sneak it in).

Also “The Slosher” forgot her own nickname in the newsletter and thought it was “The Sloshballer” instead as if I would give her such an unelegant nickname. She was so sure however that she bet me a drink on it. Naturally she lost so now she owes me a drink at some point in the future.

Finally to round off the night I noticed “The Slosher” had her phone beside her on the Partiful unlocked so I decided to give her one last gift before heading out via a text blast on Partiful.

I told her exactly what I did after I think she was a bit too deep in her cups to remember ;).

The day begins with trash pickup as per usual. After pickup I went to a memorial picnic for “The Climber!” It was a joint effort organized by “Sergeant Sassy” and a few other folks. It was a lot of hanging out and chatting with some volleyball in the beginning. I really think “The Climber” would have loved to see everyone come together like this and have fun so I think it was a great tribute to him. What made it even better was we were joined by some random people who were playing Volleyball separately and wanted to play with us! “La Tacqueria Royalty” was also there and he was quite the good volleyball player!

There was also a run that happened since many people including myself met “The Climber” through Midnight runners. Sadly my foot prevented me from participating so I mainly talked with “La Tacqueria Royalty” and another guy about One Piece instead.

Originally I thought the picnic would end around sundown but we wound up talking in the dark for even longer. Needless to say it was quite a good time!

After the picnic me and “La Tacqueria Royalty” got dinner, talked more One Piece, and I mentioned that I saw one of his and “The Enabler’s” friends on Hinge which was pretty funny. She is cute so maybe I should send her a like and see what happens.

On my way home from the picnic I was having a phone conversation on the bus and some guy said I was disrespecting everyone else by doing so and started cussing me out and dropping the f bomb which was certainly surprising. Regardless of what you think of having a phone conversation on the bus I do think that was over the line. Looks like I became someones nemesis today.

Sunday

This is the first Sunday in a while I did trash pickup as somehow I was busy with something every Sunday for the past while now so it felt good to get back to the basics.

After trash pickup I hopped on a call with “The Professor,” “The K-pop dancer,” “The Taco,” and “The Quant”. Since “The Professor” moved to Vienna we’re doing once a month calls now to keep in touch and it was cool learning about his life in Vienna and some of the new things he’s doing.

Once that was done I got Garden Creamery with “The notetaker,” and “The Raja of Rhythm”. “The Notetaker” asked me what my default emotion is and I said happy. But then he posed an interesting question back to me and said “Is happy your default or is happy the word you use to describe your default?” In general I think I’m happy by default. I might not be radiating joy each day but I find happiness in a lot of little things that happen to me all the time which generally makes it the default. Others said numb was their default and I don’t think thats true for me?

From there me and “The Raja of Rhythm” went over to “The Ravenkeeper’s” abode to play Blood on the Clock Tower. Finally this meant I got to see “The Ravenkeeper" in her natural element. It was fun playing the game as per usual! Given how the game goes we only got through 2 games after playing for about 5 hours but they were some good games.

I went home and started to write the newsletter but decided to do some interview prep and instead and elected to finish tomorrow.
