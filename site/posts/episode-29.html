Monday

We have a work deadline coming up for a big project we want to ship by end of week so things are pretty intense. I don’t really do much after work as I am at the office longer than I usually am this week. And while I’m at work I’m much busier too with less time spent randomly chatting with co-workers.

Tuesday

The deadline draws ever nearer and unfortunately there are some problems with one of the parts of the project that I’m personally working on. I can’t say too much about it in the newsletter but this is unfortunately something that you have to get as close to perfect the first time without much room for errors. Generally one of the things with software engineering as opposed to other forms of engineering like say mechanical is that there is a lot more room for bugs (within reason). People are a lot less likely to be angry about a buggy web page than a buggy Golden Gate Bridge. Sadly thats not the case here and that coupled with a very large (and from our perspective expanding), surface area leads to what may well be described as type 2 fun once its all over.

Wednesday

Not much to say here other than the day continues and we realize that multiple parts of the project won’t be able to land on time so with my part we take a step back and figure out as a team what the best way is to fix all the bugs and come up with a more comprehensive test plan. Sadly I have to skip midnight runners but with how I’m working on these bugs I’m definitely sprinting :D.

Thursday

Not too much to add here honestly. Just gotta keep werkin. I go to bar night with “The Lads” for a bit only to find a particularly urgent bug that has to be fixed before tomorrow so I have no choice but to leave earl and head home and keep working.

Friday

I am starting to feel better about the project but its clear I gotta work over the weekend. At some point I reach my cognitive capacity for the day and I step out to go get KBBQ with “The Quant”. Its as delicious as it always is and a very welcome break from the week.

Since I go to the KBBQ place I go to a lot the waiter and I start talking and towards the end of the meal he gives me a 5% coupon off my next meal

I may have been slammed at work this week but I got 5% off Kogi Gogi so I’ll chalk this up as coming out ahead :D.

Afterwards I go home and code for a couple more hours before crashing.

Saturday

My morning begins with trash pickup as per usual. Jk, it actually begins with me working, but then I go to trash pickup.

Today we have “The Ravenkeeper,” “The Politician,” and “The Grandma” along with some others. A pretty solid group. After trash pickup we stop by Tacolicious and I add another Yelp review to my list of Yelp reviews at places I didn’t eat at. You can read it here if interested.

After the restaurant I depart to what is undoubtedly the highlight of the week. The Amazing Race! “The Enabler” and a couple of his friends put on an Amazing Race esque event thorughout SF. The basic idea is that we have teams that we break into and we are given clues to go to different parts of the city and once there we have challenges to solve. Successfully completing the challenge will take us to our next clue. This goes on until the end. I’ll go through each phase of the race to give a sense of how it went down.

Team formation and the pre race challenge

When I get to the starting point of the race (“The Enabler”’s friends apartment in Hayes Valley), I meet my team for the race. Most people came up with teams beforehand but unfortunately the person I was originally going to do it with couldn’t make it so I spent the morning before frantically calling my friends to see if any of them were down. Unfortunately I couldn’t find anyone who was free for the entire afternoon as required. Additionally I don’t drink which poses a bit of a problem as the aforementioned challenges above come in drinking and non drinking variants and my not drinking reduces the options that teams have.

Thankfully I find some people at the last minute (some of “The Enabler”’s other friends) who also aren’t drinking and we all become a team! They’re both really cool so I lucked out with a great team. We also have the best team name!.

Once thats settled we begin the pre race event. The hosts explain to us the rules. The basic gist is that we have the clue -> challenge format listed above, and when going between clue to clue you can only walk or take public transit with the exception of a one time Uber use. To stagger the start of the race there is a pre race trivia contest and the folks that do the best at that are allowed to leave earlier.

The trivia is mostly Bay Area trivia ranging from pretty easy questions (“Where is Stanford located?”), to much harder questions (“What was the purpose of Alcatraz before it became a prison?”: The answer is military fort in case you are curious :D).

Our team finishes roughly 5th in the trivia. We fumble a bit at the beginning but pickup towards the end so we’re feeling ready to go at it!

Clue # 1.

Once we’re off we get the first clue and head off. For those reading at home I invite you to think about this clue a bit before I talk about how we approached it.

When I read the first part “cherry blossoms bloom”, I immediately thought JTown since its the most iconic place with those. For Korean food I thought Daeho since its the most well known Korean place there. We head there (while racing a bit to keep up with some other teams) and it turns out we’re right!

We’re rewarded with our efforts with the next challenge

Drinking Challenge: Find a place that lets you do sake bombs and do sake bombs. Take videos.

Non drinking challenge: Buy group size + 1 bags of roasted edamame (100g) from nijiya market, and a food item with a big enough container to hold the edamame. On checking out ask for number of pairs of chopsticks for group. Hold onto these items until you get to the location of the next clue. You can eat the food item at any point. Make sure you always keep the edamame and chopsticks.

Naturally we go with the Non drinking challenge and head to the market to buy our stuff. We almost mis read the instructions and got group size bags of roasted edamame as opposed to the +1. Good thing we remembered at the last min before buying. We buy some random mochi croissants because we think the container can hold the edamame but we don’t think too deeply about it. Look how happy I look in this picture. So cute!

Clue # 2

With this clue we immediately latch onto “SFs largest park” and make a beeline for GGP. At this point we use our one Uber figuring that thats probably the biggest distance we will have to traverse in the race. Whether thats the right decision is a question we will leave for later but for now we embark!

The question then becomes what attraction in GGP do we go to? We’re really not sure so we decide to start over at The Conservatory of Flowers and work our way westward. Our thinking is that there are a lot of attractions densely populated together in that area like Conservatory of Flowers, Academy of Sciences, DeYoung Museum, and Botanical gardens. We try all of them to no success. We’re really not sure what to do here and have a bunch of random ideas for different attractions to go. One of us suggests Shakespeare gardens thinking that that might be related to “notable”. At this point we ask the hosts for help and they give a hint “get on the right track”.

At this point I do a facepalm as I immediately realize we need to get to Kezar stadium! Astute readers may have noticed that the clue started with “On your mark. Get set. Go!”. Sadly we totally ignored that part and only realized once we knew where to go. From what we gathered a lot of teams struggled with this clue and they gave the hint likely because of that.

Once we get to Kezar stadium we find the hosts and we get the next challenge.

The non drinking challenge is to take the edamame that we got and use the chopsticks to pick it up and put it in the food container. If we spill any we have to pick it up and put it back. This is brutal. I am very bad with chopsticks and go at a snails pace.

Eventually after a long and tedious process with hand cramps galore we manage to get them all in. I am definitely the slowest here. Props to one of my team mates for streamlining the process and giving us tips for doing it faster!

The drinking folks on the other hand have to have someone do a beer mile while everyone else drinks the same number of shots. After this they are significantly inebriated to the point where they are allowed to go directly to the next clue while we have to do another challenge to nerf us a bit.

This happens to be disc golf. We have to find a hole and each of us has to get a disc in the hole from 10m away. There is a disc golf place north west of where we are so we go there as quickly as the 7 and 29 busses let us.

Once there we disc golf and it is a bit of a struggle. I am not very good at this and it takes me about 7 minutes to get it in. My other teammates aren’t great but they are better than me at least.

Eventually after much struggling I do get it in and we move onto the next clue.

Clue # 3

With a “night to (not remember)” it clearly has to be a bar of some sort. When we get this clue my first thought is originally an Irish bar near by as I think of pot of gold with over the rainbow. Someone else wisely suggests Castro instead and after a bit of debating between Irish bar in GGP, a wizard bar in the Marina (its a long story), and going to Last Rite in the Castro we decide on Last rite. With “over the rainbow” we’re pretty convinced its Castro but we don’t know which bar in particular. We look for $10 deals in Castro bars and we can’t find any thats a strong candidate. Our basic strategy is to to Castro and try to brute force by finding popular bars and sending pictures until we are allowed to move on.

Unfortunately our decision to use the Uber earlier comes back to bite us as we have to rely on the 5 + 22 public transit combination which is not in our favor when the 22 bus gets delayed and the 5 bus has a lot of stops. Eventually we make it to the Castro but we’re still not sold on which bar. We ask the hosts about Last Rite and they say no. We think about some others and decide to try Detour because there is some Amazing Race event called a Detour apparently.

The hosts give us our next challenge.

Drinking challenge: Find pride themed drinks with more than one color. Take a video of you drinking it.

Non drinking challenge: Go to a karaoke lounge and order a (non alcoholic) drink. Sing a song and incorporate the drink’s name into the song. The moment you replace a word or phrase with the name you have to keep replacing that word or phrase for the rest of the song. Take videos.

We decide to go to The Mint nearby. As we are walking there we wonder how other teams are doing. Apparently 3 teams already finished :O. At this point we are debating giving up and just getting food but we decide to press on. One other rule is that the top 6 teams get a prize and we think we are pretty close to last. But since The Mint is so close we figure we may as well finish.

When we get to The Mint we think about what song we want to do. We figure it would be fun to do “I want it that way” and replace “that way” with “orange juice”. Unfortunately since this is a public karaoke lounge as opposed to a karaoke place with private rooms there is a long line to put on a song we decide to just use whatever song is already playing. I forget what the song is to be honest as I don’t think about it in the moment. We just pick a word in the chorus (it was “love” I think) and we replace it with orange juice.

The challenge goes well and now we are into … the final clue.

The last clue

We speed walk back to the hosts apartment (about 14 mins from where we are). While there we aren’t sure what to expect. How many teams have finished? Whats our standing? We cast aside those questions and focus only on what we can do and get there as quickly as we can.

We tie with another team for getting back. When we get up to the apartment we find that the final challenge is … Jenga.

One detail I forgot to mention until now is that the hosts said the last challenge is one that is better done sober. Unlike before the drinking and non drinking challenge is exactly the same. Do jenga! We have to build a jenga tower up to 22 blocks high (it starts at 16). To increase the height you have to put 3 blocks on a given layer. That means we need to put 18 blocks total. And since there are 3 of us we all have to put 6 blocks on each.

At this point 5 teams have finished so to secure our spot in the top 6 we have to beat the team that just got back. Thankfully they’ve been drinking so we do have an advantage here. Like with everything else that required careful coordination I am not a good Jenga player but thankfully my teammates give me advice on which blocks I should pick.

Its tense as we try to build our tower. One other rule is that if everyone in the group takes a shot the height of the tower gets reduced by 1. This means the drinking group only needs to get to 21 and they win if they take a shot. We slowly but steadily build our tower. As we get to the last few blocks the tower begins to lean so we can’t make any mistakes. It really becomes nervewracking picking blocks and moving them as slowly as possible while aware the other team is catching up. I’d do a doodle here but I can’t draw a jenga tower to save my butt.

We keep cool heads and don’t move any blocks too fast. We’re on the 22nd layer right as the drinking team is on the 21st. If they finish their shots before we finish our last blocks we lose.

Thankfully we barely manage to finish before they finish the 21st. We anxiously call the refs over and we get our 6th place finish!!

Post race

This was a really fun event! Big thanks to “The Enabler” and friends for running. Afterward we have a debrief where I talk about the race with other teams and learn some interesting things.

Apparently for the disc golf there was a place right near Kezar stadium where we did the edamame but its pretty obscure and they meant for us to go to the course further away to nerf us a bit more since we didn’t do the beer mile.

Afterwards we all go out to some bars. Unfortunately one of the hosts is moving to NYC so this is also a farewell event for him. Bye Chris! It was great to have you here and hoping NYC is a lot of fun!

I don’t stick around for long as I have more work to do. I go home and work for a few more hours and then go to bed.

Sunday

The day begins with some early morning work followed by a brunch at Plow. I meetup with “The Professor” and “The Pickeballer”. I get my usual nothing here which means I can leave a Yelp review that you can find here.

After completing this review I earned a recognition :D.

After Plow I go to trash pickup. Today at trash pickup I met one of the more “interesting” people I ever came across there. I asked my usual ice breaker “whats something cool or interesting about you that people wouldn’t expect?” and she mentions that shes been vegan for 9 years. I’ve met a number of vegans (including some of you reading this newsletter) and my response to that is basically “ok cool.”. I later learn she doesn’t have a job and when asked what she likes to do since she doesn’t work she mentions she goes down to L.A and partakes in vegan protests. Ok, so at this point she isn’t vegan, she’s VEGAN, in that she makes it a large part of her personality which I can’t say for any of the other vegans I know.

Interestingly despite not having a job she mentions she likes buying luxury goods and was debating buying a Channel handbag but didnt want to because it was 12K but the max she was willing to pay was 7K. Make of that what you will.

After pickup we eat brunch. Well they all eat brunch and I don’t. She’s eating some pancakes and I ask if shes concerned about the dairy in them. She pauses for a moment, realizes that there is dairy and then a couple seconds later responds with “well I need to eat”. Considering the actually vegan options like the potatoes I truly don’t know what to say to this.

I also meet a guy that has a youtube channel and had a viral video with Pokemon memes so that was cool!!

After trash pickup I go home and work for a few more hours.

From there I go to “The Professor”’s place and hang out with him, “The Plower,” “The Pickeballer,” and “The Professor’s” mom. She is pretty cool and it was fun meeting her. She’s in town til tomorrow so she and “The Professor” and “The Pickleballer” (who is also “The Professor”’s sister) were hanging out. Not much to say here but it was a good time.

From there I go home, finish work for the weekend and write up the newsletter!.
