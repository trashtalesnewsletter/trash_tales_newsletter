Monday

Today is a pretty chill day for the most part. After the 35 mile walk “The Shadow Brigade” and I did last week I wanted to recover for a bit. I think my legs are definitely getting stronger because the last time I did a walk of this magnitude I would not have been able to do a run club the next day but towards the evening I felt good enough to go to Mission Run Club with “The Raja of Rhythm" which was great! Not that it matters much but I was the first to finish so I wasn’t dying the whole time :D.

Along the way I met a really cool guy who turned out to be one of the main people behind the no dancing sign that used to be in GGP. He talked a lot about his different art installations and some of the cool underground art parties that he went to which were quite interesting. After the run we went to a bar at Alamo Square Drafthouse, a movie theater in the Mission. I hadn’t been there til now and honestly it makes me want to. It was a very aesthetic movie theater where you can order food directly to your seats. As some of you know I’m not a big movie guy but honestly I kinda want to go for the experience. Maybe on a cloudy day I may just make it out.

Tuesday

The day begins with a bunch of interviews, most of which go well. After the interviews I go for a long walk from my apartment through Presidio to the Ferry Building. Put another way, I do most of the Roundabout Trail that was not finished last week.

After the run I go play Spikeball with “The Spiker” and some others in the Panhandle. Since daylight savings time is ending this will be the last spikeball tuesday we have until the daylight savings starts again. I hadn’t played much spikeball in a while and it definitely showed since I was not that good but it was still a lot of fun as always. After a few games of spike we went to “Aquaman’s” house and hung out for a while. I didn’t get to participate in this season for long but it was fun while it lasted!

Wednesday

Today I did a fair amount of personal project work. There was an aspect of deep learning I really wanted to understand which I only had superficial knowledge of so I spent a while staring at equations and playing around until I got it. I wouldn’t say I’m at 100% understanding now but I am better than I was before.

In the evening I went to Midnight Runners with “The Quant,” “La Taqueria Royalty,” and "Sergeant Sassy”. On top of that I got to meet a reader of this newsletter I hadn’t met until now being "Sergeant Sassy’s” man friend who I heard was a fan. They say you shouldn’t meet your heroes so hopefully he wasn’t too scarred by the experience.

I realized earlier today that I want to make air purifier runs a normal thing for me as they do help me to get stronger so naturally I brought my air purifier with me this time. Amusingly everyone thought it was part of a costume of some sort so when asked what my costume was I said I was a guy named Humza from a weekly autobiography series called “Trash Tales”. Technically I wasn’t wrong but they admired my dedication to the character which I guess was technically true. I feel like if Humza were here right now he would be ok with how I depicted him in the run.

This was definitely my easiest air purifier run yet as I was actually able to run the whole way and at a decent pace. Towards the end of the run “The Quant” declared that he wouldn’t lose to a guy carrying an air purifier so he sprinted hard to try to beat me. I raced him to the finish line and I ended up winning. He claimed afterwards that he let me win but I think we all know that that's cope.

After the run me, “La Taqueria Royalty,” "Sergeant Sassy,” her man friend, and another person went to Kayah nearby. I wrote a yelp review here

Thursday

In the morning I went to Almost Friday Run Club which was good. During the club they asked us an icebreaker question “Whats your hottest take?” The leader said that we shouldn’t over think it and I should go with whatever came to mind. The first thing that came to mind was that I’m not ethically opposed to eating dog meat and kinda want to try it to see what its like. Sadly fear kicked in and I went with a safer opinion that I find travel to be overrated in our cultural zeitgeist but honestly I wish I just went with my first answer as I think that would have been more interesting.

To be honest for most of the day afterwards I honestly just bedrotted. For whatever reason I didn’t really have motivation to do much else.

Thankfully in the evening I did a Horatio Alger and pulled myself up by my bootstraps to go get some cooking lessons from “The Fisherman”. She offered after reading my last newsletter where I mentioned not having indoor hobbies and offered to teach me some basic cooking recipes.

For this cooking lesson we went over how to take chicken and marinade it to make chicken tacos. I mainly focused on cutting up the chicken, cooking it on the pan, rubbing in the marinade, and finally heating up the tortillas. It's not a lot but I did learn things I didn’t know before so now I’m that much better at cooking. Look out world!

She was dog sitting a new dog via Rover so I got to play with a dog whose breed I’m not sure about but gave off more cat energy on account of not being as active as most dogs. Though like any small dog it did bark quite a bit which was funny. Additionally said dog was peeing all over the floor resulting in some creative solutions for absorbing said run off.

Once we finished eating our tacos and walking the dog I headed off to bar night. Not a lot of folks were there today so I mainly chatted with “The Mosher” for a while. He taught me a lot about Kerbal Space Program,a game about making and launching rockets. Apparently a lot of the fun comes in decompiling the source code and modifying the game to make it play the way you want. He told me a lot about the different mods he was working on like changing how the physics engine works and how certain objects render to cut down on weird rendering issues. He even got invited to a secret Kerbal Space modder discord so he is one of the elite modders.

Friday

Happy Halloween to all who celebrate and a happy fright to all who don’t! Before all the festivities I mainly did a bunch of admin work like reading over the contract I got for my startup acquisition which told me less than I would like about the exact specifics of the money and when I would get it a but a little bit more on how much I could expect. Tldr is I won’t be a fat cat but it's at least something.

For this Halloween I decided to dress up as a penguin since I already had a penguin onesie. And for extra fun I carried around my inflatable penguin Boris with me. When people asked why I had Boris I would give a variety of answers based on how much of my bullshit I thought they could handle. For the less initiated I said that Boris was meant to be just a kid penguin. For the more initiated I brought up that penguins have been known to commit necrophilia and that Boris is actually dead, further implications being left as an exercise to the listener.

I started my actual festivities off by going to a party hosted by a friend of “The Enabler” and “La Taqueria Royalty” both of whom were there. “La Tacqueria Royalty” dressed up as Ash Ketchum and in that spirit he asked people to give him challenges so he could earn gym badges. I gave him a challenge but I won’t say what it is now because it is something I assigned him to do on Sunday so look forward to that in a bit!

The party itself was great but sadly I had to leave earlier than I wanted because there was another halloween party happening for which 3 people were my +1s so I couldn’t exactly leave them hanging. I did delay leaving as much as I could so I could also see “The Dentist” with whom I overlapped for all of 2 minutes before making a non ceremonious departure.

The next party was hosted by a different friend I met via trash pickup. I invited “The Fisherman,” “The Quant,” and another guy I met through Mission Run Club. There were a lot of trash friends at this party like “The Writer,” “Granola Girlie,” and “The Yogi”. Things were definitely more packed here than at the previous party which came with the usual plusses and minuses that that would entail. “The Fisherman” killed it with her Toph costume, it was easily the most popular costume I saw that Halloween night.

The party we were at was explicitly meant to end at 9:30 as a place to pregame before your final destination so then I wandered around with “The Writer,” “The Quant,” “The Fisherman,” and some other folks we met at the party for a while. We first got some street hotdogs and I have to say I thought street hot dogs in The Mission would taste better than they actually did.

Mine and “The Fisherman’s” hotdog touching tips

We went through Dance-O-Ween for a while which was a lot of fun! In my opinion it had all the party vibe of a club or bar without the long lines. Seriously for any bar we saw around here there was an hour+ line to get in which made it very not worth it.

Along the way we ran into “Aquaman”, “The Spiker”, and “The Yogi”. After a while there I got a ride home from “The Quant” which came in clutch as public transit was very delayed and Ubers were $50. I went home and played a choose your own adventure Halloween game for a bit before going to bed.

Saturday

The day started off with trash pickup as per usual. We picked up a large amount of candy wrappers in the aftermath of last night. I chatted with “The Ravenkeeper” who contends that she could probably walk longer than “The Quant” and believed I should have put her above him on my walker list from last week. I think this is probably true but since I haven’t gone on a long walk with her I can’t personally put her on the list.

After trash pickup, I along with “The Quant” went to “Aquaman’s” birthday party in Panhandle. We played spikeball, kan jam, and tried to cut pieces of candy exactly in half. The best I could do was take a 17g snicker bar and cut it into pieces which were 8 and 9 grams respectively which was alright enough. We also had a challenge to estimate how many jolly ranchers were in a jar. There were 101 total and I guessed 107 which was the 3rd closest guess out of roughly 15 or so which was pretty good.

From there “The Quant” and I headed over to Green Apple books to hang out with “The Plower,” “The Taco,” and “The Pickleballer". I’m not generally a fan of books but I hadn’t seen “The Plower” in a while and she really wanted to go to a bookstore so here we are. At first I didn’t know how I was gonna take down a challenging beast like a bookstore which would chew me up and spit me out alive but it turns out that she was the only one interested in perusing so the rest of us all sat in the back with the cooking books and chatted. I tried to help some random person who came through find a book she was looking for which was the most interesting part of the book store.

Sadly “The Plower” needed to go home early so the rest of us got dinner and went to my place for boardgame night where we were joined by “The Raja of Rhythm” who got a new motorcycle, “Mirandom thoughts,” and a new friend I met a trash pickup for some good ol Secret Hitler which was fun.

Afterwards I did briefly try to come up with some other Halloween plans after being inspired by “The Fisherman” but after failing to come up with any I just crashed and went to bed.

Sunday

Today started off with a run with “La Taqueria Royalty” and “The Dentist”. Remember when I said on Friday I gave “La Taqueria Royalty” a challenge to earn a gym badge that would be happening today? The challenge was to do our 5K run while carrying Franklin the air purifier. Unfortunately when I showed up to his apartment with Franklin to start the run he turned down the challenge. This meant I wound up doing the run with Franklin instead which still worked out because if I want to meet my goal of doing a half marathon or running up to Twin Peaks with Franklin I need to be able to do a 5K like this.

All in all it wasn’t the easiest but certainly not the hardest either. I remember way back in the beginning of the year (January 1st to be precise), I did a run with “The Dentist” and “The Enabler” and got my butt kicked. For any One Piece fans, imagine Luffy vs Kizaru on Sabaody. Since then I’ve gotten a lot better at running and this run was more like Luffy vs Kizaru on Egghead Island.

This time I could keep pace with the both of them while carrying Franklin! The run itself was beautiful since the day was nice and we ran through The Presidio and got to see Mountain Lake which was delightful. What was less delightful was when I awkwardly took off my jacket and had it slipping around Franklin before we stopped and I tied it around my waist but I persisted through that.

After the run we walked around a bit before meeting up with “The Enabler” and some other friends for brunch. During the walk I gave “La Taqueria Royalty” another chance to earn the gym badge by carrying Franklin until we got to brunch. In a way this is like how Ash earned his Kanto gym badges by not doing the original task and instead doing something slightly different so perhaps it fits.

We got brunch at Rice and Shine which was great. I wrote a Yelp review here.

From there I went fishing with “The Fisherman” at Horseshoe Bay which was a lot more fun than I expected. Going into the fishing trip I wasn’t expecting to catch anything I just came for the vibes. Little did I know I would be able to catch a big af Eel and some crabs. Since we were actually taking the Eel back home to eat I killed the Eels with a rock since they can survive without water for 36 hours which is pretty crazy. Killing the Eel with said rock felt a bit like playing whack a mole as the little guy would continually slip around as I tried to cave its head in.

Me catching my first fish!

Once we finished fishing we went back to “The Fisherman’s” place to cook and eat said fish. I learned more about how to clean and gut fish which was certainly informative and by the end we had a meal that tasted like a fishier version of chicken which was interesting. This was the first time I ever caught and cooked my own meal which was honestly pretty cool. I felt kinda like a real hunter back in the day except with less day to day dread and more existential dread instead. Cool!

While we were preparing the fish, the rover dog she was watching hung out with us. In order to distract the dog while she was finishing up the cooking I talked a bunch with the dog as if she was a person. I dove deep into her backstory about her original owner who according to my improv was so fat he needed 3 seats on airplane, got a divorce because of his 30 sidechicks, and a son who was so stupid he tried deep frying and eating weights in order to become stronger, and the dog herself was a fan of Andrew Tate. It was actually a pretty fun form of improv, all things considered.

What said fish turned into!

After that I went home and wrote the newsletter.
