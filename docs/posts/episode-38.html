Humza reviews foreign public transit

Hey there all you numtots! Its time for the foreign public transit review. In todays episode we go over Barcelona. Since this is our first episode we’ll go over the criteria used. 1. Frequency of trains 2. Coverage of transit 3. Ease of paying 4. Reliability of transit

Frequency of trains

I’d say Barcelona gets an A- here. I don’t find myself needing to wait any more than 7 minutes for a train. Definitely better than SF but worse than London or Japan.

Coverage of transit

This gets an A from me. I was able to use it to go all over Barcelona (for the times I didn’t decide to walk places to boost my step count) and it was quite easy. I never needed to walk more than 5 minutes or so to get a station and I could take the transit all over.

Ease of Paying

This one gets an A+ from me. I can pay using my credit card at any point or I can buy a clipper card and keep using that. I’m glad BART is finally letting you pay by credit card. Take notes MUNI.

Reliability of Transit

This one gets an A. Its not Japan levels where things come exactly on the dot but generally the difference between when public transit comes and when it says it will come is at most 40 seconds.

One thing I liked about a lot of the stations in Barcelona was that they had a minute and second counter of when the next train would get there.

I found this to be a small but nice quality of life improvement. The trains also came much more frequently as to be expected from Europe.

The food itself was a solid thumb sideways. I noticed the prawns tasted fresh in the sense that I could taste the “pier” in them which I don’t think I quite liked.
