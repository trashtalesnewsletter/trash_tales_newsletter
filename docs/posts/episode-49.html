Friday

The main activity today is of course the aforementioned onsite. The onsite was structured into 4 technical rounds and lunch. The exact schedule was

Coding (1.5 hours)

Lunch

System Design (1 hour)

New Framework (1 hour)

System Design (1 hour)

I won’t really go into too much detail on each specific interview except to say that the coding interview actually was kinda a combination of system design plus coding. For those unfamiliar with a typical system design interview a lot of the interview involves getting a vague set of requirements and being expected to ask a bunch of clarifying questions to understand specifics and tradeoffs you can make depending on the set of assumptions. You can’t really blindly go and start building something because what may work for one set of requirements may not work for another and part of the interview is being able to understand different tradeoffs. Once you do that you propose a solution and then the interviewer will say something like “Ok this solution works for X but lets say I now want to introduce Y new complexity. How will you adapt your solution?”

I’d probably say the coding interview was about half clarifying the requirements for the problem and half coding it. The two system design interviews were pretty much exactly what I described above and I think they went alright. The last interview was a new framework interview in which I had to solve a problem I never really did before with the idea that the interviewer wanted to see my thought process as I looked stuff up, ingested information, and worked with frameworks.

I think all the interviews went alright but in each there were definitely things I could have done better. Of course that doesn’t mean I failed but as someone who tends to be pretty cautious in assessing interview performance I’m not 100% I passed either but we shall see. During my last System Design interview I could definitely feel my throat get sore from all the talking I was doing. During that interview I think I drank water at least 7 times 😅.
