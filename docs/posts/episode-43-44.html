Tuesday

Today was a pretty chill day in Vienna. I wanted to mix things up and go on a hike in the nature surrounding the city. Granted Vienna is no SF but there is still at least a little bit to do. The best view I could find looked a little something like this

A solid 5.7 out of 10. If I didn’t come from the Bay Area I might have

rated it higher

All in all the hike wasn’t the greatest but it was a change of pace so I’m glad I did it.

When I got back to the main city I found they were shooting a movie in part of downtown Vienna

I can’t quite tell what the movie is supposed to be about from what I see here. This looks to be a love story of sorts. My head cannon is that the woman is the scion of Vienna’s biggest hotdog industry and the man is a washed up smash bros champion who now works at Vienna’s retro gaming museum and against all odds they find a way to make it work. After some more wandering that results in no noteworthy content I meet back up with “The Professor” and we find a cafe that actually has some good mango matcha latte and we hole up for a while and play Gomoku. Wednesday

Since this is my last full day in Vienna I decide to do some of the more random stuff I thought about but never actually did. I went to Danube tower which has Europe’s tallest slide. I meant to take a video of myself riding down it only to realize I forgot to start the video so instead heres a doodle

Also here are some views from the top of this tower

Much like yesterday these views reinforce my opinion that Vienna is a city best viewed at ground level where you can look up at the imperial towers rather looking down on generic skyline with a couple interesting things if you squint hard enough.

I got a slightly better experience when I went to a tower in St Stephen’s cathedral in the center of Vienna. Since this tower wasn’t as high up it wasn’t as disappointing

Amusingly the elevator ride to the top of the tower was playing Sabrina Carpenter which definitely wasn’t the vibe I was expecting for a centuries old cathedral. I’d have gone with more Gregorian chants myself but hey if that's what the people of Vienna want than who am I to judge.

What I will judge on the other hand was the labubu store that I saw. It's bad enough that I see these things in normal stores but to have a whole store for them makes me wonder if the arguments that civilization as we know it has morally rotted may have more merit than I initially anticipated. I won’t be showing any pictures here to save both your and my eyes from looking at them but it was a whole store of the little shits.

At the request of “The Professor” I’ll highlight a bit he had going on for the last 3 days. Whenever we would meet up after he finished work he’d always send me a message to indicate that he was on the train headed my way. These would include things like

Things are in motion that you couldn't possibly comprehend

Events have been set in motion that cannot be undone

The cards have been dealt, and the wheels of fate approach an inevitable end

He was quite proud of this bit and at his request I included it here so you may all bask in his brilliance.

We go to the same cafe as yesterday so I can performative male it up and get a strawberry matcha latte and after that its time to turn in and say goodbye to Vienna!

Thursday

I head to the airport early in the morning to catch my flight to Dubrovnik where I’ll be meeting up with my family. Funnily enough the Vienna airport actually had Switch games you could play at the gates to pass time :D.

Not gonna lie this was possibly the most fun I’ve had at an airport waiting for a flight! Once we get going and start getting close to Dubrovnik I see scenery like this

I’m excited for a nice change of pace from imperial city life! Curiously after descending for a while I notice we start ascending again. A minute later I hear an announcement in German which gets a bunch of people in the crowd laughing and applauding. The same announcement is said in English a moment later. “We will be going back to Vienna and landing in about an hour”. Wait what?! We’re going back?! Why?! The pilot doesn’t really give an answer for a bit so I come up with a list of guesses as to why

A weather issue

A medical emergency

An issue at the airport

Eventually after looking at the forecast in Dubrovnik I find evidence of severe wind.

Eventually the airline informs us that wind was indeed the issue. We land back in Vienna and its a mad dash as everyone goes to the airline counter to figure out what the airline is gonna do to help them out. When I finally get my turn I’m told that they can put me on a flight to Warsaw tonight and then catch a flight from Warsaw tomorrow. Initially I’m leaning towards this option but decided against it. Thankfully “The Professor” lets me stay at his place again and I instead decide to take a direct flight via Ryan Air the next day.

Once I left the airport after traveling so far to get nowhere I met up with “The Professor” again and I got some really good orange chicken which was the perfect way to lift my spirits after the above.

In the spirit of his bit above I figured I’d play along and send a message of my own in the format like so “Fate has unlocked a new canon event the consequences of which will ripple throughout the multiverse”.

Incidentally my family who I’d be meeting up with was flying from London to Dubrovnik today as well. They hit the same issues I did only worse. While we flew directly back to Vienna they landed in a different city in Croatia and then after a bunch of people got off from there then they went back to London. Not only did they have a longer flight in both directions (2.5 hours for them vs 1 hour for me) they also waited a couple hours at the airport for the people leaving from there to do so before the plane went back to London. And of course they didn’t have a Professor to stay with so they had to work out hotels and stuff with the airport which sounded real fun from how they described it.

Friday

Alright its take two will our hero, protagonist, dude bro make it to Dubrovnik this time? We start off on the wrong foot (wing?) knowing that the flight will be delayed by 20 minutes. I do get some good news though that my family’s flight which left before mine did land in Dubrovnik so there is hope. Eventually my flight does take off and much to my surprise we do indeed land in Dubrovnik!

Once I get settled I go out and start exploring the city and I have to admit it is actually cool. Maybe the wait was worth it?

One fun fact about Dubrovnik is that its where Westeros was set in the Game of Thrones TV show which the town really leaned into with some of its stores.

Admittedly the town itself while cool does feel a bit touristy. Its kinda like Venice in that the majority of the people here are tourists rather than locals which aren’t generally my favorite places to go to but its still got some cool elements.

Once I got into the city I caught up with my family and we hung out for the rest of the day and got some dinner and sorbet.

Saturday

I started off the day with some solo travel. Unfortunately I tend to wake up about 4-5 hours before the rest of my family so my family trips often have at least 15% solo travel at the beginning of each day. Today I got away from the main city and went on some hikes. The nature here is much better than in Vienna with some actually nice views

Unlike the places I went to yesterday I saw a bunch of people with their pets which made it feel more local which I appreciated. I walked around the area and at one point went to an ice cream place which was cash only. When I told him I had no cash he curtly said “Goodbye”. I found his matter of fact attitude pretty funny and after I left I wished I said something back like “Goodbye our meeting was as brief as it was memorable” to see how he would have reacted.

Afterwards I went to a mountain nearby where you can overlook the entire area around Dubrovnik

These views were great even by my standards! The hike itself was moderate, certainly not challenging especially for any of y’all seasoned Bay Area hikers or walkers but it was a great change of pace and it was the first sort of challenging walk on this trip.

By this point my family had awoken so I went and caught up with them. We explored the city some more.

I think there was a wedding here

Today there were at least 4 different weddings that we had seen either on a boat or just around town. There were roving processions of people going around taking pictures and what not which was cool. Some of these weddings had a bunch of people dancing outside the church afterwards as a post wedding celebration and I figured it was only right I join in and dance along with them for the vibes. I also joined a tour of what looked like German old ladies at some point and looked like I was participating in it which my mom found pretty funny.

A side story while I’m away

While I was away I thought it would be a fun bit to host brunch and a boardgame day at my place without me being there. I tasked “The Taco” and “The Quant” with being my proxies. Ideally if anyone asked why I wasn’t at brunch they would keep saying I’m in the restroom for a while and we’d see how long it takes people to realize I’m not actually here. Sadly people caught on quick from what they told me. Also they all drove over to my place and for once couldn’t find parking and left. Sadly some bits just weren’t meant to be but thanks y’all for trying. It would have been great tho!

Sunday

I kicked things off by doing a wall tour of Dubrovnik and seeing the city from its massive walls.

These walls were so nice it made me wonder if Mexico paid for them.

Unfortunately it started raining as I was finishing up the walls which is extra unfortunate in an area with slippery cobblestone streets as it makes slipping quite easy. I had a couple close calls but I made it through without slipping. I caught up with my family and we walked around for a bit but in the spirit of Dubrovnik’s Game of Thrones lore you could say that Winter had come and a bit of a storm picked up and we all retreated to the AirBnB my family was staying at and hung out there for a while. We all kinda just chilled and didn’t really go out much after that. I played some Gomoku with my older sister which to my shagrin she picked up much faster than me. I was hoping based on my bouts with “The Professor” I could stay ahead of her for at least a day but she got good after like 5 games. Thankfully at this point I had seen pretty much everything I wanted to in Dubrovnik so if there was ever a time to be rained on this is a not terrible time for it. The one thing I wish I could have done was take a bus since I never got to see any of Dubrovnik’s public transit. The one time I didn’t walk I Ubered to meet up with my family which while probably efficient felt like one of the less Humza choices I could have made. Ah well.

Monday

Today we leave Dubrovnik and head to Istanbul. Unfortunately as we are taking a taxi to the airport it turns out my sister may have caught a bit of food poisoning which is really unfortunate especially on a travel day :(. To make things worse we learn before getting to the airport that the flight we were going to take got delayed by 1.5 hours. We don’t know why yet but when we get to the gate I learn its because the plane had a hard time landing because of … wind. Here we go again, right? Thankfully this gets resolved after only another half hour so it seems leaving Dubrovnik is not as bad as entering but its still a tad annoying.

After landing and getting to Istanbul most of my family except me is pretty tired and stays in the hotel. I walk around for a while and check out a number of areas including The Grand Bazaar, downtown, and others.

Hey “The Fisherman” I see your people here

Perhaps most surprising of all I see a store that has the world record for largest unisex luxury perfume company which I didn’t think was a thing but here we are.

I immediately rectify my mistakes in Dubrovnik and buy Istanbul’s version of a clipper card. Unlike in Prague and Vienna here you buy a physical card and tap on to get through turnstiles kinda like you would the BART. In Istanbul a lot of the public transit is bus and light rail which is pretty good overall except for if there is heavy traffic which there can be. At that point I think walking may actually be faster than bussing.

Thankfully towards the end of the day my sister is starting to feel better from her food poisoning so hopefully things should be back on track tomorrow.

Tuesday

Today starts off interestingly enough when I wake up at 1AM and feel a strange lurch in my stomach. Initially I try to go back to bed but this feeling continues. I worry that I myself need to throw up which is what my sister did yesterday and unfortunately I realize I may be right. I make a beeline for the bathroom where I indeed do so. Oh fiddlesticks. Looks like what my sister had may not have been food poisoning and it may be a bug instead.

I called my mom where I learned that my dad also threw up just 10 minutes before me. What timing, right? This confirms a bug and after some searching I suspect norovirus. Based on the fact my sister recovered so quickly I’m inclined to believe it. For those unaware norovirus is a bug symptomized by vomiting and diarrhea which typically goes away in 24-36 hours. In other words, it’s a day-long bug.

I go back to bed and wake up later. It seems my sister is almost back to normal while my Mom and Dad are both feeling pretty weak :(. At this point my energy levels are actually fine and other than not feeling that hungry I don’t have that many symptoms so I actually head out albeit I can’t walk as fast because I notice when I walk fast my stomach feels a bit queasy. I also can’t really eat all that much :(. I meet up with my sister and we go through the bazaar together and then I chill for a while in my room. I then walk some more and get dinner with my sister and my Dad who is feeling well enough to make it outside while my Mom is not :(. All in all it definitely could have been worse. I easily had the most mild case as other than the vomiting episode in the morning I was pretty much ok today and I still got 18K steps which by Humza on vacation standards is low but you know you win some you lose some.

Wednesday

Thankfully today my whole family is now back at it. Of course the morning is still solo time which I spend going over to the Asian side of Istanbul where I go up a tall but disappointing tower and getting some really good lamb and eggplant in a less touristy neighborhood.

At this point I catch up with my family and we walk around some more and take a ferry over to another neighborhood in Istanbul with some nice mosques. For those unaware Saltbae of the Saltbae meme is from Istanbul and has a chain of restaurants here that I saw

There is a “Saltbae burger” which costed $28. I wanted to try it for the bit but I didn’t wanna pay that much for it.

Thursday

Today my vacation comes to an end and I come back to the U.S. Things are generally pretty uneventful on my journey back here. The only somewhat interesting thign was the fact that Istanbul happens to be one of the most security intense airports in the world which I learned by the fact that to get to my gate I had to

Go through a screening where I take out my stuff and put it through a conveyor belt

Go through passport control

Repeat step 1

Have my passport and boarding pass checked and stamped 3 separate times right at the gate

In case anyone was wondering why step 4 has 3 of the same checks I asked the people running the checks and they gave me a pretty vague answer. I looked it up online afterwards and apparently its because these checks are run by different agencies. Also apparently these checks are requested by the country you are flying into and because of terrorist activity in Turkey the U.S requests a lot of these checks. Thanks unc Sam!

After a 13.5 hour flight I made it back home! Originally I was planning on going to bar night and catching up with “The Philosopher” and the usual gang but I instead crashed :(.

I think this year I’ve travelled more than any other in recent memory having been gone for 4.5 weeks. Generally I only travel 2 weeks max a year. Some of you may know that I go on record as finding travel to generally be overrated. Don’t get me wrong I don’t dislike it but I think people tend to make it out as some amazing life changing experience and while I think it's fun I don’t strictly speaking think it's that much better than any other hobby. It doesn’t help that people list it generically as a hobby in a way that tells me almost nothing about them and kinda becomes a buzzword.

I will concede though that when I think back on the trip it did feel like a lot more happened in these 2 weeks than generally does. Usually when I think back over a 2 week span I’m able to mentally compress a lot of it as I generally do a lot of the same things whether it's the same old work, same old trash pickup and so on. It was also cool being in a different environment. With that being said though I did also definitely get fomo especially as the trip went on. I missed out on stuff I would have liked to be here for like trash pickups, poker night (with Leo coming back no less), “Granola Girlie” eating her six cans of beans and other things too. Granted I’m sure a lot of these things will still be here when I’m back but nonetheless as someone who greatly values community it did start to weigh on me after a bit and I was definitely excited to come back.

One other thing I find interesting as well is that after spending 3-4 days in a city I generally get bored and want to move on and start walking around somewhere else. SF on the other hand I still enjoy walking around even now. Maybe it's the fact that with SF I get a kind of joy I only get being somewhere for a long amount of time, but I also think its just because SF is a pretty great city and being away makes me appreciate some of the things I might otherwise take for granted here.

I go home and finally write up the newsletter. I really hope I don’t delay a newsletter this much again :P.
