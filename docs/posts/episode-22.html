Tuesday

Today is a pretty normal day. I go to work. I work. I go home and chill.

Wednesday

Today after work I finally go back to Midnight Runners. Its really nice having my knee recovered so I don’t feel like a fat slug anymore (I’m just a regular slug now). I’m joined on this run by “The Quant” and together we kick ass and take names. Amusingly I forgot my run clothes so I did the whole 5 miles in jeans which surprised my coworkers (little do they know I’ve done it in jeans + backpack with laptop in it). The run itself goes quite well. I really felt myself getting into my stride after the 2nd mile and I think I definitely have been approving.

During the run me and “The quant” are talking with a guy and “The quant” makes up a story about how I supposedly walk 25 miles every day. Since he isn’t usually the type to do so I’m caught off guard by this but play along. I’m really proud of the fact that now he is the one making up the bit and I follow along with it rather than the other way around. In my head I thought to myself “Thats my quant.” Also really proud of the fact that he has taken a lot more social initiative than he has in the past which is something he said he wants to work on. Love that self improvement!

After the run I talk with some folks over at the bar. Lately I’ve been borrowing an icebreaker question from “The philosopher” which is “Whats something unexpected about you people wouldn’t guess?”. I quite like the question as it catches people off guard and can lead to some pretty interesting stories. One of the people I talk with says that one night when was living in Manhattan she decided to become an Uber driver out of boredom. She went out from 8PM to 3AM and did Uber eats on a bike. She said she wouldn’t recommend it and she wound up sloshing some food. But it made for a fun story! Another guy said thats not the sort of question he would answer sober and left it at that. From there I go home and chill.

When I get home I try something I learned about on Twitter. Apparently the o3 model from OpenAI is very good at predicting vibes based on photos and I wanted it to see what my vibe was.

The tldr was that it told me I’m excited but awkward. To be honest this isn’t surprising to me as I realize I’m pretty bad at taking photos and I can never quite smile right. I wanna try to be better at taking photos (ideally starting with not having my eyebrows raised and my tongue out).

Thursday

Today I go to work and then go to bar night with some of my friends. Mainly its “The philosopher” and some of his friends. While at the bar we strike up conversation with an interesting guy who is apparently an indoor ski instructor around here.

To be honest I’m not a big fan of winter sports and the idea doesn’t really appeal to me but it was interesting to find an indoor ski instructor around here.

We then chit chat for a while about nothing of particular interest until I get tired and tap out around 9:30 and go home and sleep.

Before I sleep I come across an interesting essay on receiving compliments. Like many people I can sometimes squirm and feel uncomfortable receiving compliments depending on the complement. The essay talks about this and goes into a bit more detail about why it can be. A lot of times people care more about compliments in things they have invested in and not so much about things that they don’t. I really like these paragraphs in particular

Is someone complimenting something you don't care really meaningless? Could it not still be a moment of warmth?Personally, just because I don’t value a certain trait in myself doesn’t mean no one else should. It doesn’t mean their appreciation is fake.

Letting something land, especially something nice, means dropping your guard. It’s like stepping into a light and letting yourself be seen. You have to stop rationalizing, stop minimizing, and just be there with it. I wonder: if your perception of self is built on staying in motion, on self-improvement or humility or keeping your edge, even a second of that can feel... like you might let them down? Or giving something up. Or that you don’t deserve it. But it's a false trade-off. Receiving doesn't make you arrogant. It makes you reachable.

One of the big points here being that receiving compliments can counterintuitively be a sign of vulnerability. There have definitely been times where I’ve received compliments in a rather ungraceful manner. Part of why I write this newsletter is to help myself be more vulnerable and I think another good step would be being better at receiving compliments in areas I may not be as invested in myself.

Saturday

Today I leave early to go run an AAPI heritage month 5K over at the Marina that “The dentist” invited me to. I go there around 8 only to find nobody there. Unfortunately after checking with “The dentist” I learn that I went to the wrong place as there are multiple AAPI 5Ks and the one I’m actually supposed to go to starts at 10AM over in Japantown.

This puts me in a bit of a bind as I was supposed to lead volunteer trash pickup around 10. My initial plan was to run the 5K that I thought would be at 8:30 and then head over to the Mission to pickup at Trash. For a little while I debate what I want to do as I really wanted to do this 5K but at the same time I don’t want to let trash pickup down. This is extra tricky since the regular leads are out of town so I have to scramble to find a replacement. Eventually I reach out to “The writer” who comes in clutch and agrees to lead trash pickup despite never doing it before. Major shoutout “The writer”, you’re a real one!

With trash pickup leadership secure I walk over from The Marina to Japantown to start this 5k.

When I get there I meet up with “The dentist” and two of her friends who I’ll call “The other yelp elite” and “The girl who is moving to London in a week” (yeah I’ll admit these are among the worse nicknames I’ve come up with but I don’t expect them to read this or be recurring characters).

The first hurdle of the event comes before any of the running in that there are a bunch of boring AAPI heritage speeches I have to listen to. I don’t like these during trash pickup and I don’t like them here either. Thankfully in due time we clear the first hurdle and the actual race starts.

This is the route we wind up running

The group I’m running with is definitely faster than the typical Midnight runners pace. I’m not totally out of my depth but I definitely have to put in the work to keep up with the others. I’m at a bit of a disadvantage here as I have my backpack with me and a couple times during the race my backpack unzips open and some of the content comes flying out. Thankfully I don’t have anything particularly valuable but I did have to stop and pick up the tape that came out at one point.

One recurring theme is that people here clearly don’t care much about traffic lights as we run a surprisingly large number of red lights. I guess AAPI heritage stops for nothing. At a couple points “The dentist” tries to be a voice of reason and calls out that we’re running these but eventually I guess we all just decide to go with the crowd here.

Most of the race is flat or downhill. The only hill we knew would be coming was the one when we go up Grant to get to Chinatown. That one wasn’t too bad and afterwards we think we’re in the clear and should be smooth sailing. Turns out before the event ends we have a final boss we need to clear and that is these two blocks here

These were two blocks of very brutal uphill.

Before this point I was able to run everything even if I did have to exert myself to do so but this really wrecked me. I was able to run up 95% of it or so but I did need to stop once or twice to catch my breathe. Only “The dentist” was able to run up all of it without stopping. “The other yelp elite” and “The girl who is moving to London in a week” decide to walk up the hill. And keep in mind they’re both really athletic soccer players so that should say how bad the hill is

After clearing the hill the heat definitely starts getting to me, but thankfully the last bit isn’t too bad. We clear the final stretch and finish over at Plymouth Park!

We run at roughly a 9min/mile pace so now I think I can upgrade myself from a slug to a 3-toed sloth. To celebrate heres some concept art reflecting this important evolution in me.

After the run we get some Chinese food from a place near by and hang out over in Washington square park. Interestingly this place had us wait for 40 min which was longer than it took for us to actually run this 5k. While at the park I get to to know “The other yelp elite” and “The girl moving to London next week” and they’re both pretty cool. I ask them the same question I asked on Wednesday “Whats something unexpected about you people wouldn’t guess?”. I learn from this that they were both biotech majors in college which you wouldn’t guess from the fact that they work in finance / consulting.

From there I head over to GGP and hang out with “The doctor” and “The doctor’s sister”. We come up with a card game that combines Coup with Exploding kittens which works as follows

You start with two coup cards just like in regular coup

On your turn you can draw a card from the pile of cards or spend a card to do an action (adding Exploding kittens mechanics to Coup)

You can do as many actions as you want (like in Exploding kittens) but your turn ends when you draw a card or take money from the treasury

You can lie about what actions you are performing but can be called out with same penalties as Coup

Winner is last one standing

The idea is interesting but the game ultimately winds up being pretty imbalance as the optimal strategy is to horde cards. It was a noble attempt and I think we’re on to something but the game isn't quite there yet.

After hanging out in GGP for a while we go to play ping pong. “The doctor” and “The doctor’s sister” are both really good at ping pong as it turns out and I”m starting to get a feel for how it works. I’m pretty ok at hand eye coordination and backhand but my front hand and top spin still need quite a bit of work.

From there we go to Sanppo restaurant in SF and have some food which is kinda meh. Thankfully since I didn’t eat here I can leave a Yelp review to help me reach my goal of becoming Yelp elite while not eating at the places I review which can be found here for any interested reader

After that I walk home and chill before going to bed

Sunday

The day starts with trash pickup. Its a smaller than normal crowd today with “The yogi”, “The frog” and “The raven keeper” joining me. Turns out today for trash pickup the mayor himself Daniel Lurie comes to pickup trash with us which doesn’t really change things except we get more boring speeches before picking up trash than normal, my favorite.

After trash pickup “The quant” swings by to join for the post trash pickup hang. While there we decide to ask Daniel Lurie some questions. I start off by asking him how he would feel about changing the expression “wearing the pants” to “wearing the Levi’s”. In case you don’t know Daniel Lurie comes from the Levi family and a lot of his fortune (which he used to fund his campaign race), comes from there. He was genuinely startled by the question at first and then cobbles together a “sounds good to me” while laughing. I thought it was pretty funny. I kinda wished I led this trash pickup so I can make the joke that between the Levi family stuff and being the mayor Daniel Lurie truly wears the pants in the city.

At the request of “The yogi” I also ask what his agenda is for changing SF. He gives a very political answer in which he says the same thing in 5 different ways none of which are particularly helpful. I don’t mind since I got to ask my original question but I wasn’t impressed by this answer.

Afterwards me, “The quant”, and “The frog” go to Garden Creamery where we meet up with “The doctor”. Unfortunately it looks like they stopped serving Mujicha which is a darn shame. Interestingly the folks at Garden creamery now recognize me and immediately tell me such when I walk in which is funny.

From there we go to Dolores park and chill for a bit. Afterwards I go home and chill and then writeup the newsletter
