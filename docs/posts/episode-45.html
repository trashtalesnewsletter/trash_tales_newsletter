Monday

Today was a pretty chill day. I spent some time doing a bit of reading but didn’t really do much else. The weather was pretty bad so I didn’t have too much of a reason to leave my apartment.

Tuesday

Today I started work on a personal project of mine. One thing I realized while working is I don’t really get a lot of time to dive deep on certain things that can be quite useful but I don’t have the time to learn so I figured now is as good a time as any to do so so thats what I did. Mainly getting things started.

In the evening I went to a Blood on the Clocktower meetup. For anyone unfamiliar its a social deduction game somewhat like Mafia except A LOT more complicated. There are a whole bunch of special roles which make the game pretty interesting. A whole round takes about 2 hours to play so we only got through one game but I greatly enjoyed it. One of my friends got her nickname “The Ravenkeeper” through this game actually. If you’re reading this you should come to one with me sometime!

Wednesday

Today certainly proved to be quite the rollercoaster of a day.

I woke up to a message from my college ex who I hadn’t spoken to basically since we broke up asking if I was free to meetup today since she would be in SF. I’ll admit that message surprised me more than anything had in quite a long time. I won’t go into all the details here but we had a pretty bad breakup and she certainly wasn’t interested in talking. I did try to reach out once back when we were both in college and that meeting was incredibly awkward. I saw her on graduation day and then opted not to talk to her.

So what do I do now when faced with this message? I thought for a moment and decided I would meet with her. In my mind I expected this to be a half hour meeting at most. Worst case its slightly awkward but you know whatever.

Today I also learnt that one of my previous startups may be acquired. This was a startup I joined shortly out of college back when it was just getting started. As a result I got a reasonably large number of shares for a new grad. This is public knowledge but what I don’t actually know is exactly what my upside would be here. I did some math which if true would make it reasonable, enough that I could extend the Humza unemployment arc for quite a bit if I wanted to.

Between the two things above I was far too distracted to do any work on my personal project instead choosing to dive deep into hypothetical futures where the acquisition makes me bank and also what exactly this conversation with my ex would be like.

Eventually the time comes to go meet with my ex and honestly the conversation went much better than I thought it would! What I thought would initially be a 30 minute meeting wound up going for 2 hours and even then we only stopped because we both had plans afterwards we had to do. We spent quite a bit of time catching up talking about life after college, travel, and occasionally AI.

Amusingly she thought I was C suite at some AI startup and would either be too busy to meet or all I would want to talk about is AI. As it turns out she finished her PhD and is doing her own unemployment arc traveling around before she looks for jobs and happened to come here to visit people.

Since we hadn’t spoken to each other since college we talked a lot about what we had been up to since college. Since we’re both nearing the end of our 20s she asked me a pretty interesting question “What adjectives would you use to describe your 20s?”. I thought for a moment and couldn’t come up with any specific words.

After thinking a bit more though I did describe in great detail the overall arc of my 20s. One thing I realized is that most of you reading this have only known me for the last 2-2.5 years and thus don’t really know the whole arc so I figured I may as well share what I told her (slightly cleaned up) here because why not.

Humza’s 20s in an arc

After college I started working at a startup (the same one that may be acquired). At this point I didn’t have a very clear idea of what I wanted to do other than something in Machine Learning (the term AI existed but wasn’t thrown around as much as it was today) and I didn’t really forge out as much of my own identity. I didn’t really have too many hobbies to speak of. To be honest after work I mostly just chilled at that point. My work life balance right now was pretty bad with me working a lot of weekends. I didn’t like it but it was what it was.

I remember back in those days I would panic if anything seemed like it was going even slightly wrong. If QA ever filed a bug with my code or time estimates were off I’d freak out and frantically try to fix it with far more stress than I should have. Experience definitely taught me when and when not to freak out :D.

Come to think of it before the pandemic I barely had any friends (not just outside of work but in general). Boy is it weird for me to think that given my current situation.

Around a year and a half after graduating college we got into the pandemic. I remember I went home right around when it started for what I thought would be a couple days for my dad’s birthday only to be there for several months and then come back to my apartment in SF just to move out since paying rent in COVID seemed dumb. Interestingly I had more friends early on in the Pandemic since I reconnected with some people from college since everyone was online anyway we were all as far or as close as anyone else.

It was actually during the Pandemic that I started going on long walks. After a few months I’d come back to SF on the weekends and just go for a long walk. In those days I’d do a reverse bay to breakers and walk from Ocean Beach to the Ferry Building.

Also around that time I started doing stand up comedy via online classes at the same place “The Enabler” currently takes stand up classes.

This was really where my hobbies started to cultivate and I got a much clearer sense of what I liked to do and could feel myself carving out a more distinct idea of who I am.

Work wise some big projects I was on gave me my innate belief that as long as I work hard things generally work out. Not always the way I might expect and with some roadblocks but more so as a general point to not stress too much about work. It also taught me that I’m definitely more of a work to live person than the other way around.

A year later I’d get a new job and did some digital nomading. It was my first time solo traveling and I learned how to manage an evolving trip and how to start making friends in a completely new city. I went to Miami as part of it and started breaking into the local standup scene. I got to know some of the folks there who were mostly cool. There was also the guy my dad’s age who invited me to go on a cruise with him 1:1 but that's neither here nor there.

One year from there I moved to SF. Because of all the time I spent walking in GGP I really wanted to be in Haight Ashbury specifically because it was close to there which wound up being a pretty good decision (I’m writing this newsletter from GGP as we speak).

At this point I was doing some casual improv but I didn’t really have much of a community in the city. From 2023 to now I started to build that up first via a meetup where I met “The Professor,” “The Plower,” “The Quant,” “The Taco”, trash pickup where I met too many people to name here and finally run club.

I only realized after I said all that just how much detail I went into. To summarize I guess my 20s were essentially an arc of developing more confidence that things would work out and finding more of what I really want to get and cultivating community. Well now I have a much more succinct answer to the question that doesn’t involve going through my whole 20s but sometimes you only know the path after going through all the detours.

I won’t give the full description of what she said but the broad themes were largely similar in terms of understanding more of what she wanted to do and now having a bunch of fun new hobbies. Back in college I always thought of her as the work to live type always grinding on something to get ahead and now here she is talking more about her cooking club and bike backpacking trips than anything else.

We wound up spending a lot of time talking about travel which was interesting. It turns out she also travels about as spontaneously as I do (ie not booking return flights, quickly finding hostels) and so on. This conversation actually inspired me to travel a bit more and placed Central Asia near the top of my bucket list after remembering how much I like Uzbeki food. I still want to spend a bit more time here after my last trip but who knows what lies in the future of the Humza unemployment arc.

After 2 hours we did unfortunately have to end even though we could have kept talking for quite a while. It really is crazy just how much people can change. To be honest I didn’t think the two of us would ever see eye to eye again but I think we both became better people than who we were back in those days and now we can start over and the fact that such reconnection is possible made me really happy. It inspired me to reach out to an old friend from college who I certainly wasn’t on bad terms with the way I was with my ex but who I still hadn’t seen in quite a while and we’ll be reconnecting later this week which I’m quite looking forward to.

Once we wrapped up I met up with “The Fisherman” and another friend from that group to cook dinner. We made some eggplant and pork which I’m told was delicious and some pasta which I can confirm for myself was good. Given the personality of me and “The Fisherman” we made a bunch of eggplant jokes but I told myself I’d limit myself to one here so the one I’ll go with was normally her boyfriend would be the one to give her eggplant but since he wasn’t around I guess I had to do it.

Amongst the 3 of us I was definitely the least experienced chef so if “The Fisherman” was the head chef, the other friend would be the sous chef and I was the intern. I learned quite a few things from this including how to use a can opener which I never did until now.

I may not have skill but I have a *can do* attitude

Once we wrapped up cooking I headed to Midnight Runners to catch the last part of socializing. I caught up with “The Mamacita” and hung out for a bit before heading back.

I then went on a walk and then went to bar night with “The Philosopher,” “The Mosher” and “Thats nuts”. Friday

Today I did some more personal work and had another interview which was fine.

In the evening I went to a pitch a friend dating event where friends get up on stage and present a power point presentation describing why you should date their friend who is also on stage.

I made a presentation and was all set to have “The Quant” pitch me! Unfortunately I didn’t realize that the pitch tickets were separate from normal spectator tickets which already filled up. This was a bit of a setback since I was hoping the pitch would get me some attention. Sadly this meant I had to go out and talk to everyone myself which if you know me you can already see the problem here.

There were about 24 of these presentations that I watched. Most of them were reasonable enough. One girl went who I thought would be cool although apparently she said to not go out with her if you’re weird which I realized was a bit of a disqualifier for me. “The Quant” even mentioned that I wear weird like a badge of honor which is usually true :D.

As the presentations went on you could see people get more fatigued which is a good reminder to go early to stay in the zeitgeist.

After the presentations concluded I kept a note of the people I liked and tried to find them. One of the girls was always with some of her friends which is a situation I didn’t exactly know how to penetrate. I mean if I’m trying to ask out a girl I can’t exactly do that with her friends around. With her I settled on cold messaging her on insta since I found her on insta and we have some mutuals. This went about as well as you’d expect but I figure you miss 100% of the shots you don’t take.

I had to scout around for a while until I found one such girl who was alone and I talked with her for a while. Interestingly she grew up in Prague so my recent trip there gave me some stuff to say! Eventually I did get her number and I need to message her at some point. While we were talking some other guy awkwardly came up and tried to ask her out. He commented on the fact that she mentioned biking in her presentation and asked if she’d be down to go on a bike ride tomorrow. She then said her bike was broken and wasn’t sure when it would be fixed. Honestly my heart goes out a bit to that guy. Its tough.

Saturday

The day starts off with trash pickup as per usual. I hadn’t been back at the Saturday pickup in a while so it was great to see everyone again.

After pickup I get lunch with “John Wick,” “The Raja of Rhythm,” “The Yogi” and a friend of “The Yogi”. It was good to see them all again since I hadn’t seen them in quite a while. The friend of “The Yogi” is one of the few people I’ve met who I think is a troll in similar ways to me which was pretty cool to see. He spent a while doing a bit about how stuck up people from private schools are specifically because “The Yogi” was the only one amongst us who went to a private school but it was done in a pretty tasteful way :D. He also apparently has someone convinced for 8+ years that he doesn’t know what Disneyland is, which was just great.

From there I went to a brief pregame at “John Wick’s” house and then to a halloween party hosted by “The Writer”! One thing he did which made the party quite fun was he hid a bunch of toy skulls around the apartment with clues to where each one is. It was quite fun to solve these and the first 25 people to do so get a sticker. I had an advantage arriving early (read on time) and spent a bunch of time looking before anyone else got there and did it before anyone else.

Sadly I started getting tired around midnight and headed out around then and got a bit of food with “The Raja of Rhythm” before heading home

Sunday

The day begins extra early today as instead of trash pickup I help “The Filmmaker” with his movie! I’m an extra in one of the dance scenes he has. Since its a dance scene I don’t have any lines to learn, instead I mainly spend time learning choreo. Despite having the least to do I’m pretty sure with my lack of coordination it takes me the longest to learn but nevertheless it was a good experience!

For a bit of context we’re doing a scene in a MUNI where we dance across the length of a MUNI car. Given the challenges of doing this in a MUNI we first practice over in Dolores park until we have the moves down which takes a few hours and then we do it in the MUNI itself.

We get lucky as there are very few people in the MUNI when we do it which makes the actual filming easier. The moves we do require a bunch of space which would have been really awkward in a large car. As it was I’m pretty sure we got some confused looks from the people in the car with us while we were doing this which I find kind of funny.

We did this dance number to “The Subway” by Chapel Ronin. I never heard this song before and now I’ve got some pretty distinct memories dancing to it. In my opinion it's the kind of song that I can see why its popular even though I don’t really like it. It's too sad for me. For extra irony the place where we got lunch after filming was playing another song by the esteemed Chapel.

From there I caught up with “The Quant,” “The Taco,” “High Entropy,” and “The Lawyer” and watched most of them eat Poke and hung out in Dolores park.

Afterwards I went to “The Mosher”’s for movie night with “The Philosopher,” “That’s nuts,” and “The Juggler”. We watched Airplane. It had been many years since I saw it and I forgot just how absurd the humor was, which was great! If you haven’t seen the movie I highly recommend it! We also watched Naked gun which ostensibly had a plot but if so I couldn’t find it amidst all the shooting and sex scenes. To be honest I was getting pretty tired after the long day and wanted to leave in the middle of this movie but I stayed because another friend was supposed to eat a whole bunch of bandito eggs which I wanted to see.

It turns out the bandito eggs tasted horrible. I had a bite and was brought to tears with “The Mosher” concerned if I was ok. The friend had two bites before throwing the rest in the trash.

Once that was done I made my eggscape and went home. Sadly by this point it was 10pm and didn’t have time to write the newsletter so its getting delayed a bit.
