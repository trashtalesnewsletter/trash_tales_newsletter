Monday

I start off by going to Barry’s Bootcamp at 6:15AM in the morning. I got the inspiration to do this after seeing a gym rat girl at Marina Run Club on Saturday who I thought was pretty cute. I realized if I wanted to attract that kinda girl maybe I should go to the gym. Either way doing something painful like that is probably good for my character arc so regardless of outcome I figure this would be good.

Getting out of the house at 5:30AM felt like a miserable slog. The workout itself was a mixture of weights and cardio. The way these workout classes work is you either do weights -> cardio -> weights -> cardio or cardio -> weights -> cardio -> weights. Either way the class is basically half weights and half cardio.

The weights part you do a whole bunch of different lifts and such. I don’t know a good way to articulate this other than they yell out random things and you do them with the weights. Naturally you get to pick your weights so you’re not overwhelmed.

The cardio part was actually pretty fine for me considering my running experience. The way they do the cardio is they have you turn the intensity on the treadmill up and down and do X for however many seconds. The numbers on the treadmill correspond to MPH so the lowest people would typically do is 3 (casual walk) all the way to 12 (5 min/mile). They said the advanced people should do 10-12 which I was able to. Woo!

The weights were significantly harder but sadly I don’t remember enough to articulate it well. It was a whole bunch of random lifts followed by some core work.

I actually felt kinda good after that!

Unfortunately I learned later that when I went urchin foraging the week before I made a bit of a mistake with regards to my first impression training. When I was out on the rocks someone asked me if I liked sea food and I curtly said no and inadvertently ended the conversation. For a bit of context this was likely asked when I was trying to climb the rocks and I was super nervous trying not to slip and fall. I wasn’t really focused on the conversation so much as I was focused on not falling. Nevertheless I can totally understand how it comes off as rude if I just curtly answer the question and show no interest in engaging or following up.

Also while I admit I don’t like urchin I should try to couch my reaction to urchin a bit more when everyone there really liked it. Like I shouldn't be so open about the fact that it doesn't taste the greatest to me :(. I really did hope I would like it the second time I tried it but sadly it seems urchins and I just aren’t meant to mix.

After work I continue my 1:1 series with “The Menace". We go to Anatolian table which he thought was really romantic :D. This is good to know if I ever have a date at some point and want to take her somewhere. Perhaps this place could be the move.

We have a real good time catching up talking about career stuff, dating stuff and other random things. Speaking of romantic things, the view from Dolores Park looked great. If this was with a date I think it would have been the perfect time for a kiss or something. If I was feeling cheesy I’d say the view is almost as nice as she was but I get the feeling that wouldn’t go over well most of the time.

She really liked the way I framed the and joked that I should charge for these which is extra funny considering that I’m paying for em :D.

I told her about some of the big life updates such as my goal to find a parter and the first impression training and my goal of going to the gym more and she said as a muscle mommy in training she supports me. Thursday

From there I go to bar night with “The Philosopher,” “That’s nuts,” “The Finer things,” “The Juggler,” “The Juggler’s girlfriend,” and another guy. We went to a bar in Northbeach called Comstock Saloon. I was told this could be another chance to work on my first impression so I dialed in when I was there. Since I never had oysters before I had two different plans here. Plan A is I try them for the first time and wind up liking them. Plan B is I do a good job hiding my negative reaction. Either way it should be a win win so long as I get it right. By the time the oysters do come I am incredibly sleepy so I am holding on to dear life trying to stay awake. I try the oysters and … well let’s just say we put plan B into action here. I think I do a reasonably good job couching it. Since those reading this already know me I’ll tell you that in my personal opinion they tasted very briny. And it stuck around too. When I went home I had a few spicy Plantain chips to get the brine out of my mouth.

Today my stomach is feeling better and I can go about my day like normal again. I start with trash pickup with “The Notetaker,” “The Raja of Rhythm,” “The Yogi,” and others. After trash pickup I planned to meet up with “The Fisherman”, her Russian dissident friend, and a friend of hers that I met back in Hawaii almost a year ago and haven’t’ seen since.

In any case once we all met up we went to Camera Obscura over by Ocean Beach. It was pretty cool to see. For anyone unaware its basically a giant pinhole camera where light travels into a dark room and inverted on a lens so you can see the outside. Essentially it’s a giant camera showing what Ocean Beach looks like and you can get a live view in a dark room!

Afterwards we walked around the sand dune park, ran into a family crabbing which naturally piqued “The Fisherman”, and we went to an Irish bar to see the half time show. It was super cool because one guy made a bunch of food for the whole bar so we got to eat courtesy of this dude. You can tell this bar is a real community type vibe. The half time show was pretty good. I’m not a huge bad bunny fan but I like him and thought it was catchy. I’ll admit I thought the choice to fly the Puerto Rico flag was pretty bold.
