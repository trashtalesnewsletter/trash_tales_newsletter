Monday

In the morning before work I got a text from someone I hadn’t seen in two years asking if I was free to meetup for coffee. I was interested in seeing him so I set a plan to meet with him at Asha’s over lunch. Little did I know this would turn into one of the stranger experiences I would have.

We set a plan to meet around 12. I get there right on the dot and ask what his ETA will be. 8 minutes later he replies that he's going to be late. I ask him how late and his response is “You tell me.” I didn’t really know what to say to that. He follows up a minute later by saying “Dude I have no idea what's going on here.” Following up with “I’m very tired. And slow.” I think he’s going through something and I do hope he is alright if so.

Once we catch up I tell him a bit about how my life is going and he asks me to pick a number between 1 to 5. I pick a number and he writes it down in his notes app. I asked him what the number was for and he wouldn’t tell me. I ask him how law school is going and he says he doesn’t know if he should continue and asks me if he should or not. Remember that I haven’t seen him in two years and not since he started law school so it's not as if I have a whole lot of information to answer the question for him. I try to get a bit more information but the search proves … elusive.

He then shows me his phone background which looks to be a lake of some sort with a mountain and a couple of boats in it. He asks me where I think it is. I suspect that it's Lake Tahoe based solely on vibes. I try to offer my guess but he then waives it away. At a couple other points in our meeting I ask if I should tell him my guess and he says no.

Once we finish up at Asha’s we walk around a little bit before coming across an art gallery. He says he wants to go in so thats what we do. We get to the 4th floor of the building where they tell us everything on that floor is closed for the day but there should be stuff on the 5th floor. We take the stairs up and he mentions his legs hurting. I ask if we should go back and take the elevator and he says no. He then asks why we’re here before I reply “Uh … you said you wanted to come?” I truly am lost at this point and wonder what he wants to get out of this.

We make it to the 5th floor and see some of the art

I had what I thought was a cool interpretation for the 3rd one which I forgot. Maybe something about our inner dreams being manifested as shadows?

We then leave the art gallery and I vaguely mention I should head out soon to go back to the python mines and he says “thanks for meeting” gives me a hug and then walks off. It seems like he may have been going through something or on drugs perhaps. Either way I hope he is ok. I don’t really know what to make of all of this.

I go back and continue wrangling my workflows. Once that's done I go to the gym with “The Professor” and “The Quant”. I think I’m starting to get better with the dumbbell lifts. I still can’t lift a lot of weight but I don’t need to be reminded (as much) about my form. I remember to keep my shoulders back and tense up my abs. Unfortunately my pride does get the better of me as I try to keep lifting despite my form breaking down. I don’t think I overdid it too hard but I may have gone a couple reps past where they suggested I stop. Oopsie poopsie.

On the plus side I get a new PR in the plank of 2 minutes and 44 seconds. I’m pretty close to the 3 minute mark but I decided to listen to “The Professor” this time when he mentioned that my form is breaking down.

Tuesday

Today is mainly spewing my source code at the ol office space and then I head home and do nothing of particular interest.

Wednesday

Today after another pilgrimage into Python I go to run club with “La Tacqueira Royalty”. This is the first run where I can show off my new Midnight Runners shirt which is awesome. The bad news is that it is a bit cold out which means that my nip nips (also pronounced as nipples) in some cultures are quite cold. This improves somewhat as the run goes on but towards the end it certainly becomes an ordeal. Normally I’d stick around afterwards for a post run hang but instead decide to go to game night at “The Professor’s” place.

The main game we play there is Exploding Kittens. For most of you who haven’t played with me I used to quite hate Exploding Kittens and found it quite boring. What changed was that whenever I play I decided to play my own game within the game where I try to die first by aggressively giving away my diffuses and proactively seeking bombs. I was able to do this pretty successfully for a while until “The Professor” decided to troll me by beating me to the punch and trying to die before me. This has turned our group Exploding Kittens dynamic into a game within a game where the two of us try to beat each other to the punch in dying while everyone else tries to win.

I was all set up to play that way today until “The Professor” changed things up and gave me a deck with 4 diffuses and himself none. What then wound up happening was we played a “raid boss” variant of Exploding Kittens where everyone teamed up to keep me alive as long as possible to prolong my misery and everyone else trying to die. The win condition for them is that I’m in the final two players with the person who makes it to the end with me being the true winner.

Since this is a raid boss variant I figured the funniest way to depict this game would be by using a manga fight with all of us substituted in as the characters. For context it will be “The Plower,” “The Quant,” “The Kpop dancer,” “The Professor,” and “Mirandom thoughts” all vs me.

Since he rigged it so he has no diffuses he started things off by dying immediately and removing himself from the game. While this does mean he can’t win he definitely was the MVP in terms of defeating me with regards to setting up the plan, giving me a bunch of diffuses, and some posthumous contributions we shall get to later.

My next opponent proved to be “The Plower”. She was a no nonsense nemesis to say the least. Her use of nopes proved vital in restricting my actions. At one point I knew there was a bomb on the bottom of the deck so I tried to use a draw from the bottom. This succeeds and I get the bomb I want to get rid of one of my diffuses but she then places a nope card. Normally this wouldn’t be allowed but “The Professor” intervenes posthumously with “house rules” to say that it is and I’m not allowed to draw the bomb.

At this point “The Quant” also joins the fray and it quickly becomes a 2 v 1 showdown.

Eventually “The Plower” goes down. I should also mention at this point that “The Kpop dancer” and “The Sparkle” don’t really participate in the fight and just play the game normally so they won’t be included in any of these pictures. They both die sometime around now but to be honest I don’t remember their order as its not particularly relevant to the main raid boss fight.

Now its down to me vs “The Quant” and “Mirandom thoughts”. Throughout the fight I manage to burn off all my diffuses. At one point I use the ol trick of using a shuffle card and “happening” to shuffle a bomb right on top before I draw. Its pretty clear there are no rules at this point so you gotta do what you gotta do to win. Unfortunately “The Quant” and “Mirandom thoughts” collude with some more posthumous help from “The Professor”. They set up a gambit where “Mirandom thoughts” takes the diffuses from “The Quant” and then plays an attack card forcing “The Quant” to draw a bomb.

With that its now down “Mirandom thoughts” and myself and “Mirandom thoughts” wins.

Good game y’all. As blatantly rigged as it was it was certainly a lot more fun than the dreck that is normal Exploding Kittens.

Thursday

Friday

Today I went to my daily code communal as per usual. On my way home from work I run into someone from a charity called Nature Conservatory asking me to donate money. Most people when faced with someone like this will look away, walk by quickly, and awkwardly mumble a “no thanks” when asked to donate money. From a social perspective that is indeed the safest course of action. I on the other hand choose not to play it safe and instead have some fun. When they approach me I talk with them and see how long it takes before they back away from me. When he talks about all the nature and protecting the Marin Headlands I mention that I love going there to throw my trash. I tell him I’m a huge polluter and love nothing more than making the world a little bit worse. To my surprise he doesn’t back down and commits to the bit. He mentions he loves polluting and if I donate to them they will help me pollute even more. This goes on for quite a bit and I was pleasantly surprised how much he committed. At first I wanted to get him to back away but it got to the point where I was enjoying the banter itself. I didn’t want this to end. Heck I was almost gonna donate if for nothing else than my sheer impressment. However I also thought that if I donated I’d lose the battle of pride and out of respect to the principles of the engagement I can’t back down. We then switch to me pretending to donate and getting my demographic info wrong. Apparently he could see through the fact that my DOB was not in fact 1910 or that my zip code somehow was not 6 digits. He then mentioned that he’d love to keep this going but he has an incentive to get people to sign up so he can go home so he was gonna focus on some others. I guess I won but it doesn’t feel like a real victory. I was hoping to be pushed more to my limits. To truly feel like I couldn’t keep committing to the bit anymore. To feel truly locked in. He then says next week will be his last week at the job and he’ll be out here for one more week. I hope I find him again. I want to continue our battle of pride properly.

host what I originally intended to be a board game night but turned into me inheriting a bunch of “The Professor’s” furniture as he moves out followed by a board game night. I get an extra couch, a papasan chair, a trash can, a night stand, and a shoe rack. I’m told my place looks very different and much improved now. Big thanks to “The Quant,” “The Sparkle,” and “The Professor” for moving all this stuff over to my place. I can now comfortably seat somewhere around 10 people with no need for me to bring out the air purifier.

Afterwards I headed over Madrones for “The Politican’s” bar crawl. Originally I intended board game night to last around 2 hours or so and end around 9 so I can go to this bar crawl. Fortunately / unfortunately we ended up having too much fun and the event that was supposed to end at 9 ended at 11:45. By the time I get there I realize that Madrones will be both very loud making it harder to talk to people and I need to pay a $10 cover. I don’t think tis worth it for a place like that so I wind up heading home.

Saturday

Today begins with trash pickup. Through the pickup I feel a bit down mentally. Forgive me as this next part will be hard to articulate and I debated putting it in here but it is important. I felt a bit down because I worry that I don’t bring enough value to my friends. I sometimes think that I’m not enough and those thoughts for whatever reason got to me now. Why now? Sadly I cant’ articulate this even to myself. Sometimes it just feels like the thoughts randomly come in. Its kinda like if you leave your mental door open and then they come in to your mental house and start harassing me. Whats worse is they didn’t even take their mental shoes off, those bad thoughts! Like if I’m not funny enough, helpful enough what is the point of me? I wish I could say there was a real resolution here other than I somehow just acknowledged those thoughts and then sorta accepted them and they went away for a while. Not permanently I suppose. Again this is not a well articulated point I know but my rule for these newsletters is to share anything I deem “interesting” for either better or worse regardless of how it makes me look and so I did.

After that little episode I wrap up trash pickup and go to a food crawl for “The Professor,” “The Quant,” “The Pickleballer,” “The Taco,” “The Plower,” “The lawyer,” “High entropy,” “The sparkle,” “Mirandom thoughts” and other friends of “The Professor”. Since he is moving out a bunch of us do a food crawl where we go to all of his favorite food places. We go Bon Nene, Garden Creamery, Tartine, Five Star Truffles, Detour to play Killer Queen, and finally the Humza cave where we get some Dumpings from Dumpling House.

I won’t go through all the details here and instead link you all to yelp reviews I wrote for each place. Since I wrote these Yelp reviews I naturally ordered the nothing at all of them so I can keep my bit of writing Yelp reviews at places I don’t’ eat at to become Yelp Elite going.

Between Five Star Truffle and Dumpling House we go to Detour to play some Killer Queen which is quite fun. Big shoutout to “The Sparkle” for having a killer instinct and being quite good at aggressively attacking.

From there we go back to The Humza cave and play more games and hangout. Around this time “The Shadow Brigade” comes as I manage to catch her on one of her monthly visits to the Bay.

After a while we play game called Psycopath where everyone but me is aware of a “rule” that I have to guess. Everyone sits in a circle and the guesser (me) has to ask questions to one member at a time until I can figure out the rule. A past example of the rule is that when I ask a question to someone they answer as the person on their left and if they get the answer wrong (according to the person on the left) they say “psycopath” and they both switch places.

In this case the rule is that there is no rule which I basically manage to get. One advantage I have in a game like this compared to most is I’m very good at battles of attrition. Usually when the game is played the person guessing the rule wants to give up but in my case I can keep going long enough that I make the people who know the rule want to give up. Sadly “The Professor” saw this coming and at one point nerfed me so I can ask at most 10 questions. I still managed to get it but I really want to test my stamina here and keep this up for a while. Perhaps an hour. The first time I played this game and I was the one figuring out the rule I figured it out part way through and kept going for another 10 minutes until they gave up. That was a while back when the group didn’t quite know my limits and naively assumed I’d crack first. They have certainly wisened since.

Sunday

The day begins with trash pickup as per usual without any of the random “mental breakins” from yesterday. I catch up with “The Raja of Rhythm” after his journeys to parts unknown and also see “Always causing a Racuqet”. The post trash hang today is fairly short and I go to a party hosted by one of my coworkers. My coworker has a bunch of friends who are very techy, more so than I would generally like to the point I was originally not going to go but decide I may as well.

On my way to the party I meet some interesting people on the bus who like my custom “Mooni” hoodie. We talk for a bit and they encourage me to set up an online store and if I did that they’d buy them. I love meeting random people this way and I’m glad I got an interesting conversation because of my hoodie. One of the people I met had an art piece displayed in the world’s smallest children's art gallery (yes this apparently a thing).

Afterwards I get off the bus and go the party. I was correct in my earlier assessment that the vibes of the party certainly weren’t what I’m used to.

I meet this one guy whom when I ask “Whats something cool or interesting about you that people wouldn’t expect?” mentions that he’s unemployed (in my admittedly judgemental opinion I don’t think thats a good answer to the question) and then the conversation becomes a laborious effort of me trying to extract info out of him. He says that his startup exited and he is wondering what to do next. I ask him if he has been doing anything fun and he says no he is thinking about what to do next. Then I ask what he wants to do next and he says he wants to build something. “Build what?” I ask. He says he doesn’t know and wants to build something. Then he walks away.

I also meet a guy who says he used to be a finance bro. I ask him what his recs are for the best places in SF are to get some cocaine and when he says he never had any I knew he must be a fake finance bro. Ok this interaction was actually pretty good but I found the joke too funny not to mention here.

Most of the people there already know each other so its a bit hard for me to break in. I do have some actually normal conversations here but I had more conversations like the first than I would normally care to. Ie ones where people aren’t that interested in getting to know me and quickly walk away. All in all not my vibe.

I then meet up with “The Professor” and “The Pickleballer” and hang out with them for a bit and take a bit more stuff from “The Professor” as he moves out. I take an old calculator of his that he had since high school that he wants to throw away but always kept taking regardless. I take it specifically so I can throw it away as soon as I get home. I then go home and write the newsletter.
