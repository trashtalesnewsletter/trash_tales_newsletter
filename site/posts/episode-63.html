Tuesday

Just another day at work.

Wednesday

Today after my stint in the mines I went to Midnight Runners with “Top of the Rope”. Originally I planned to take MR a little bit chill. To give you an idea of how that went I first get this text from her

Now naturally of course I had an out here. I could have just said no and I could have had a nice chill time lollygagging around.

We did not in fact lollygag around. My competitive side came out and I have to admit I actually enjoyed pushing myself like that. I had to put in some work but it was satisfying. And I finally got below 7 minutes on a mile

Sadly I realize I also have more work to do on myself personally at MR. After the 4th mile I was admittedly pushing myself and this guy came up to me and said “You looked pretty out of breath during that last mile”. I was caught a bit off guard by that remark and tried to play it off with a “Oh I wasn’t that out of breath”. He then said “No you were pretty out of breath”. I was kinda stunned at this point and just looked him square in the eyes and said “I don’t really know what you want me to say here”. He then said “Just telling the truth” before awkwardly leaving. It’s been a while since someone could really annoy me like that. I was genuinely ticked in a way I hadn’t been before. So much so that my goal for the last mile was to sprint hard and beat his ass. Looking back I wish I hadn’t gotten annoyed though. One of my personal goals (outside of the whole dating feedback thing) is to try be annoyed as little as possible and this does give me an opportunity to try and improve that and get my personal annoyance at minor things closer to 0. It may never be exactly 0 but I can always try to get it a little bit closer.

Thursday

After work I went to bar night with “The Mosher” and “That’s nuts”. I learned that the usual bar tender here just got into twitch streaming which is super cool! I really want to check out one of his twitch streams sometime. I think it would be fun!

While there I learned more about beer and skiing. On the beer side I learned there is something called dark beer which is called so usually for its color but sometimes for its flavor note. It is malted in water and often pairs well with coffee and chocolate. I also learned that sours can change perspective on taste and if you are drinking multiple beers you may need a palate cleanser lest you want everything to taste like sours.

On the skiing side I learned that there is a certain curvature of skiing called bowl shaped skiing where you can ski generally without trees on a wide bowl shaped slope. My understanding here is that this kind of skiing is generally preferable because it's easier and more fun.

At the same time we were also watching the Attack on Titan finale (the actual one not finale part 1 part 3 or something). In a rare break from what usually plays on the TV at bar night I actually knew what was happening and served as the main catcher upper of folks for “That’s Nuts” who had seen a lot of Attack on Titan a while back but not what just came before and “The Mosher” who hadn’t seen Attack on Titan at all until now.

Friday

After work I have another 1:1 with “The Big Youtube short” and his girlfriend “function(get_rid_of_cancer)” at Noe Valley Indian Cusine. I’m not generally a big fan of Indian food but this place was really good! The eggplant pakoras were great as was the garlic and pesto naan.

But what was even better of course was the conversation. I really liked learning more about “The Big Youtube short’s” job and how passionate he is about it. He is really into the problem of recommender systems because right now Youtube shorts is not very good at recommending videos. I told him I don’t watch a lot of Youtube shorts because I’m skeptical it's not just a center for brainrot and he told me it's precisely because of the recommender problem. I’m not able to be recommended shorts that I may actually like and enjoy hence I just got the lowest common denominator slop. If the recommender system was better tuned I could get educational things that could broaden my horizon and let me explore more beyond the niches I typically explore. For the first time I had some genuine faith in youtube shorts. And beyond just that I love seeing people passionate about things whether it's work or hobbies.

And it was also great catching up with his girlfriend too. She works with patient data and joked that I have some of the best deidentifying of personal data she has seen. This is actually extra funny because at the health care startup I was at I worked on deidentifying patient data for a while which was certainly … interesting in retrospect. “The Pyramid Schemer”, if you are reading this part you should tell the office about this. Some of them might find that funny. I learned she also did bee keeping research for a while wherein she learned that some of the main causes of queen bees dying are pesticides and parasites. The two Ps of the aPocalypse if you will. She also wrote some code for a project to help someone become completely cancer free, hence her nickname I just came up with today for her “function(get_rid_of_cancer)”.

Saturday

Originally I wanted to go to Marina run club to put my new joggers to use helping me look better but my legs weren’t really feeling it so I skipped. One problem with Marina run club is its awkwardly right before I need to go to trash pickup meaning its hard to talk with people, and at the same time I’m not convinced the conversations go on long enough that its actually worth skipping trash pickup for. Maybe I could skip and just come for the post trash hang? Who knows.

Anyway, I went to trash pickup with “The Notetaker” and a bunch of others. It was a fun time as usual.

From there I go and get brunch with “The Kpop dancer,” “The Sparkle,” “The Pickleballer," and “The Taco” over at Peacock Pansy. A brunch place I took from looking at my Yelp Elite Mentor (aka “The Dentist’s” yelp reviews) and the place didn’t disappoint. I quite liked the ube french toast there and thought it was delightful. “The Kpop dancer” is getting married in May so it was fun hearing about her getting ready for that. I’m gonna be going to her wedding in L.A which I’m very excited for. Apparently there is a restaurant there called Great Outdoor Restaurant, I really want to go there and sit inside for the irony of it all. The food there is supposed to be good from what “The Pickleballer” told me but that’s not really what I’m here for.

It turns out “The Sparkle” started a new job at Zoox but hadn’t rode in one yet so I took her on her first Zoox ride so she can see the fruits of her labor in action.

Later in the day I met up with “The Fisherman” and “The Shadow Brigade” and we all went to Detour. We mainly just hung around and talked for a while with the occasional break to play some arcade games.

Sunday

Today was a trash pickup marked by unexpected faces. I saw “The Trash Queen” and “The Rugby Player” for the first time in a minute there. They came with “The Trash Queen’s” dad and one of her other volunteer friends. It was great to catch up with them. I learned that at “The Rugby Player’s” old job at an autonomous tractor company he went to blueberry and potato conferences to try and sell the tractor he and his company were developing. He said the job really exposed him to all sorts of different people which was cool.

I also met a friend of a friend who I last saw back in 2023 during the really old days of trash pickup. Longer term readers who read episode 36 may remember me mentioning that I got a text from someone I hadn’t seen in years who I met up with. This person is a friend of the aforementioned person I met up with.

After trash pickup I caught up with one of my longest friends who I’ve known since the 3rd grade. We hung out for a while and watched him get a burrito and chilled in Dolores park. It was cool catching each other up on our lives and seeing some of the different things we are up to. He’s a neuro science Ph.D student at USC who is going to be doing a post doc at some institute in L.A soon which is quite exciting. Even as a kid he was always more the academic type than me. Being a professor is something I can totally see for him. I remember way back in 2022 I suggested he use ChatGPT and now he is a Codex power user :D. Whenever we meet he always likes to ask me about my thoughts on AI which I am quite happy to give.

Later in the day I walk around Duboce park and I see some girls offering lemonade if I tell them an interesting story. I tell them about the time I surprised “The Professor” in Vienna which they were sufficiently impressed by to warrant a lemonade. I turn the question back on them and ask them interesting stories about themselves. One of the girls mentions a time she went mountain biking and went down hard on a slippery mountain and managed to come out of it with minimal injuries. The other girl can’t really come up with a story unfortunately. I also ask them about the most interesting story they heard that day and they mention someone wound up in airport prison after losing their passport which sounds incredibly scary.

Finally I have a 1:1 with “The Filmmaker”. He regales me with his trip to Mardi Gras. As a seasoned Mardi Gras attender he knows now which streets have all the action and which ones to go to for the off the beaten path jazz clubs. He even ran into someone he met last year there which sounds like a really fun time.
