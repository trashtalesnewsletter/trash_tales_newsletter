Announcement

This is the 1 year anniversary of trash tales! Hard to believe I’ve been writing this for 52 weeks now! Attached with this newsletter is my attempt at a one year anniversary special. Stay tuned after the regularly scheduled content

Monday

Today I had two interviews. One of which was with a company I was really interested in technically and thought could be a really strong fit. Sadly though I underperformed in this interview and mind kinda froze for a while which really sucked. Interestingly though after the interview I was able to solve the problem within like 5 minutes a fact of which I told them about in the slim hopes it would change things. Naturally it didn’t but you miss 100% of the shots you don’t take right?

Afterwards I had another interview with “The Raja of Rhythm’s” company. The interviewer was one of the nicest interviewers I ever had. He was super cool and really enthusiastic! I got through the question quite easily and we talked for a while about MUNI since he noticed my MUNI sweater :D. Small shoutout to David for being such an awesome interviewer.

After a bit more walking around I met up with “The Shadow Brigade”. We walked around some more and made another attempt to go to Haru by Sam’s only to learn its closed on Monday :(.

Tuesday

I was inspired by “La Tacqueria Royalty” on Strava and decided to do a run up to Tank Hill from the Humza cave.

I have a goal of being able to run up to Twin Peaks at about an 8 minute pace which sadly I am still far from but I guess being able to go up that kind of elevation at just over 9 isn’t objectively bad in and of itself.

Later in the day I got a formal offer from the company I did an onsite with a few weeks ago! To be honest the package looked quite compelling in and of itself but at the moment I told the co founder who gave me the offer I wanted to interview with other companies first and get back later which she was very understanding about!

From there I go to Fatboys run club over in GGP and run for a few miles where I learn a very crucial life lesson. Specifically, never eat 3 burgers an hour before running. During the last part of the run my stomach was really hurting so I had to stop early :(.

Afterwards I go to The Detour for the weekly game night with “The Writer”. We play a really interesting game called The White Castle which is basically a game about managing resources, moving Samurai and a few other things with a feudal Japanese aesthetic.

Wednesday

Today was all about foraging for urchins. “The Fisherman” took me and “The Shadow Brigade” up North to forage for urchins. Way back in episode 3 I went for the first time to this spot and tried foraging for all of 5 minutes or so before my feet got wet and I gave up. But not this time! This time I brought rain boots and I was ready to rumble! Well for all of like 20 minutes anyway.

On this trip I made a discovery about myself that I have a real fear of losing my balance, slipping, and falling on rocks. While the rain boots did help my feet to not get wet they did unfortunately make moving around harder and I didn’t have as much tactile sensation in my feet which made me uneasy. Unlike the other two who took to it like a fish (urchin?) to water, I took to it more like a software engineer to water, that is to say I short circuited.

Unlike last time I did actually do some foraging and grabbed around 20 urchins before heading back. Thankfully it was a nice day on the beach so I did some impromptu dance choreo and hill repeats. In order to get to the beach you have to go down a hill so I figured I’d run up and down the hill in order to level up my speed. Sadly the best pace I got was just around 8 min/mile when I was really hoping for something more like 7:30 min/mile. Like many things related to foraging for urchins here it did not go my way.

When they were coming back over the rocks with the buckets full of urchin “The Fisherman” asked me to take one of the buckets back to the beach and what followed was a good 2 minutes of me freaking out while slipping a bit with the bucket being really panicked about trying to make it back. On my way back I stubbed my foot against a rock and hurt my hand a bit. Not a lot but it didn’t exactly put me in the best headspace either. “The Fisherman” asked if she should take the bucket back but I figured I should do at least something on this trip and not be a totally limp noodle so I persisted and earned the rank of only a mostly limp noodle.

After collecting urchins we went back to “The Fisherman”’s place to cook and prepare the urchins for dinner with the help of “The Fisherman”’s mom. I guess this is where I shined (relatively speaking) because unlike “The Shadow Brigade” I had no qualms with killing urchins. While it wasn’t as much fun as the time I beat an eel with a rock there was still something satisfying about cutting them open and taking out the gonads that we ate :D.

Sadly the satisfaction did not extend to the taste. As someone who generally doesn’t like seafood it shouldn’t be too surprising that I didn’t really care for the urchins or the mussels. When “The Fisherman”’s mom asked me why I wasn’t eating more I tried to politely say it was because I was already full but somehow I don’t think she really bought it when I only ate about 3 bites total. Also to continue the streak of me really messing up today I fumbled with the chopsticks I was using to eat the uni pasta. “The Fisherman”’s mom asked if I needed help and while I said no the next minute of me fumbling caused her to override said answer and just get me a fork. I could feel her judgement on my soul which “The Fisherman” did later confirm. Although apparently she also found it funny so oh well I guess.

As it turns out I’ll be coming back here on Saturday because a Muslim girl “The Fisherman” knows who is interested in doing this so “The Fisherman” set up another trip so we could meet which I quite appreciate. This time I’m going to use “The Fisherman”s helmet to alleviate my fears of falling.

Thursday

The first thing I do today is change my mind about interviewing with other places and accept the offer I got! I realized I was already really into this place anyway and my Dad gave me some good advice that while keeping options is good it’s also important to trust my gut. So I did! Huge thanks to “The Raja of Rhythm” and everyone else who gave me referrals and provided help throughout the process whether it was the offer of a referral, taking me out to Kogi Gogi, or just being a good friend!

With this I’m now finally done with job interviewing and will be starting my new role at the beginning of next year! I’m a little bit nervous but quite excited too!

I go to Downtown First Thursday with “The Fisherman” and “The Shadow Brigade” where we check out a men’s choir that is almost certainly gay.

Afterwards me and “The Shadow Brigade” head over to Haru by Sam’s and I finally get to show her where I spend my Thursdays with the gaggle of Canadians. We hang out with “The Philosopher,” “The Bartender,” “That’s nuts” and “The Mosher”. A pretty solid time as per usual with a slightly amusing incident where “The Shadow Brigade” assumed that the person who runs Haru by Sam’s is “The Bartender” from the newsletter when they are in fact different people :P. Sadly “The Shadow Brigade” had to leave before she could meet “The Mosher” as I think they would have hit it off as they both do space related stuff for either a work or hobby.

Friday

Today I head over to Berkeley to go see two of my friends from college give their Ph.D dissertations. As it happened both of them were on the same day! One of these friends reached out and asked if I was attending either over Zoom or in person and since I’m still in the unemployed arc I said I would love to come in person. I reached out to the other who said she was finishing around now and lo and behold same day! What great timing!

Dissertation 1 (Packing up them genes)

The first dissertation I went to was from my old college floormate who is graduating with a Ph.D in microbiology. For her Ph.D she studied proteins that pack genes. One thing I really appreciated about this talk was that she started by giving a high level overview of the concept for people without a background in Biology. She likened packing genes to intelligently putting spools in a basket by first wrapping them up and then putting them all as opposed to just chucking all the spools in the basket. Personally, this talk took me back to my childhood as I imagined myself packing a suitcase by chucking clothes in and my mom telling me to neatly fold them so you can put more clothes in.

The talk itself ran for about 40 minutes with 20 minutes worth of questions. I kinda wanted to ask some questions too but considering the caliber of questions being asked I figured it was best to not ask my rudimentary level questions like “Can you explain those last 5 slides again?”.

After the talk we went to go get lunch at Mendocino farms and I caught up with her some more on her research and the different things she’s doing now.

Now I have a couple hours before the next dissertation so I walk around the campus for a while and chill in Memorial Glade for a while where I wind up overhearding a surprisingly morbid conversation about a girl whose mom died when she was 13 and her sister attracting bad men. Since they didn’t realize I was listening there's a part of me that feels like I should have found a different spot to sit but I’ll admit I couldn’t help myself here

Dissertation 2 (Models + Application layer)

After that I head to the next dissertation that a friend of mine from an ML club I was in in college did. Unlike the last dissertation I could actually understand this one pretty well. And good thing too because unlike the last one he didn’t really try to add intro level analogies for people who weren’t already familiar.

This dissertation largely centers around combining pretrained models with applications like Cursor, Lovable etc. for domain specific models for maximal performance.

Sadly unlike the last dissertation there were only 5 of us in person with most people (including this guy's Ph.D advisor) remote on a Zoom call. Granted this was the week of a major ML conference but still the contrast felt pretty stark.

This talk also lasted about 40 minutes but there weren’t really many questions afterwards. It seems in CS there isn’t really a culture of asking questions.

From there me, the guy giving the dissertation, and a couple of our other friends walked around Berkeley a bit more.

I went back to SF to meet up with “The Shadow Brigade” at a pasta restaurant which she was very interested in taking pictures and videos of for her Nudge which if you aren’t familiar with is an app where people put up videos of fun activities to do and she is something of an influencer on. I get some olives at the restaurant and they wind up being worse than I thought but that's fine because the vibes were good.

Originally our plan afterwards was to go to a comedy show but it turns out the venue accidentally double booked so the show got cancelled.

“The Shadow Brigade” decided to slink back into the shadows and I met up with “The Fisherman,” “The Trash Queen,” and “The Rugby Player” at “The Trash Queen’s” apartment and we hung out and talked for a while which was great.

I learned that the Muzzie girl may not be able to come on the foraging trip which I was both disappointed and relieved by. The relief came from the fact that to be honest I’m not really that keen on going foraging again since as I mentioned I didn’t really enjoy the experience and it is a 2+ hour drive each way so it’s not really my thing.

Saturday

As I leave the Humza cave I learn that someone else cancelled on the fishing trip which really sucked. For a bit of context it is a 2+ hour drive each way and there are few enough people that one of the drivers (either “The Fisherman” or “The Quant”) would have to drive alone which is quite annoying. I figured since “The Fisherman” did initially set this up in part to help me meet this Muzzie girl I should go anyway to boost the numbers so nobody has to drive alone.

To be clear I don’t dislike fishing in general or even the concept of urchin hunting. It's just that particular spot which combines a long drive with fear inducing rock climbing that leads me to classify it as a spot of horrors.

Before I get into what that entails today at trash pickup we wound up having the largest in Saturday trash pickup history because by chance both a local girl scout troop and a local Questbridge alumni network along with a bunch of regulars for a record breaking 77 people in attendance.

Afterwards me and “The Quant” go to Plow to meet up with “The Taco” and “The Kpop dancer” and get on a call with “The Professor” to catch up on how his life in Vienna is going. There are other cameos including “The Sparkle,” “Mirandom thoughts,” “The Mushroom hunter,” “The attorney,” and someone else I don’t know well enough to have a newsletter nickname for them. It was nice to catch up with a lot of the meetup gang and see what kinds of cool and interesting stuff they’re up to.

From there me and “The Quant” head off to urchin hunting land. While little miss muzzie wasn’t there it turns out that one of the people that “The Fisherman” met off of tiktok is actually a coworker of “The Raja of Rhythm” (it was Justin btw). Unlike last time I made no attempt to go out on the rocks since I don’t really have intrinsic interest, it's darker which adds a whole new complexity, and most importantly there are no girls I want to impress. So instead I decide to sit on the beach and see how long I can last without any external stimulation like music or phone which winds up being an hour and a half which I guess is something in its own right. I was also supposed to guard “The Quant”s In-N-Out with my life but due to the bag getting wet the burger fell in the sand :(.

Once the urchin gang returns we help a bit with cleaning urchins and dumping seawater on them. While doing this someone using one of the spigots at the place sprays me a bit with water and I make a growling sound.

It wasn’t a lot of water but it gave off vibes that this place really doesn’t like me which is why I growled in a bit of resentment. Sigh. I don’t have much luck at this place. It’s going on the list for things that annoy me which are as follows

Settlers of Catan

Hadestown the musical

This particular foraging ground for urchins

Originally as this was happening I was looking forward to going on a long hyperbolic tirade about this place for the comedic value but now I’m just like well hopefully I never have to come back here. I’m definitely down for a wide variety of other fishing things that are both closer and less rocky but I really don’t want to come back here :( and it seems this place doesn’t want me back either. I’m down to be mutual enemies and hate this place if that's what it wants.

Also I know “The Quant” really wanted to go on more fishing excursions so I am glad he came here and liked it at least.

“The Fisherman” and a bunch of other folks stay to camp overnight while me, “The Quant” and someone reading this who I really should have a nickname for but don’t at the moment drove back. On the way “The Quant” doubted my left handedness which wasn’t helped by the fact that I can only snap with my right hand despite me writing with my right.

Sunday

Nothing like a good ol Sunday morning trash pickup to soothe the soul after going to that urchin spot. I pick up trash with “The Raja of Rhythm” and a bunch of other people for a while which was exactly what the doctor ordered.

Afterwards I get brunch with “The Enabler,” “Tight Knit,” and “La Tacqueria Royalty” over at Kitchen Story where surprisingly enough I actually ate. It was great catching up with them and I tried an interesting combination of grapefruit, ketchup, and tabasco which I highly recommend you all try.

From there I head home and write up the newsletter.

Anniversary special

Normally the above would be all there is to it and I’d send it out. But we’re doing things a little differently today. I knew I wanted to do something to commemorate 1 year of me doing this. For a while I was thinking about what it would be and I decided on a two parter.

Part 1

I figured I’d start by answering a question nobody really asked but feels spiritually fitting which is “What’s been the most unexpected thing about writing a weekly newsletter?” Good question, thanks for asking! I think the answer would have to be how much I think about the newsletter throughout the week when I think about events as they are happening. I’ll have thoughts like “Oh the readers will like this!” Or “Man this is gonna suck to write about.” I’ll also go through drafts in my head as something is happening in real time. I definitely wouldn’t have expected that at the start but now that the newsletter has become a weekly staple I now find it all but inevitable.
