Tuesday-Wednesday

Today I boarded a flight to Prague. Since I got laid off and had some extra time I thought it might be fun to go back to Europe. Part of this is also because my family happens to be in Europe for a vacation I originally turned down. On top of this “The Professor” moved to Vienna so why not pay him a surprise visit?

There are no direct flights to Prague so I wind up going SF -> Frankfurt -> Prague. The flights themselves were long but relatively uneventful. When I was transferring in Frankfurt I had to go through multiple buses to get to where I needed to go which makes the shuttle system in SF and many other airports look like magic in comparison.

While on the flight I finally decided to nominate myself for Yelp Elite. For those unaware I got inspired by “The Dentist” and have wanted to become Yelp Elite for a while. My reasoning being that there are Yelp Elite events you can go to and I’m trying to make more friends so I figured this could be a good way to do that. One thing though is I hate food reviews as a concept so to make it more palatable to me I only leave food reviews at places I don’t eat at. In other words I go with friends, take pictures of their food, and go off of vibes to write a review. Now that I have 20+ such reviews under my belt I figured I’d nominate myself. Let's see how this goes!

After a short flight I land in Prague and start to check it out. I quickly go over to my hostel and check in and start going out for drinks with some other travellers. Far and away I’m travelling for the shortest time at only 2 weeks. Everyone else seems to be traveling for a month plus with many people going for multiple months. To be honest I don’t know how people travel for so long without getting sick of it. If I was away from home for that long I’d be pretty sad. While this trip has been fun I have missed out on many things including “The Hombre’s” going away party, “Granola Girlie” eating 6 cans of beans and many more. If I extended this trip too long, who knows what else.

I met an interesting cast of characters including one guy who had “every job you can imagine” including building construction, bar tending, landscaping and more. He’s one of those who is travelling until a fixed budget has ran out.

I met someone else who was very coyish about saying where she's from. Generally at hostels the standard ice breaker is to say where you are from but she refused instead asking us to guess. We all put our racial profiling hats on and guessed everything from Vietnamese, Canadian, Netherlands and more. Apparently the answer was Indonesian which was a hard fought struggle.

There were some other folks too but I can’t remember anything notable about them at the moment.

After a while we all go out and walk around the city. We wind up going to St. Charles’s bridge which is a famous bridge in the city with a whole bunch of statues on it

Thursday

Sadly today begins at a temperature of 40 degrees and raining. Its dreadful weather yet I can’t waste a good Prague so venture out I must. Naturally I didn’t bring an umbrella with me but thankfully the hostel I’m at has one I can borrow which winds up being a life saver.

I go to a breakfast place which has a small serving of pancakes that winds up being perfectly ok. It didn't spark joy but it wasn’t terrible either. After walking around for another 10 minutes the rain picks up and I go inside to another place in part just to escape the weather. The pancakes here are absolutely terrible and I get through about one bite before deciding to leave. Thankfully though the waitress doesn’t make me pay for them on account of only eating a single bite.

I go back to the hostel and meet up with some of the gang and we go to a 3rd place where I get a beef goulash that is actually pretty good. They thought I was joking when I said it was the best meal I had today though. Apparently the cafe I went to was one that both Einstein and Kafka regularly went to which they were not shy about advertising. Sadly I think the average IQ of the cafe just went down after I went though.

Chameleon the cafe had. Looked so serious I thought

It was a German chameleon

After breakfast I split off and went to Prague Castle which actually looked really cool.

I’m a sucker for very elaborate architecture

I walk around a bit more and get to a good vantage point and get some views of the city.

The views are nice but the weather certainly dampens them :(. After a bit more walking the cold and jet lag demand I take a nap so I make a strategic pivot to conserve energy.

A few hours later I do a bit more walking, get some food, and then I go out to another bar with some of the folks from the hostel. Sadly this bar lets people smoke indoors which is terrible for me. I lasted all of 2 minutes before promptly doing an American exit.

When I get back I chat with some new folks who moved into my room. Among them I meet a pair of 18 year old British boys who have been traveling on their own for around a month now. I’m intrigued as its rare in the U.S to find 18 year olds who are already traveling by themselves and are well versed in the “adult arts” (smoking, drinking, gambling etc.). I think this is because the drinking age in Europe is lower than the States (16/18 vs 21, and underage drinking is less of a big deal). Come to think of it, I wasn't carded once when I went to bars in Europe which was interesting in and of itself.

I also met another pair of female British tweens who wanted my opinion on whether what they were planning to wear for their night out was too slutty. Considering that it was 40 degrees out and just rained earlier I was impressed they could wear short shorts and a tank top out. Personally I thought they were built different.

From there I head off to the train station to go to Vienna. Interestingly this train station had a book store with its very own Manga section which I wasn’t expecting.

After a 4 hour train ride I find myself in Vienna. I get quite excited here as my plan to surprise “The Professor” is finally underway. For those unaware, “The Professor” recently moved to Vienna for a job and part of my motivation for this Europe trip was to come and visit him. He isn’t aware of this at the moment which makes it all the more fun. I got his apartment address from his younger sister and plan to show up there when we have a group call scheduled to make sure he is home at the time. As I make my way through Vienna I have to be extra cautious to ensure I don’t run into him before I’m ready.

I camped out at a Five Guys for a bit since a pescatarian like him would never show up there. Eventually I make my way to his apartment and with about a half hour before the call I park myself right outside the door. The anticipation grows ever more. I’m really excited to see the look on his face and waiting the last half hour is torture. I almost break early but thankfully “The Quant” keeps me on track

Eventually the call starts and I’m finally ready to make my move. To make the bit even better I specifically asked “The Quant” to start the call by asking “The Professor” how he has been liking the city and if he has gone to any meetups. This would prompt “The Professor” to say we should all come visit him and then right on cue I would ring his doorbell. This works exactly as I imagined it would and when he opens the door his exact words were “Humza … what the fuck?! … Well come in I guess.” Not gonna lie this might be one of the best bits I have ever done.

To help lay the groundwork for this me and “The Quant” made it look like I was still in SF when I was in Europe. For example we posted some photos in the group chat that were taken last week but posted them on Wednesday

This was actually taken at Midnight Runners last week not this week

I also had to be sure not to look at any messages outside of normal PST hours to avoid suspicion here.

Once I filled “The Professor,” “The Taco,” and “The Plower” in on the joke, we played a game of online Dune which I lost but I won the war here.

Sunday

Today I largely hang out with “The Professor” as he shows me around. We get food and then go to a gaming history museum.

This museum has games from all different types of consoles such as the old sega genesis, arcade games like Streetfighter, all the way to the modern switch. It was pretty interesting playing the OG version of Mariokart and seeing just how far we’ve come.

We vibe around some more before going to a carnival in the evening. Vienna has a standing fair over in a park which is certainly … something.

I wind up going gokarting in which I have exactly as much fun as I look like I’m having.

Can you feel the joy?

Monday

Today while “The Professor” was at work I had a relatively chill day wandering around the city and taking a nap in the park. I saw some more of Vienna’s architecture which actually looks pretty sick.

After that I decided to go to a run club. Yesterday I actually saw a run club running around and wanted to find one to do.

The one that I found tended to attract a lot of locals which was kinda cool except for the fact that by default everyone was speaking in German and I had to really fish to find people speaking in English. Even I couldn’t imagine myself being like “Yo mind if I disrupt this whole conversation and y’all speak in an entirely different language here”.

The run itself was pretty good. We did a 6K run through the perimeter of Vienna’s city centre that hit up a bunch of fancy buildings. One thing I noticed about this run is that people were running at their own pace, meaning if you didn’t know where you were going it might be a bit difficult. Thankfully I was keeping up with someone who did. This made me appreciate how good a job Midnight Runners does of having captains throughout the run guiding people.

After the run I hit up the bar and tried talking with people. As mentioned a lot of conversations defaulted to German which made them impenetrable to me. I did find some English speaking folks and chatted with them for a while. A couple people doubted I did the run since I was wearing jeans and a jacket which forced me to whip out the Strava shown above (for what its worth out of ~40 people I was the 4th person to finish which meant I definitely finished before the doubters 😤)

When I’m mingling with some of the runners they ask me if I run before and I reply with one of my usual retorts (Humza joke #5 as “The Philosopher” might put it), “only from my problems” and I get stone silence followed by a … “thats great”. Clearly that joke didn’t land. The Austrian mind can’t comprehend.

I catch back up with “The Professor” and we get dinner and try unsuccessfully to find a good ice cream place. Interestingly a lot of the places here don’t like it when you sample. At one place he asked the lady working there glared at us and asked if we were actually gonna buy something. I was almost tempted to respond to such pettiness with pettiness of my own by making sure to sample and *not* buy something but thankfully cooler heads prevailed.
