Monday

Today was a pretty even split between interviews and walking. The interviews themselves were fine with nothing too much of note.

In the evening I went to Mission Run Club with “The Raja of Rhythm,” “The Filmmaker,” and “John Wick”. Since I twisted my foot the last time I came I decided to take it chill and go at a nice casual pace so as to not wreck my foot on the unpaved streets which thankfully worked out.

It turns out that one of the organizers was moving out of SF after this run so this doubled as a goodbye run for him. In his honor we did an especially hilly run up to Bernal Heights which I thought was thoughtful since now I certainly missed him less as I was going up that big hill.

Tuesday

Today I had an all day onsite with a startup I’m interviewing at. Unlike last week’s onsite this one I had to work on a single project for about 8 hours or so and then present it. The problem was actually a pretty interesting Computer Vision task with some messy data that definitely reflects the kinds of challenges you see in the wild.

One thing I did note as I was at this company was that the vibes just seemed … off. Its hard to say exactly what it was but I noticed that I didn’t really click with the people as well as I normally do at these things. As the day went on I was more interested in solving this problem to see if I could rather than because I was excited about working here.

In the middle of the day I had an “informal” chat with one of the ML engineers there who asked me what I was looking for in a job. Usually I might think to tailor my answers to whatever company I’m talking to but here I basically just said that I want to work for a company I think will actually be a net positive for society and can do something to help everyday people. I’m not honestly sure this company meets those conditions but I didn’t care enough to really think about it here.

Around the end of the day I went to present my solution and explain it to a few different people. From my perspective the presentation seemed to go well. I walked through what I did and there weren’t as many questions as I was expecting. A couple sure, but not really a lot.

I then talked with the CEO who basically asked what it would take for me to say “Yes” and I mentioned standard stuff like comp aligning and then I basically gave the same answer I did above.

From there I walked out and stopped by the bar I usually go to with the Canadians every Thursday and caroused with some of the locals.

Afterwards I went to The Detour for a game night that happens every Tuesday. I haven’t gone to one of these in a hot minute but it was a good time even in spite of the fact that we started with Codenames. I got to find this cool new cooperative game where we all have to work together to roll different kinds of multicolored dice to meet certain conditions (like all 3s, all different colors) within 10 minutes which kept us on the edge of our seats.

When I got back home I learned I was rejected from the company I did my onsite with today. Unusually the CEO said he was happy to give feedback if I was interested. Naturally I said yes and I learned that it came down to my communication. Apparently there were times where my reasoning and ideas were strong but didn’t come through the clearest. If that's the case I’m surprised they didn’t ask more questions during the presentation. Ah well, like I said I’m not that interested in this company and the fact that the CEO told me working 12+ hour days was normal certainly didn’t make me feel any worse about not getting it.

Wednesday

Yet more interviews abound today including one that I messed up due to some really stupid bug which kinda sucked.

After the interviews I went to Midnight Runners today with Franklin the air purifier. It was my first time with him since I twisted my foot so I was happy to see that I could not only hold my own but I finally got a sub 9 mile while carrying him :D.

Look at me go! And with a not terrible smile too :D

I will admit that as the run went on my increased pace did start to weigh on me a bit :(. By the last mile I had to drop down to like a 10 minute pace which was a bit less than great. There was also a bit of irony at play here because normally I love when people ask why I’m carrying Franklin but today people were doing it when I was running my last mile out of breath and in my head I’m like “Please just wait til I’m done. I’m dying here 🫠”.

Ideally I would have stuck around at Underdogs for the post run hang but due to most of it being booked for a private event what little was left proved to be too crowded so I went home.

When I got home I learned that my air purifier run got more traction than normal this time :D.

Thursday

In addition to my normal interviews today I finally decided to start ground flight school. A few episodes ago I decided to try flying a plane and resolved I’d start taking more lessons and today I finally did that by starting ground school.

In order to get certified you have to do both flight training and do an online course. I’m starting with the online course right now and working my way through the lectures. Currently I’m learning the basics of aerodynamics and some more of the physics behind flight.

In the evening I went to a pitch my friend dating event with “The Quant” and “The Philosopher”. Unlike last time I made sure to sign up for a slot ahead of time and “The Quant” prepped a presentation!

Huge shoutout to both “The Quant” and “The Philosopher” for getting up on stage and pitching me. I could definitely feel the love and in the end isn’t that the most important thing? Regardless of how the actual results of the event the love was there!

Sadly the actual results were … not the greatest. I’ll admit that maybe there were a couple typos in the presentation

In all fairness 90% of my name is spelled correctly here

It didn’t help that I also wasn’t interested in most of the other presentations. Amusingly some of them mentioned the ARRs of the startups they founded which was truly an “Only in SF” moment.

The way the event was structured was there was a set of presentations, a 15 minute break, and then another set of presentations. Mine was one of the last ones in the first block so my plan was to stick around until after the break and then bounce. I figured that as more presentations happened the mind share for me in particular would go down and whatever interest I got in the first half would be it.

There was one girl I was interested in but sadly I didn’t get much time to talk with her during the intermission so I figured I would send her an insta message instead. During the presentation she mentioned the fact that she was related to Alfred Nobel so I thought I would send her this

In case you didn’t know Alfred Nobel invented dynamite

Everyone told me this would be a bad idea though so I opted not to. It was one of the few times I ever heard “The Quant” give such an emphatic “NO!”

So instead I decided to send her something more generic

After the presentation we all headed over to bar night to meet up with “The Mosher,” “That’s nuts” and a couple of the other Canadians. It was a good time but in the back of my mind I did feel a bit bummed I wasn’t really able to talk with as many people as I wanted.

Friday

I started off by doing a special Mission Run Club pastry run that happens one Friday morning every month. For this one we went up to Tank Hill and got some great views

Later in the day me, “The Quant”, and “The Plower” went to an event called “Fall of Freedom” over at Green Apple books in inner sunset. This was basically an event raising money for Palestinian folks via art, tarot card readings, and soap.

I met a guy there who got really into soaps and now imports olive oil soap from Palestine. I never knew someone could be so passionate about soap and here he was telling me how this olive oil soap thats been made for one thousand plus years is some of the best hes ever had whether its for skin or hair. And in all fairness his hair is quite lush so perhaps there’s some truth to that.

I wound up buying Donkey Milk soap mainly because the name intrigued me and I figured the novelty was worth the $5.

After hanging around there and making my usual bomb jokes (or Humza joke #1 as “The Philosopher” would put it), I went to a Friendsgiving hosted by “The Enabler” and “Tightly Knit” which included some other Google folks like “La Taqueria Royalty” and “The Big YT short”. Those two did an amazing job cooking! The turkey thighs were quite good and the carmelized pear with pecans slapped. It did give me a slight sense of foreboding as I thought about the Friendsgiving I would be hosting tomorrow and realized there was definitely gonna be a big gap in food quality here.

It was a really good time, we all hung around and talked for many hours before playing a team based board game where we all had to keep a bunch of multi colored timers from running out. This proved to be a pretty intense game as we were all frantically scrambling to turn over hourglasses. I think we won one of the ten times we tried and even then that win may have been a bit disputed.

Saturday

Another Saturday morning another Sisters trash pickup. Nothing out of the ordinary here really.

Afterwards I tried and failed to run up Twin Peaks. Granted I was doing it with jeans and a jacket but I still feel like I could have pushed myself harder. I ran for a few minutes and a couple hundred feet of elevation before I felt like collapsing. This sadly means I’m not as cool as “Top of the rope” who did that run earlier today, but I suppose thats to be expected :D.

Once my heart recovered I prepared for the Friendsgiving that I would be hosting. This wound up being quite fun as I had a large group of people come including “The Quant,” “The Taco,” “The Pickeballer,” “The Mosher,” “The Philosopher,” “The Enabler,” “Tighlty Knit,” “Mirandom thoughts”, “The Boxer” (and his girlfriend), “Top of the rope”, and “The Fisherman”.

I made my usual lamb pasta, “The Mosher” brought mashed potatoes and a french dish and “The Quant” brought some Korean Fried chicken from Aria. And “Top of the rope” and “The Fisherman” brought some sparkling water and wine. I’ll admit without y’all we would have had to subsist on lamb pasta, water, and pies so y’all definitely were the MVPs of this whole event.

This event broke the record for most people I had in my apartment at once. We had to use all the chairs, Franklin, my bed, and I had to take a cushion from the papasan chair and put it on the floor. I’m quite glad we had such a good turnout, it may just be one of my favorite events that I hosted yet.

For dessert I made a deal with “The Quant” that he got to choose the pie I brought with the condition that he has to eat it with pepper which he did! He says it tasted fine which I certainly found to be quite informative :D.

Sunday

On my way to trash pickup today I ran into a homeless guy who kept gesturing at me with the come here motion

I wasn’t quite sure what to do in this situation so I kept walking forward until I passed him at which point he proceeded to follow me for half a block. At this point I planned for the worst case scenario that he was after me and sprinted for a block to make sure I lost him.

Trash pickup proceeded as normal which was good. From there me and “The Raja of Rhythm” checked out a cookie picnic event in Dolores Park. I found this event by looking at Partiful and seeing what events my mutuals were going to and lo and behold this popped up. The lesson here is that Partiful can be an interesting source for events you didn’t know were happening and now I’m going to check it periodically to broaden my horizons.

The picnic itself was pretty good! A bunch of people brought cookies and we all just stood around and talked. Since we heard about this at the last minute we didn’t bring any cookies but there were more cookies than people by the end so the host was practically begging us to take some regardless.

Amusingly I ran into a bunch of people I hadn’t seen in a while like “The Writer”’s roommate, a friend of “The fisherman”, some people I played Spikeball with, and someone I met at the meetup where I met “The Quant”, “The Professor” and that group of people. Truly a small world.

I also met one guy who I noticed as being distinctly awkward. He was quite keen on connecting with me even though we only talked for a couple minutes and most of that time I don’t think he was really paying that much attention to what I was saying or really reacting to much. Its a bit hard to explain but I just got weird vibes from him. Nothing bad, just not really the vibes I wanna hang out with again. Don’t get me wrong though, the event was still great.

I went home and watched some more flight school lectures and then went to KBBQ with “The Italian Stallion,” “The Raja of Rhythm,” “The Quant,” “La Tacqueria Royalty,” “The Boxer,” and “The Mosher”. It was my first time bringing “The Italian Stallion” and “La Tacqueria Royalty” so I’m glad I got to introduce them to the good stuff :D.

From there I went home. I should have worked on the newsletter but I just chilled and went to bed so I’ll have to pick up the slack tomorrow.
